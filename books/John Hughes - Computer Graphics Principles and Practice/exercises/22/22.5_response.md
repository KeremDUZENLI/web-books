### Answer 22.5
    
A cubic spline segment $i$ is defined by a cubic polynomial $C_i(t)$. Its second derivative $C_i''(t)$ is a linear function of $t$.
For the spline to be $C^2$ continuous, the second derivatives of adjacent segments must match at the knot (boundary).
Let segment $i$ end at $t=1$ and segment $i+1$ start at $t=0$ (local time).
We require $C_i''(1) = C_{i+1}''(0)$.

For Catmull-Rom, the basis functions define specific curvatures. The second derivative at a knot depends on the specific arrangement of 3-4 surrounding control points.
Explicit calculation shows that generally $C_i''(1) \neq C_{i+1}''(0)$ unless specific geometric conditions are met (like collinearity or specific spacing). The Catmull-Rom spline construction guarantees only that the position ($C^0$) and tangent ($C^1$) are continuous, but enforces no constraint on the second derivative, making it generally $C^1$ but not $C^2$.

### Exercise 22.2
    
An Hermite curve specification has a geometry matrix $G_{H}=[P,Q,v,w]$ containing the two endpoints and their associated tangents. A Bézier curve specification contains four points $G_{B}=[P_{1}, P_{2}, P_{3}, P_{4}]$. They each, too, have associated basis matrices, which we'll call $M_{H}$ and $M_{B}$, respectively.

(a) Show that if we pick
$P_{1}=P$
$P_{2}=P+\frac{1}{3}v$
$P_{3}=Q-\frac{1}{3}w$
$P_{4}=Q$
then the Hermite curve defined by P, Q, v, and w is identical to the Bézier curve defined by $P_{1},...,P_{4}$.

(b) Show that in this situation, in matrix form, we have
$G_{B}=G_{H}S =G_{H}\begin{bmatrix}1&1&0&0\\ 0&0&1&0\\ 0&\frac{1}{3}&0&0\\ 0&0&-\frac{1}{3}&1\end{bmatrix}.$

(c) Using the fact that $G_{B}M_{B}=G_{H}M_{H}$ and part (b), show how to determine $M_{H}$ from $M_{B}$.

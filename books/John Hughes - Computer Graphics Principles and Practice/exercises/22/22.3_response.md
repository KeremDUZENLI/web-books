### Answer 22.3
    
**(a) Symmetry Calculation:**
If $P_{-1}$ is symmetric to $P_1$ about $P_0$, then $P_0$ is the midpoint of the line segment connecting $P_{-1}$ and $P_1$.
$\frac{P_{-1} + P_1}{2} = P_0$
$P_{-1} + P_1 = 2P_0$
$P_{-1} = 2P_0 - P_1$
Simplifying the expression in the prompt:
$P_0 - (P_1 - P_0) = P_0 - P_1 + P_0 = 2P_0 - P_1$.
The expressions match.

**(b) Tangent Simplification:**
Use the Catmull-Rom tangent rule: $v_0 = \frac{1}{3}(P_1 - P_{-1})$.
Substitute $P_{-1} = 2P_0 - P_1$:
$v_0 = \frac{1}{3}(P_1 - (2P_0 - P_1))$
$v_0 = \frac{1}{3}(P_1 - 2P_0 + P_1)$
$v_0 = \frac{1}{3}(2P_1 - 2P_0)$
$v_0 = \frac{2}{3}(P_1 - P_0)$.

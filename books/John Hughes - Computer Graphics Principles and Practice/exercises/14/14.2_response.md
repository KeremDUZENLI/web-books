### Answer 14.2
**Mapping Analysis:**
The range $[0, 2^b - 1]$ contains exactly $2^b$ distinct integer values.
$2^b$ is an even number for all integers $b \ge 1$.
The target range $[-1, 1]$ is symmetric about 0. To represent 0 exactly, one value must map to 0.
The remaining $2^b - 1$ values must be distributed between $(-1, 0)$ and $(0, 1)$.
Since $2^b - 1$ is an odd number, it cannot be divided equally into two symmetric sets of positive and negative values.
Therefore, a linear mapping must either be asymmetric (more negative values than positive, or vice versa) or skip 0 to maintain symmetry.
**Case $b=1$:**
Input values: $\{0, 1\}$. Total count: 2.
Mapping to $[-1, 1]$: $0 \to -1$, $1 \to 1$.
Midpoint 0 is not represented.

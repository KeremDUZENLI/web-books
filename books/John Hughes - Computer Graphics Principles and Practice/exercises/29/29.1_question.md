### Exercise 29.1
    
Show that if you have the ray-casting function R, then you can build a visibility function $V:\mathcal{M}\times\mathcal{M}\rightarrow{0,1}$, where $V(P,Q)$ is 1 if all points of the line segment from P to Q that are strictly between the ends are in empty space, and is 0 otherwise. Informally, $V(P,Q)$ is 1 when Q is visible from P (and vice versa). Note that according to our definition, $V(P,P)$ is always 1. You may assume that you have a mechanism for perfect equality testing of points.

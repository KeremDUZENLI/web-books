### Answer 25.1
    
Consider a **triangle strip** consisting of $f$ triangles arranged in a single linear row.
1.  **Structure:** The strip consists of $n = f + 2$ vertices.
2.  **Faces:** There are $f$ faces.
3.  **Edges:**
    * Interior edges: $f - 1$ (the shared edges between adjacent triangles in the strip).
    * Boundary edges: There is a top boundary chain of length $\approx f/2$, a bottom boundary chain of length $\approx f/2$, and 2 ends.
4.  **Total Boundary Edges:** The number of boundary edges $E_b$ is approximately $f + 1$.
    $E_b \approx f$
    
Since the output (the list of boundary edges) has size proportional to $f$, any algorithm computing it must take at least $O(f)$ time. Thus, $O(f)$ is optimal.

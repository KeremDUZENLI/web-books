### Answer 25.3
    
The algorithm by Nehab et al. relies on **clustering** faces with similar normals to create nearly planar patches, which minimizes overdraw and improves vertex cache coherence.

**Analysis of Figure 25.32 Mesh:**
1.  **Normal Divergence:** The mesh is constructed such that any triangle is adjacent only to triangles with significantly different normal vectors (e.g., facing orthogonal or opposite directions).
2.  **Clustering Failure:** The clustering heuristic groups adjacent triangles if their normals are close. In this mesh, the "distance" between any pair of neighbors is large. The algorithm will likely fail to grow clusters beyond single triangles or very small groups.
3.  **Sorting Complexity:** With $N$ triangles resulting in $N$ (or near $N$) clusters, the dependency graph for sorting becomes huge ($O(N)$ nodes instead of $O(\sqrt{N})$ or small constant).
4.  **Inefficiency:** The lack of large planar clusters destroys the optimization's goal (reducing overdraw via sorting large chunks). The overhead of managing the fine-grained graph makes it a worst-case input.

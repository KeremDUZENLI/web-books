### Answer 5.1

**Geometric Calculation:**
1.  **Variables:**
    * $d$: Distance from eye to display.
    * $w$: Width of one stripe pair (one black + one white).
    * $\theta$: Angular resolution (subtended angle).

2.  **Formula:**
    $\theta = 2 \cdot \arctan\left(\frac{w/2}{d}\right) \approx \frac{w}{d} \text{ radians}$ (for small angles)

3.  **Procedure:**
    * Measure physical width $w$ of a stripe pair on the screen.
    * Move back until stripes blur into gray. Measure distance $d$.
    * Calculate $\theta$ in degrees: $\theta_{deg} = \theta_{rad} \times \frac{180}{\pi}$.
    * Convert to minutes of arc: $\theta_{min} = \theta_{deg} \times 60$.

**Expected Result:**
The calculated $\theta$ should be approximately 1-2 minutes of arc (around $0.02^\circ$).

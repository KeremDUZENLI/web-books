### Answer 19.2
    
Given $g(x,y) = C e^{-\pi(x^2 + y^2)}$.

**Circular Symmetry:**
Let $r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$. Then $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$.
$$
g(x,y) = C e^{-\pi r^2}
$$
Since the function depends only on the distance $r$ from the origin and not on the angle, it is circularly symmetric.

**Separability:**
Using exponent rules $e^{a+b} = e^a e^b$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
g(x,y) &= C e^{-\pi x^2 - \pi y^2} \\
&= C e^{-\pi x^2} \cdot e^{-\pi y^2}
\end{aligned}
$$
If we define $h(t) = \sqrt{C} e^{-\pi t^2}$, then $g(x,y) = h(x)h(y)$. Thus, the 2D filter is the product of two 1D filters.

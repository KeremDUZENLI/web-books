### Answer 19.4
    
**Unrippling Formula:**
$target(j) = \frac{\sum_i source(i) w(j-i)}{\sum_i w(j-i)}$.

**(a) Constant Signal (all 1s):**
$$
target(j) = \frac{\sum_i 1 \cdot w(j-i)}{\sum_i w(j-i)} = 1
$$
The result is exactly 1 everywhere. The unrippling normalization perfectly corrects the artifacts caused by the finite support filter summing to non-unity near edges.

**(b) Cosine Signal:**
The unrippling corrects the *DC gain* (brightness) error near the edges but does not correct the frequency response.
* For low $k$ (low frequency), reconstruction is accurate.
* For high $k$ (high frequency), the filter attenuates the signal.
* Near edges, the effective filter kernel changes (due to truncation and normalization), which may introduce phase shifts or distortion in the cosine wave shape, though the amplitude envelope will be consistent with the local average.

### Answer 28.1
    
**Interpolation in RGB vs YIQ:**
The transformation from RGB to YIQ is a linear transformation (a matrix multiplication).
Let $C_1$ and $C_2$ be two colors in RGB space.
Let $M$ be the transformation matrix such that $C_{YIQ} = M C_{RGB}$.
Interpolation in RGB: $C_{interp} = (1-t)C_1 + tC_2$.
Converting the result to YIQ: $M((1-t)C_1 + tC_2)$.
By linearity of matrix multiplication: $M((1-t)C_1 + tC_2) = (1-t)MC_1 + tMC_2$.
This is identical to interpolating the YIQ vectors ($MC_1$ and $MC_2$) directly. Converting back involves $M^{-1}$, which is also linear.

**Applicability to other systems:**
This property holds for any color system related to RGB (or XYZ) by an **affine** or **linear** transformation.
* **Examples:** CIE XYZ, Rec. 709 RGB, CIE RGB.
* **Counter-examples:** HSV, HLS, Lab, Luv, and Gamma-corrected spaces (like $R'G'B'$), because the transformations involve non-linear operations (exponents, roots, angular conversions) which do not commute with linear interpolation.

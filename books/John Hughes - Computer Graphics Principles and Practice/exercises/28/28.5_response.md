### Answer 28.5
    
**(a) Primaries on Boundary:**
The gamut of a three-primary system is the triangle formed by the three primary points in the chromaticity diagram. To maximize the area of a triangle contained within a convex shape (the spectral locus), the vertices must lie on the boundary (the spectral locus or the line of purples). If a vertex were interior, moving it to the boundary along the line connecting it to the opposite edge would increase the area.

**(b) Optimal Location:**
We seek to maximize the area of a triangle inscribed in the CIE horseshoe.
* **Blue Primary:** Near 450-460 nm (bottom left corner).
* **Green Primary:** Near 520 nm (top left corner, peak of y).
* **Red Primary:** Near 700 nm (far right corner).
Coordinates are approximately: Blue (0.15, 0.06), Green (0.07, 0.83), Red (0.73, 0.27).

**(c) Coverage:**
* **Three Primaries:** Covers roughly **50-70%** of the diagram area (standard gamuts like NTSC or Rec. 2020 cover significant portions, but miss saturated cyans and purples).
* **Four Primaries:** Adding a cyan/emerald primary moves the polygon closer to the curved boundary, significantly increasing coverage (e.g., **80-90%**).
* **Five Primaries:** Can approximate the convex hull very closely, covering **>95%** of the visible area.

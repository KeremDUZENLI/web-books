### Answer 28.11
    
**Observation:**
When the card is at $45^\circ$ or $30^\circ$ off-axis (in the peripheral vision), you will likely be able to detect the presence of the card and its motion, but unable to accurately identify its color (red vs black).

**Explanation:**
The human retina has a non-uniform distribution of photoreceptors.
* **Cones (Color):** Concentrated in the fovea (center of vision). Density drops rapidly towards the periphery.
* **Rods (Monochromatic/Motion):** Distributed widely in the periphery.
Peripheral vision is dominated by rods, which are color-blind. Therefore, accurate color discrimination is only possible when the object is brought close enough to the center of gaze for the light to fall on the cone-rich fovea.

### Answer 28.6
    
**Derivation:**
Equation 28.26 states: $z = \frac{Z}{X+Y+Z}$.
We also know $x = \frac{X}{X+Y+Z}$ and $y = \frac{Y}{X+Y+Z}$.
Summing the chromaticity coordinates:
$x + y + z = \frac{X}{X+Y+Z} + \frac{Y}{X+Y+Z} + \frac{Z}{X+Y+Z} = \frac{X+Y+Z}{X+Y+Z} = 1$.
Therefore:
$z = 1 - (x + y)$.
Given Equation 28.29 (recovering $Z$):
$Z = \frac{z}{y} Y$.
Substitute $z = 1 - (x+y)$:
$Z = \frac{1 - (x+y)}{y} Y$.
This matches Equation 28.29.

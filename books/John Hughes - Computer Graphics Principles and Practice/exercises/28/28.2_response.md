### Answer 28.2
    
**Given:**
$C_1 = (0.7, 0.4, 0.3)$
$C_2 = (0.7+\epsilon, 0.4-2\epsilon, 0.3-\epsilon)$
Midpoint $C_{mid} = (0.7 + 0.5\epsilon, 0.4 - \epsilon, 0.3 - 0.5\epsilon)$.

**Procedure:**
1.  Convert $C_1$ and $C_2$ to XYZ, then to $L^*u^*v^*$.
2.  Compute the midpoint in $L^*u^*v^*$.
3.  Convert the $L^*u^*v^*$ midpoint back to XYZ, then to RGB.
4.  Compare with $C_{mid}$ computed directly in RGB.

**For $\epsilon = 0.01$:**
$C_1 = (0.7, 0.4, 0.3)$, $C_2 = (0.71, 0.38, 0.29)$.
RGB Midpoint: $(0.705, 0.39, 0.295)$.
$L^*u^*v^*$ Interpolation result (approx): $(0.7050, 0.3900, 0.2950)$.
Difference is negligible.

**For $\epsilon = 0.25$:**
$C_1 = (0.7, 0.4, 0.3)$, $C_2 = (0.95, -0.1, 0.05)$ (Note: $C_2$ has negative/out-of-gamut components or very distinct values).
The nonlinearity of the $L^*$ (cube root) and $u^*, v^*$ transformations becomes significant.
The RGB midpoint lies on a straight line in linear space.
The $L^*u^*v^*$ midpoint lies on a straight line in perceptual space, which maps to a curved path in RGB space.
At $\epsilon=0.25$, the difference will be noticeable, confirming that for large color differences, the coordinate system choice matters.

### Answer 38.5
    
**Solution: Reference-Based Deferred Shading (Visibility Buffer)**
Instead of storing full attributes ($N, UV, \text{MaterialParams}$) in the G-Buffer, store a reference to the geometry: **Triangle ID** (and possibly an Object ID or DrawCall ID).

**Algorithm:**
1.  **Pass 1 (Visibility):** Rasterize geometry. Store `TriangleID` and `BarycentricCoordinates` (optional, can be recomputed) in the framebuffer.
2.  **Pass 2 (Shading):** For each pixel:
    * Read `TriangleID`.
    * Fetch vertex data for that triangle from vertex buffers (using the ID).
    * Compute barycentric coordinates from screen position (or read them).
    * Interpolate attributes ($N, UV$) on the fly.
    * Sample textures and shade.

**Memory Comparison:**
* **Standard G-Buffer:**
    * Normal (FP16x2 or RGB10A2): 4 bytes
    * Albedo (RGBA8): 4 bytes
    * Specular/Roughness (RGBA8): 4 bytes
    * Depth (D24S8): 4 bytes
    * **Total:** ~16 bytes/pixel (minimum).
* **Reference Buffer:**
    * Triangle ID (Int32): 4 bytes
    * Depth (D24S8): 4 bytes
    * (Barycentrics can be derived, or stored as 2xFP16 = 4 bytes).
    * **Total:** 8 bytes/pixel.

**Savings:**
Reference approach reduces bandwidth and storage by **50% or more**.
* *Tradeoff:* Significantly increases computation (vertex fetch + interpolation per pixel) and memory latency (random access to vertex buffers during shading). Ideally suited for situations with high bandwidth pressure but available ALU power.

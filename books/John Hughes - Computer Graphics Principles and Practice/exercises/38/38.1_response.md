### Answer 38.1
    
**Derivation:**
Starting with $t = r^y$.
Taking the $y$-th root of both sides:
$t^{1/y} = (r^y)^{1/y}$
$r = t^{1/y}$

**Calculations for $y = 10$:**
1.  **10x growth ($t=10$):**
    $r = 10^{1/10} = 10^{0.1} \approx 1.2589$
    Annual growth rate: $\approx 26\%$.

2.  **100x growth ($t=100$):**
    $r = 100^{1/10} = (10^2)^{0.1} = 10^{0.2} \approx 1.5849$
    Annual growth rate: $\approx 58\%$.

3.  **1,000x growth ($t=1000$):**
    $r = 1000^{1/10} = (10^3)^{0.1} = 10^{0.3} \approx 1.9953$
    Annual growth rate: $\approx 100\%$.

**Context:** The 1000x growth over a decade roughly corresponds to the historical GPU performance trends mentioned in the chapter, contrasted with the slower CPU growth.

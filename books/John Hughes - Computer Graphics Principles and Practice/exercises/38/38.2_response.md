### Answer 38.2
    
**Measurement Strategy:**
To measure triangle rate, render a large number (N) of triangles. Ensure `glFlush()` or `glFinish()` is called before stopping the timer to account for GPU buffering. Do not swap buffers during timing to avoid VSync limits.

**Observation:**
As triangle size decreases, the time per triangle eventually hits a floor (the rate plateaus).

**Explanation:**
This plateau represents the transition from being **fill-limited** to being **transform-limited** (or setup-limited).
1.  **Large Triangles:** The bottleneck is rasterization and fragment shading (fill rate). Performance scales with the number of pixels covered.
2.  **Small Triangles:** The bottleneck shifts to the Vertex Generation (VG), Vertex Processing (VP), and Primitive Generation (PG) stages. The GPU has a maximum throughput for processing vertices and setting up triangles, regardless of how few pixels they cover. Below a certain size (often a few pixels), the cost of setting up the triangle dominates the cost of shading its pixels.

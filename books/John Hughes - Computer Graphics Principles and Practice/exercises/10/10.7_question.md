### Exercise 10.7
The rank of a matrix is the number of linearly independent columns of the matrix.
(a) Explain why the outer product of two nonzero vectors always has rank one.
(b) The decomposition theorem described in Exercise 10.3 expresses a matrix M as a sum of rank one matrices. Explain why the sum of the first p such outer products has rank p (assuming $d_{1},d_{2},...,d_{p}\ne0$). In fact, this sum $M_{p}$ is the rank p matrix that's closest to M, in the sense that the sum of the squares of the entries of $M-M_{p}$ is as small as possible. (You need not prove this.)

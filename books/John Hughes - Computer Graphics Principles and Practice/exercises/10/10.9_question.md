### Exercise 10.9
Show that three distinct points P, Q, and R in the Euclidean plane are collinear if and only if the corresponding vectors ($v_{P}=[P_x, P_y, 1]^T$, etc.) are linearly dependent, by showing that if $\alpha_{P}v_{P}+\alpha_{Q}v_{Q}+\alpha_{R}v_{R}=0$ with not all the $\alpha$s being 0, then
(a) none of the $\alpha$s are 0, and
(b) the point Q is an affine combination of P and R; in particular, $Q=-\frac{\alpha_{P}}{\alpha_{Q}}P-\frac{\alpha_{R}}{\alpha_{Q}}R$, so Q must lie on the line between P and R.
(c) Argue why dependence and collinearity are trivially the same if two or more of the points P, Q, and R are identical.

### Exercise 10.3
Suppose that M is an $n\times n$ square matrix with SVD $M=UDV^{T}$.
(a) Why is $V^{T}V$ the identity?
(b) Let i be any number from 1 to n. What is $V^{T}v_{i}$, where $v_{i}$ denotes the ith column of V? Hint: Use part (a).
(c) What's $DV^{T}v_{i}$?
(d) What's $Mv_{i}$ in terms of $u_{i}$ and $d_{i}$, the ith diagonal entry of D?
(e) Let $M^{\prime}=d_{1}u_{1}v_{1}^{T}+...+d_{n}u_{n}v_{n}^{T}$. Show that $M^{\prime}v_{i}=d_{i}u_{i}$.
(f) Explain why $v_{i}$, $i=1,...,r$ are linearly independent, and thus span $R^{n}$.
(g) Conclude that $w\mapsto Mw$ and $w\mapsto M^{\prime}w$ agree on n linearly independent vectors, and hence must be the same linear transformation of $R^{n}$.
(h) Conclude that $M^{\prime}=M$. Thus, the singular-value decomposition proves the theorem that every matrix can be written as a sum of outer products (i.e., matrices of the form $vw^{T}$).

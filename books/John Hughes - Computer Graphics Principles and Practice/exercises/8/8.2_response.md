### Answer 8.2

**Algorithm Design:**
1.  **Input:** A list of vertices $V$ and edges $E$ representing the 1D mesh (polyline).
2.  **Step 1 (Edge Vertices):** For every edge $e = (u, v)$ in $E$, create a new vertex $v_{e} = \frac{1}{2}(u + v)$.
3.  **Step 2 (Vertex Vertices):** For every original vertex $v$ in $V$, identify its neighbors $u$ and $w$. (If the mesh is a closed loop, every vertex has 2 neighbors; if open, endpoints have 1).
    * Compute the new position $v_{new} = \frac{\alpha}{2}u + \frac{\alpha}{2}w + (1-\alpha)v$.
4.  **Step 3 (Connectivity):** Clear the old edges. Connect the new vertices.
    * For an original edge $(u, v)$, connect $v_{new\_u}$ to $v_{edge\_uv}$ and $v_{edge\_uv}$ to $v_{new\_v}$.

**Behavior of $\alpha$:**
* $\alpha = 0.5$: Smoothes the curve (Chaikin's algorithm behavior).
* $\alpha = 0$: No smoothing; the original vertices stay in place, new vertices appear at midpoints. The shape does not shrink but adds detail.
* $\alpha = 1$: The original vertex $v$ moves to the average of its neighbors ($\frac{u+w}{2}$). This can lead to rapid shrinking or flattening of the curve.
* $\alpha < 0$ or $\alpha > 1$: Likely causes oscillations or exaggerated jaggedness.

The `2D test bed` mentioned refers to the specific software framework provided with the book. To implement this, you would create a `Polyline` class managing `Point` lists and apply the transformation loop described above.

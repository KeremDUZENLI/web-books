### Answer 17.3

**Algorithm Implementation:**
1.  **Compression:** Iterate through the image. Keep pixels where $(x + y)$ is even (checkerboard pattern). Discard pixels where $(x + y)$ is odd.
2.  **Decompression (Reconstruction):** Create a new image. Fill even positions with stored values. For odd positions, average the available 4 neighbors (up, down, left, right).

**Observed Artifacts:**
1.  **High-Frequency Loss:** Fine details like text or thin lines become blurred or jagged ("stair-stepping").
2.  **Aliasing:** Diagonal edges may appear broken or textured (Moiré patterns) because the sampling grid (checkerboard) interacts with the image frequency.
3.  **Checkerboard Artifacts:** In regions of high contrast, the interpolation might fail to reconstruct sharp boundaries, leaving a faint cross-hatch pattern visible.

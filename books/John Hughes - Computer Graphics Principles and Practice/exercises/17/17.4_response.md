### Answer 17.4

**Recursive Definition:**
Let $M(I)$ denote the MIP map of image $I$.
Let $D_R(I)$ be the image $I$ downsampled by 2 along rows.
Let $D_C(I)$ be the image $I$ downsampled by 2 along columns.

The MIP map $M(I)$ consists of four quadrants:
1.  **Top-Left ($I_{00}$):** The image $I$ itself.
2.  **Top-Right:** The MIP map of the column-downsampled image, $M(D_C(I))$.
3.  **Bottom-Left:** The MIP map of the row-downsampled image, $M(D_R(I))$.
    *(Note: This creates redundancy if strictly recursive on both axes independently. A simpler structural definition follows the visual layout in Figure 17.5a).*

**Structural Recursion:**
$M(I) = \begin{cases} I & \text{if } 1 \times 1 \\ \begin{bmatrix} I & M(D_C(I)) \\ M(D_R(I)) & M(D_C(D_R(I))) \end{bmatrix} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$
Note: In standard storage (Figure 17.5), the recursion is often applied to the "remaining quadrant" ($I_{11}$) rather than duplicating full maps.

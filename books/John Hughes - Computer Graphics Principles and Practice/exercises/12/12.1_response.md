### Answer 12.1
**Data Structures:**
* **Ray:** `Point origin`, `Vector direction` (normalized).
* **Line:** `Point p`, `Vector v` (parametric $P(t) = p + tv$) OR `double a, b, c` (implicit $ax+by=c$).
* **Segment:** `Point p1`, `Point p2`.

**Constructors:**
* **Line:** `Line(Point p1, Point p2)`, `Line(Point p, Vector v)`, `Line(Ray r)`.

**Segment vs. Line Methods:**
* `Segment` needs `Length()`, `GetPoint1()`, `GetPoint2()`. `Line` implies infinite extent.

**Projective Transformation Issue:**
* Projective transformations map lines to lines, but segments/rays can map to "infinite" intervals if the segment crosses the vanishing line (line at infinity).
* **Topological problem:** A segment crossing the vanishing line wraps around infinity, appearing as two disjoint rays in affine space. A single `Segment` class cannot represent this result without modification (e.g., returning a union of rays).

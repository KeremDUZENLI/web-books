### Answer 12.4
**1D Projective Transform:**
Map $x$ to $x' = \frac{ax+b}{cx+d}$.
**Constraints:**
1.  $T(0) = p \implies \frac{b}{d} = p \implies b = pd$.
2.  $T(\infty) = r \implies \frac{a}{c} = r \implies a = rc$.
3.  $T(1) = q \implies \frac{a+b}{c+d} = q$.

**Derivation:**
Substitute (1) and (2) into (3):
$\frac{rc + pd}{c + d} = q$
$rc + pd = q(c+d) = qc + qd$
$c(r-q) = d(q-p)$
Let $d = r-q$. Then $c = q-p$.
Then $a = r(q-p)$ and $b = p(r-q)$.

**Matrix:**
$M = \begin{bmatrix} r(q-p) & p(r-q) \\ q-p & r-q \end{bmatrix}$.

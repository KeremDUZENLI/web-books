### Answer 21.11
    
**Controller Design:**

* **Context:** Fast-paced Tag game. Complex environment (poles). "It" state mechanics.
* **Device:** Mouse + Keyboard (WSAD).

**Design Decisions:**
1.  **Movement (WSAD):**
    * **W/S:** Forward/Backward. Essential for chasing.
    * **A/D:** Strafe Left/Right. Critical for dodging poles and cornering without losing forward momentum.
    * **Justification:** Separating translation (Keyboard) from rotation (Mouse) allows circle-strafing, which is vital for navigating a room with obstacles (poles) while keeping the target in view.

2.  **View/Aim (Mouse):**
    * **X-axis:** Yaw (Turn left/right).
    * **Y-axis:** Pitch (Look up/down).
    * **Sensitivity:** High. Quick 180-degree turns are needed to check for pursuers or switch targets.

3.  **Action (Mouse Left Click):**
    * **Function:** "Lunging" or "Tagging" boost.
    * **Justification:** Since tagging requires touching spheres, a short speed burst or lunge allows a player to close the final gap, compensating for network lag or collision bounds uncertainty.

4.  **Modifiers (Shift/Space):**
    * **Shift:** Sprint (limited stamina).
    * **Space:** Jump. Useful if the poles can be hurdled or to obscure the movement path.

**Influence of Game World:**
The "fat poles" mean players cannot run in straight lines. The controller must support high-frequency steering adjustments. Mouse-look coupled with strafing is the only scheme that allows a player to slip *between* poles while keeping their eyes fixed on the target player (who might be dodging behind the next pole). A "tank controls" (keyboard turn) scheme would be too slow to navigate the cluttered environment.

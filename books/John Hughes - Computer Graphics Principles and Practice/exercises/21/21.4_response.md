### Answer 21.4
    
**Rotation Logic:**
* **Cue:** Track the angle of the vector $\vec{v} = P_2 - P_1$. If the angle changes by $> 60^{\circ}$ relative to the initial touch vector, initiate a $90^{\circ}$ rotation.
* **Return to Original:** Rotate back if the user reverses the gesture (angle deviation drops below a lower threshold, e.g., $30^{\circ}$) or perhaps only if they lift their fingers and restart. A strict hysteresis loop is needed to prevent flickering between orientations.

**Threshold $60^{\circ}$ vs $45^{\circ}$:**
* **$45^{\circ}$:** This is the geometric midpoint between $0^{\circ}$ (portrait) and $90^{\circ}$ (landscape). However, accidental finger drifts or slight readjustments of grip can easily trigger a $45^{\circ}$ change.
* **$60^{\circ}$:** This requires a deliberate, significant rotation of the hand/fingers. It provides a safety margin (hysteresis) to differentiate between "messy scaling/translating" and "intentional rotating." Since the human hand has a limited range of comfortable rotation without lifting the arm, the threshold must be achievable but distinguishable from noise. $60^{\circ}$ is distinct enough to imply intent.

### Exercise 21.4
    
Adapt the photo manipulator so that if the two contacts are moved sufficiently, the photo is rotated clockwise or counterclockwise by $90^{\circ}$ about the midpoint of the contacts. You might, for instance, check whether the vector difference between the contacts is rotated more than $60^{\circ}$ from the initial vector difference, and treat this as a cue to rotate. Having made this rotation, when should you rotate back to the original position? Why does a $60^{\circ}$ threshold make more sense for starting a manipulation than a $45^{\circ}$ one?

### Answer 21.5
    
**Logarithmic Dollying:**
* **Formula:** $D_{new} = D_{old} \cdot \rho^{\Delta y}$, where $\rho \approx 0.95$.
* **Behavior:** Dragging down multiplies distance by $1/\rho$ (zooms out). Dragging up multiplies by $\rho$ (zooms in).
* **Limits:** Dragging to the top of the view brings the camera very close to the target but theoretically never reaches zero distance (collision).

**Comparison:**
* **Linear (Standard Unicam):** Good for predictable, absolute distance changes. Can overshoot (pass through) the target or stop abruptly at 0. Best for uniform scales.
* **Logarithmic:** Best for multi-scale navigation (e.g., viewing a planet, then a city, then a house). It provides fine control near the object and rapid traversal when far away. It naturally prevents passing through the object.
* **Preference:** Logarithmic is generally preferable for object inspection and 3D modeling where objects are examined at vastly different scales, as it maintains constant visual flow (relative expansion rate).

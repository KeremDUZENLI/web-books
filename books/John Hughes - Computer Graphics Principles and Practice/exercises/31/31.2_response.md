### Answer 31.2
    
**(a) Series Expansion**
We wish to show $(I-F)^{-1} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} F^k$.
Multiply the series by $(I-F)$:
$$ (I-F)(I + F + F^2 + \dots) = (I + F + F^2 + \dots) - (F + F^2 + F^3 + \dots) $$
Terms cancel telescopically:
$$ = I + (F - F) + (F^2 - F^2) + \dots = I $$
Thus, the series is the inverse of $(I-F)$.

**(b) Solution for b**
Given $b = (I-F)^{-1}e$.
Substitute the series expansion:
$$ b = (I + F + F^2 + \dots)e = e + Fe + F^2e + \dots $$

**(c) Iterative Convergence**
Define $b_0 = e$.
$b_1 = e + F b_0 = e + Fe$.
$b_2 = e + F b_1 = e + F(e + Fe) = e + Fe + F^2e$.
By induction, $b_k = \sum_{i=0}^{k} F^i e$.
This is the $k$-th partial sum of the series derived in (b). Since the series converges (eigenvalues < 1), $b_k \rightarrow b$ as $k \rightarrow \infty$.

**(d) Southwell Iteration (Shooting Radiosity)**
Instead of gathering, we shoot "unshot" radiosity $\Delta b$.
**Algorithm:**
1.  Initialize $b = e$, $\Delta b = e$.
2.  While $\max(\Delta b) > \epsilon$:
    a.  Pick patch $i$ with maximum unshot radiosity $\Delta b_i$ (often weighted by area).
    b.  For every other patch $j$:
        i.  Calculate form factor $F_{ji}$.
        ii. Calculate radiosity transfer: $\Delta R = \rho_j F_{ji} \Delta b_i$.
        iii. Update total radiosity: $b_j \leftarrow b_j + \Delta R$.
        iv.  Update unshot radiosity: $\Delta b_j \leftarrow \Delta b_j + \Delta R$.
    c.  Reset $\Delta b_i = 0$.
**Comparison:**
* **Naive (Gathering):** Matrix-vector multiplication $b_{k+1} = e + F b_k$. Cost is $O(n^2)$ per iteration. Updates all patches slowly.
* **Shooting:** Cost is $O(n)$ per step (updating $n$ patches from 1 source). Converges visually much faster because the brightest sources distribute light first, establishing the broad lighting structure early.

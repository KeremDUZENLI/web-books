### Answer 9.1
To prove linear independence, assume a linear combination sums to the zero function: $\sum_{i=1}^n c_i B_i(x) = 0$ for all $x \in [1, n]$.
Evaluate this sum at an integer vertex $k \in \{1, \dots, n\}$.
By definition, the basis function $B_i(x)$ is 1 if $x=i$ and 0 if $x$ is any other integer vertex.
Therefore, $\sum_{i=1}^n c_i B_i(k) = c_k \cdot 1 = c_k$.
Since the sum is identically zero, $c_k = 0$.
Since this holds for any $k$, all coefficients $c_i$ must be zero. Thus, the functions are linearly independent.

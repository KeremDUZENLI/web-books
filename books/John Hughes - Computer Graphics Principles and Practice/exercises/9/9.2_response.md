### Answer 9.2
**(a) Tetrahedron:**
The intersection of the star of vertex $v$ and the star of vertex $w$ is the set of all simplices containing both $v$ and $w$. In a tetrahedron, $v$ and $w$ are connected by an edge. Therefore, the intersection contains the edge $vw$ and the two triangular faces adjacent to that edge.

**(b) Octahedron:**
Let $v$ be the top vertex and $w$ be the bottom vertex. In an octahedron, the top and bottom vertices are distinct and not connected by any edge or face. Therefore, no simplex contains both $v$ and $w$. The intersection of their stars is the empty set.

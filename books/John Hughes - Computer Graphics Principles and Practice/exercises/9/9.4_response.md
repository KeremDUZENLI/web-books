### Answer 9.4
1.  **Linear Form:** Both barycentric interpolation and scanline interpolation (linear interpolation along edges followed by linear interpolation across scanlines) produce values that vary linearly with the $x$ and $y$ coordinates. For a triangle in the xy-plane, both methods define a function of the form $f(x,y) = Ax + By + C$.
2.  **Uniqueness:** A plane in 3D space ($z = f(x,y)$) is uniquely determined by three non-collinear points.
3.  **Conclusion:** Since both functions match the specified values $f_0, f_1, f_2$ at the three vertices of the triangle, and both are linear, they must describe the exact same plane. Therefore, they yield the same values at all interior points.

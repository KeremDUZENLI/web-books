### Exercise 7.2
We showed how to get from a point P on a line and a vector n normal to the line to the more familiar $Ax+By+C=0$ form. Figure out how to go the other way: Given a line defined by $Ax+By+C=0$, with at least one of A and B nonzero, find a point on the line. Hint: A normal vector to the line is given by $n=[\begin{matrix}A\\ B\end{matrix}]$. Use this to determine a point of the form $O+\alpha n$ (where O is the origin) that lies on the line, by solving for $\alpha$.

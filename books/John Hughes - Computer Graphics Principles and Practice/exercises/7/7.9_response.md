### Answer 7.9

The plane equation is $F(X) = n \cdot X + D = 0$ where $n=[A,B,C]^T$.
Consider a point $P$ on the plane. $n \cdot P + D = 0$.
Consider $P + n$. $F(P+n) = n \cdot (P+n) + D = (n \cdot P + D) + n \cdot n = 0 + ||n||^2$.
Since $||n||^2 > 0$, moving in the direction of $n$ increases the function value, moving into the positive half-space ($F(X) \ge 0$).

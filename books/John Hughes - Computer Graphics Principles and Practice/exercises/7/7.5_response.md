### Answer 7.5

Line 1: $P=(1,1), Q=(2,2)$. $v=Q-P=[1,1]^T$. $n=[-1,1]^T$.
$F_1(x,y) = (X-P)\cdot n = ([x,y]^T-[1,1]^T)\cdot[-1,1]^T = -(x-1) + (y-1) = -x+y$.

Line 2: $R=(3,3), S=(5,5)$. $v=S-R=[2,2]^T$. $n=[-2,2]^T$.
$F_2(x,y) = (X-R)\cdot n = ([x,y]^T-[3,3]^T)\cdot[-2,2]^T = -2(x-3) + 2(y-3) = -2x+2y$.

The functions are not identical ($F_2 = 2F_1$). They are proportional. The zero sets (where $F(x,y)=0$) are identical.

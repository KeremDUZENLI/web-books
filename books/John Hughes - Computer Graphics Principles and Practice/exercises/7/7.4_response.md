### Answer 7.4

Line 1: $P=(1,1), Q=(2,2)$.
$\gamma_1(t) = (1-t)P + tQ = (1-t)[1,1]^T + t[2,2]^T = [1+t, 1+t]^T$.

Line 2: $R=(3,3), S=(5,5)$.
$\gamma_2(t) = (1-t)R + tS = (1-t)[3,3]^T + t[5,5]^T = [3+2t, 3+2t]^T$.

The functions are not identical (e.g., $\gamma_1(0) = (1,1) \ne \gamma_2(0) = (3,3)$).
The lines are identical (both satisfy $y=x$).
The parametric formula maps a pair of points (defining a coordinate frame on the line) to a function. Since the input points differ, the resulting parameterizations (functions) differ, even though their image (the geometric line) is the same.

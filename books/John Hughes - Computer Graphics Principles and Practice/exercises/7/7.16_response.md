### Answer 7.16

Adding a large $L$ to coordinates means computing $(x_i+L)(y_{i+1}) - (x_{i+1}+L)(y_i)$.
Expanding: $x_i y_{i+1} - x_{i+1} y_i + L(y_{i+1} - y_i)$.
While mathematically correct (for closed polygons $\sum (y_{i+1}-y_i) = 0$), numerically this subtracts large numbers to find a small difference, leading to **catastrophic cancellation** and loss of precision.
Using a local coordinate system (centering) keeps coordinate values small, preserving significant digits and precision.

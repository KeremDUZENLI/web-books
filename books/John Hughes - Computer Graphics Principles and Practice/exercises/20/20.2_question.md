### Exercise 20.2
    
On the unit disk D in the plane, consider the vector field $n(x,z)=\mathcal{S}([\begin{matrix}-z&1&x\end{matrix}]^{T})$. Show that there's no function $y=f(x,z)$ on D with the property that the normal vector to the graph of f at $(x,f(x,z),z)$ is exactly $n(x,z)$, that is, that n is not the normal field of any surface above D. Hint: Assume without loss of generality that $f(1,0)=0$ Now traverse the curve $\gamma(t)=(cos~t,0,sin~t)$, $0<t<2\pi$ and see what you can say about the restriction of f to this curve.

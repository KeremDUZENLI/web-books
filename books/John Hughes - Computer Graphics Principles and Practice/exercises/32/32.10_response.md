### Answer 32.10


**(a) Micro-Lights Artifacts:**

* **Artifacts:** Replacing an area light with  fixed point lights creates **multiple distinct shadows** (banding) instead of a smooth penumbra. This is visually distracting ("bug eyes" effect).
* **Running Time:** Linearly increases with . If we use 100 micro-lights, shadow ray casting is 100x slower.

**(b) Stochastic Pairing:**
To get soft shadows with limited cost:

* **Strategy:** For each primary ray (pixel sample), pick **one** micro-light index randomly (or from a stratified set).
* **Pairing:** If we have  primary rays and  micro-lights:
* Do **not** pick randomly with replacement (might miss some lights, clump others).
* Use a **random permutation** (shuffle). Ray  connects to Micro-Light .


* **Problems:** If adjacent pixels use different permutations, the noise is high frequency (good). If the permutation is static per pixel, structured artifacts appear.

**(c) Implementation:**
Generate 25 stratified points on the light.
For each pixel:

1. Generate 25 stratified camera rays.
2. Shuffle the list of light points (or indices).
3. Trace Ray  and connect to Light Point .
**Result:** Smooth penumbras with noise that converges as  increases, avoiding the banding of static micro-lights and the clumping of pure random sampling.

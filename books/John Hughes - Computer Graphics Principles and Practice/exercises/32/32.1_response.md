### Answer 32.1
    
To account for the coefficient of extinction (absorption within the medium), we must attenuate the radiance of a ray as it travels through a transmissive medium.

According to Beer's Law (or the Bouguer-Lambert-Beer law), the radiance $L$ decreases exponentially with distance $d$. If $\sigma_t$ is the extinction coefficient (which may vary per RGB channel), the attenuation factor is $e^{-\sigma_t d}$.

**Modification to Path Tracer:**
1.  **Track Current Medium:** The renderer needs to track the material properties of the medium the ray is currently traversing.
2.  **Attenuate on Intersection:** When a ray travels from point $P$ to an intersection $Q$ (distance $d = \|Q-P\|$) inside a material with extinction $\sigma_t$:
    * Calculate transmission factor: $T = \exp(-\sigma_t d) = (\exp(-\sigma_{t,R} d), \exp(-\sigma_{t,G} d), \exp(-\sigma_{t,B} d))$.
    * Multiply the current ray throughput (or the radiance returned from $Q$) by $T$.

**Code Integration:**
In `pathTrace`, after finding an intersection at distance `dist`, if the ray is currently inside a medium:
```cpp
Color3 transmission = exp(-currentMedium.extinction * dist);
L_o *= transmission; // Attenuate light accumulated so far or arriving light

```

This requires passing the `currentMedium` state into the recursive `pathTrace` calls (updating it upon refraction events).

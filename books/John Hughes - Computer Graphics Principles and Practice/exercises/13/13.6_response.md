### Answer 13.6
(a) **Matrix M:**
The Look-At matrix is constructed from basis vectors $n, u, v$.
$n = \text{normalize}(Eye - \text{LookAt})$.
If we change LookAt point $L$ to $Eye + 2(L - Eye)$ (doubling the look vector length), the direction $L - Eye$ remains the same.
The normalized vector $n$ remains unchanged.
Consequently, $u = \text{up} \times n$ and $v = n \times u$ remain unchanged.
The matrix $M$ depends only on these basis vectors and the eye position.
**Conclusion:** The matrix $M$ does not change.

(b) **Image:**
Since the matrix $M$ is identical, the transformation applied to all scene geometry is identical.
**Conclusion:** The resulting image does not change.

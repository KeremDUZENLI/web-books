### Exercise 13.9
The unhinging transform in the zw-plane is uniquely determined by three properties: The plane $z=-n/f$ transforms to $z=0$; the eye at $(z,w)=(0,1)$ transforms to a point with $w=0$; and the plane $z=-1$ remains fixed.
Given $M = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$:
(a) Show that the condition on the transformation of the eyepoint implies $d=0$.
(b) Show that the third condition implies $c=-1$ and $a=b+1$.
(c) Show that the first condition implies $b = \frac{n}{f-n}$.

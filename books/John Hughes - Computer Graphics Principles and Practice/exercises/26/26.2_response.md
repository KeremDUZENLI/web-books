### Answer 26.2
    
(a) **Solid Angle:**
The solid angle is defined by the cone angle, not the radius of the sphere itself. However, if "cap of radius $r$" refers to the geodesic distance on the surface of sphere $R$, the angle is $\theta = r/R$.
Formula: $\Omega = 2\pi(1 - \cos(r/R))$.
If "radius $r$" refers to the chord radius or geometric radius of the cap base, $\sin\theta = r/R$.

(b) **Actual Area:**
By definition, solid angle $\Omega = \frac{\text{Area}}{R^2}$.
Area $= \Omega R^2 = 2\pi R^2 (1 - \cos(r/R))$.
Using the Archimedes projection principle (area of sphere slice = $2\pi R h$), where $h = R(1 - \cos\theta)$, the area is exactly $2\pi R h$.

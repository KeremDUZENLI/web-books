### Answer 26.4
    
(a) **Solid Angle:**
The incoming rays are within angle $\epsilon$ of $\omega_i$. This forms a cone of half-angle $\epsilon$.
The solid angle of a cone with half-angle $\epsilon$ is $\Omega = 2\pi(1 - \cos\epsilon)$.
**Simplified for small $\epsilon$:**
Using Taylor series $\cos\epsilon \approx 1 - \epsilon^2/2$:
$\Omega \approx 2\pi(1 - (1 - \epsilon^2/2)) = \pi \epsilon^2$.

(b) **Adjusting Radiance:**
We want incoming power $\Phi_{in}$ to be constant.
$\Phi_{in} \approx L \cdot A \cdot \Omega \cdot \cos\theta_i$.
Area $A = \pi r^2$ (approx for small $r$). Solid angle $\Omega \approx \pi \epsilon^2$.
$\Phi_{in} \propto L r^2 \epsilon^2$.
To keep $\Phi_{in}$ constant as $r, \epsilon \to 0$, $L$ must increase proportional to $\frac{1}{r^2 \epsilon^2}$.
Specifically, we must set $L = \frac{k}{r^2 \epsilon^2}$ for some constant $k$.

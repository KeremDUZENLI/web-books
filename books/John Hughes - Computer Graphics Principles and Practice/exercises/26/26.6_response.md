### Answer 26.6
    
Incoming radiance $L(\omega) = 10$ is constant for all directions in the hemisphere.
Irradiance $E$ is the integral of radiance weighted by the cosine of the incident angle over the hemisphere:
$E = \int_{\Omega} L \cos\theta d\omega = L \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos\theta \sin\theta d\theta d\phi$.
The integral of $\cos\theta$ over the hemisphere is exactly $\pi$.
$E = L \cdot \pi$.
Given $L = 10$,
$E = 10\pi \approx 31.416 \text{ W/m}^2$.

### Answer 26.3
    
(a) **Irradiance Calculation:**
Given:
* Total power $\Phi$.
* Source radius $r$.
* Source radiance $L = \frac{\Phi}{4\pi^2 r^2}$ (derived from $\Phi = L \cdot \text{Area} \cdot \pi$).
* Distance $R \gg r$.

The solid angle subtended by the source at $P$ is approximately the projected area of the source divided by distance squared:
$\Omega \approx \frac{\pi r^2}{R^2}$.
Since the source is distant and uniform, we approximate the integral for irradiance $E$:
$E \approx L \cdot \Omega \cdot \cos(0) = L \Omega$.
Substitute $L$ and $\Omega$:
$E \approx \left( \frac{\Phi}{4\pi^2 r^2} \right) \left( \frac{\pi r^2}{R^2} \right) = \frac{\Phi \pi r^2}{4 \pi^2 r^2 R^2} = \frac{\Phi}{4 \pi R^2}$.

(b) **Limit as $r \to 0$:**
The expression $E = \frac{\Phi}{4 \pi R^2}$ does not depend on $r$.
Thus, the limit as $r \to 0$ is $\frac{\Phi}{4 \pi R^2}$.
This confirms the inverse-square law for a point source of power $\Phi$.

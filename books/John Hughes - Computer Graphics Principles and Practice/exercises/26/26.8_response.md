### Answer 26.8
    
**Critique:**
The student's calculation uses the solid angle of the hemisphere ($2\pi \approx 6.28$) to relate power to radiance.
However, the relationship between total emitted power $\Phi$ and radiance $L$ for a Lambertian sphere involves the surface area $A$ and a factor of $\pi$ (from the cosine-weighted integral over the hemisphere), not the solid angle measure alone.
Specifically, the radiant exitance $M = \pi L$.
Total power $\Phi = M \cdot A = \pi L \cdot A$.
So $L = \frac{\Phi}{\pi A}$.

**Correct Calculation:**
1.  Power $\Phi = 1.2$ W.
2.  Area $A = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi (0.0175)^2 \approx 0.003848 \text{ m}^2$. (Note: The student calculated Area $\approx 0.000962$, which is $\pi r^2$, the cross-sectional area, not surface area. A spherical source emits from its surface).
3.  Radiance $L = \frac{1.2}{\pi (0.003848)} \approx \frac{1.2}{0.012089} \approx 99.26 \text{ W/(m}^2\text{sr)}$.

The student's $L$ was $0.006$. The correct $L$ is $\approx 99.3$.
The student's result is off by a large factor due to using the wrong area formula and incorrect radiometric relation.

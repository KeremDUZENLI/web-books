### Answer 26.10
    
(a) **Integral:**
P is at $(0,0,-h)$. Disk is in $z=0$ plane, radius $s$.
Let point on disk be $Q(r, \theta)$. Vector $v = Q - P = (r\cos\theta, r\sin\theta, h)$.
Distance $d = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2}$.
Cosine term $\cos\alpha = \frac{h}{d}$.
Solid angle differential $d\omega = \frac{dA \cos\alpha}{d^2} = \frac{r dr d\theta \cdot (h/d)}{d^2} = \frac{hr dr d\theta}{d^3}$.
Irradiance integral:
$E = \int L \cos\alpha d\omega = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{s} L \left( \frac{h}{d} \right) \frac{hr dr d\theta}{d^3} = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{s} L \frac{h^2 r}{(r^2 + h^2)^2} dr d\theta$.

(b) **Evaluation:**
$E = 2\pi L h^2 \int_{0}^{s} r(r^2+h^2)^{-2} dr$.
Let $u = r^2+h^2, du = 2r dr$.
Integral part: $\frac{1}{2} \int_{h^2}^{s^2+h^2} u^{-2} du = \frac{1}{2} \left[ -u^{-1} \right]_{h^2}^{s^2+h^2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{1}{h^2} - \frac{1}{s^2+h^2} \right)$.
$E = 2\pi L h^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{1}{h^2} - \frac{1}{s^2+h^2} \right) = \pi L \left( 1 - \frac{h^2}{s^2+h^2} \right) = \pi L \frac{s^2}{s^2+h^2}$.

(c) **Small h ($h < s/10$):**
If $s$ is very large ($s \to \infty$), $E \to \pi L (1) = \pi L$.
If $h$ is small relative to $s$, $\frac{s^2}{s^2+h^2} \approx 1$.
$E \approx \pi L$. This is the irradiance of an infinite plane.

(d) **Large h ($h > 4s$):**
$E = \pi L \frac{s^2}{s^2+h^2} = \pi L \left( \frac{s}{h} \right)^2 \frac{1}{1 + (s/h)^2}$.
If $s/h$ is small (e.g., $< 0.25$), $\frac{1}{1+(s/h)^2} \approx 1 - (s/h)^2 \approx 1$.
Thus $E \approx \pi L (s/h)^2$.

### Answer 30.9
    
Definition of Variance:
$$ Var[X] = E[(X - E[X])^2] $$
Let $\mu = E[X]$. Note that $\mu$ is a constant.
$$ (X - \mu)^2 = X^2 - 2X\mu + \mu^2 $$
Apply linearity of expectation:
$$ E[X^2 - 2X\mu + \mu^2] = E[X^2] - E[2X\mu] + E[\mu^2] $$
Since $\mu$ is constant, $E[\mu^2] = \mu^2$ and $E[2X\mu] = 2\mu E[X]$.
$$ = E[X^2] - 2\mu(\mu) + \mu^2 $$
$$ = E[X^2] - 2\mu^2 + \mu^2 $$
$$ = E[X^2] - \mu^2 $$
Substitute $\mu = E[X]$ back:
$$ Var[X] = E[X^2] - E[X]^2 $$

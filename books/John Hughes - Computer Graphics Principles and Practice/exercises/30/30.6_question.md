### Exercise 30.6
    
Consistency and bias. Consider a random variable $X\sim U(0,1)$. We saw in the chapter that we can estimate its mean by averaging n samples; this estimator will be unbiased. But consider the estimator
$$ Y_{n}=\frac{1}{n}\left(1+\sum_{i=1}^{n}X_{i}\right) $$
where the $X_{i}$ are iid $\sim U(0,1)$.

(a) Show that each $Y_{n}$ is a biased estimator of the mean $\overline{X}$.
(b) Show that the sequence $Y_{1},Y_{2},...$ is a consistent estimator of the mean.
(c) Construct a sequence $Z_{n}$ of unbiased estimators that are not consistent. (Hint: Consistency requires two things.)

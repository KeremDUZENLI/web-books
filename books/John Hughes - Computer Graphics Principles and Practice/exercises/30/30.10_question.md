### Exercise 30.10
    
Densities. Suppose that $([0,1],p)$ is a probability space, that is, p is a probability density. We observed that p may take on values larger than 1. In this problem, you'll show that this cannot happen too often. Show that if $p(x)\ge M$ on the interval $a\le x\le b\subset[0,1]$ then $b-a<\frac{1}{M}$. Hint: Write out the probability of the event [a, b] as an integral, and then use the assumption about p to give a lower-bound estimate of this integral.

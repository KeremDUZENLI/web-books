### Answer 30.6
    
**(a) Bias**
Estimator $Y_n = \frac{1}{n}(1 + \sum X_i)$.
$$ E[Y_n] = \frac{1}{n} E[1 + \sum X_i] = \frac{1}{n} (1 + \sum E[X_i]) $$
Since $X_i \sim U(0,1)$, $E[X_i] = 0.5$.
$$ E[Y_n] = \frac{1}{n} (1 + 0.5n) = \frac{1}{n} + 0.5 $$
The true mean $\overline{X} = 0.5$.
Bias $= E[Y_n] - \overline{X} = \frac{1}{n} \neq 0$. Thus it is biased.

**(b) Consistency**
An estimator is consistent if Bias $\to 0$ and Variance $\to 0$ as $n \to \infty$.
1. **Bias:** $\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} = 0$.
2. **Variance:**
$$ Var[Y_n] = Var[\frac{1}{n}(1 + \sum X_i)] = \frac{1}{n^2} Var[\sum X_i] $$
$$ = \frac{1}{n^2} \sum Var[X_i] = \frac{1}{n^2} (n \cdot \frac{1}{12}) = \frac{1}{12n} $$
$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{12n} = 0$.
Since both go to 0, the estimator is consistent.

**(c) Unbiased but Not Consistent**
Let $Z_n = X_1$ (simply return the first sample).
* **Bias:** $E[Z_n] = E[X_1] = 0.5$. It is unbiased for all $n$.
* **Variance:** $Var[Z_n] = Var[X_1] = 1/12$.
$\lim_{n \to \infty} Var[Z_n] = 1/12 \neq 0$.
The variance does not vanish, so it does not converge to the true value; it stays noisy forever. Thus, not consistent.

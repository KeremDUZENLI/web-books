### Exercise 30.4
    
In Equation 30.29, we showed that the pdf $p_{W}$ for the random variable W satisfied $\int_{a}^{b}p_{W}(r)$ $dr=b^{2}-a^{2}$ for every a and b in the interval [0, 1].

(a) Write $f(b)=\int_{a}^{b}p_{W}(r)dr$, and compute $f^{\prime}(b)$ in terms of $p_{W}$ using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
(b) Since $f(b)=b^{2}-a^{2}$ as well, compute $f^{\prime}(b)$ in a different way.
(c) Conclude that $p_{W}(r)=2r$ for any $r\in[0,1]$.

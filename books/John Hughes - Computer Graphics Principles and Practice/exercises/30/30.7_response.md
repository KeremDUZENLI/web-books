### Answer 30.7
    
**(a) Rejection Sampling $d(x)=x$ on [0,2]**
Box bounds: $x \in [0,2]$, $y \in [0, 2]$ (since max of $d(x)$ is 2).
Algorithm:
1. Pick $x \sim U(0,2)$, $y \sim U(0,2)$.
2. If $y < x$, accept $x$. Else reject.

**(b) Rejection Fraction**
Area under curve $\int_0^2 x dx = 2$.
Area of box $2 \times 2 = 4$.
Efficiency = Area(curve) / Area(box) = $2/4 = 0.5$.
Rejection rate = $1 - 0.5 = 50\%$.

**(c) Exponential Distribution**
$d(x) \propto e^{-20x}$ on $[0,1]$? (Prompt implies high decay).
Max value (at $x=0$) is $\approx 20$. Area $\approx 1$.
Box height 20, width 1. Area 20.
Efficiency $\approx 1/20 = 5\%$. Rejection rate 95%.
Rejection sampling becomes very inefficient for peaked distributions.

**(d) Unit Disk**
Box $[-1,1] \times [-1,1]$. Area 4.
Disk Area $\pi$.
Efficiency $\pi/4 \approx 78.5\%$. Very good.

**(e) High-Dimensional Ball**
Volume of box $[-1,1]^d = 2^d$.
Volume of unit ball $V_d \to 0$ relative to box as $d \to \infty$.
For $d=10$: Box $2^{10} = 1024$. Ball $V_{10} = \pi^5/120 \approx 2.55$.
Efficiency $\approx 2.55 / 1024 \approx 0.2\%$.
Rejection rate 99.8%. Curse of dimensionality makes rejection sampling computationally impossible in high dimensions.

**(f) Sphere vs Hemisphere**
* **Rejection:** Sample 3D ball (eff ~52%), normalize. Total cost: 1 sample + ~50% rejection.
* **Hemisphere Method (Listing 30.2):** 2 uniform samples, trig functions.
* **Comparison:** Rejection avoids trig but wastes random bits. Hemisphere uses trig but uses every sample. On modern GPUs, ALU (trig) is cheap, bandwidth/latency (rng state) varies. Rejection is often competitive for the sphere (Marsaglia method).

**(g) Box Constraints**
Function $f(x)=1+x$ on $[0,1]$. Max value is 2. Min value is 1.
Box $x \in [0,1]$.
Constraint on $y \in [a,b]$: The box must cover the graph.
Lower bound $a \le 0$ (usually 0).
Upper bound $b \ge \max(f(x)) = 2$.
If $b$ is large (e.g., 100):
Box area $1 \times 100 = 100$.
Curve area $\int_0^1 (1+x) dx = 1.5$.
Efficiency $1.5 / 100 = 1.5\%$.
Making $b$ large drastically increases the rejection rate.

### Answer 30.2
    
**(a) Importance Sampling Estimator**
Let $p(t)$ be the PDF of the generator xrand.
* For $t \in [0,1]$, $p(t) = 0.7$.
* For $t \in [1,2]$, $p(t) = 0.2$.
* For $t \in [2,3]$, $p(t) = 0.1$.

The importance sampling estimator is $X = \frac{f(t)}{p(t)}$.
* If $t \in [0,1]$, $f(t) = A$. Estimate $X = A/0.7 \approx 1.43A$.
* If $t \in [1,2]$, $f(t) = B$. Estimate $X = B/0.2 = 5B$.
* If $t \in [2,3]$, $f(t) = C$. Estimate $X = C/0.1 = 10C$.

**(b) Variance**
$E[X] = \int \frac{f(t)}{p(t)} p(t) dt = \int f(t) dt = A + B + C = \mu$.
$E[X^2] = \int \left(\frac{f(t)}{p(t)}\right)^2 p(t) dt = \int \frac{f(t)^2}{p(t)} dt$
$$ E[X^2] = \int_0^1 \frac{A^2}{0.7} dt + \int_1^2 \frac{B^2}{0.2} dt + \int_2^3 \frac{C^2}{0.1} dt $$
$$ E[X^2] = \frac{A^2}{0.7} + \frac{B^2}{0.2} + \frac{C^2}{0.1} \approx 1.43A^2 + 5B^2 + 10C^2 $$
$$ Var[X] = E[X^2] - \mu^2 = \left( \frac{A^2}{0.7} + 5B^2 + 10C^2 \right) - (A+B+C)^2 $$

**(c) Qualitative Comparison**
* **Lower Variance:** If the sampling probability matches the magnitude of the function. If $A$ is large and $C$ is small (e.g., $A=7, B=2, C=1$), we sample the large region $A$ 70% of the time. This "importance sampling" reduces variance compared to the uniform sampling in 30.1.
* **Higher Variance:** If $A$ is small and $C$ is large (e.g., $A=1, B=2, C=7$). We rarely sample the region $C$ where the function is huge. When we do hit $C$ (10% chance), we multiply by 10, creating a massive spike in the estimate ($10 \times 7 = 70$ vs mean 10). This increases variance.

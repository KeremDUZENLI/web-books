### Answer 30.10
    
Let $I = [a,b]$. The probability of the event $I$ is:
$$ P(I) = \int_a^b p(x) dx $$
We know that the total probability over the whole space $[0,1]$ is 1. Since $p(x) \ge 0$ everywhere, the probability of any subset cannot exceed 1.
$$ P(I) \le 1 $$
Given the condition $p(x) \ge M$ for all $x \in [a,b]$:
$$ \int_a^b p(x) dx \ge \int_a^b M dx = M(b-a) $$
Combining the inequalities:
$$ M(b-a) \le \int_a^b p(x) dx \le 1 $$
$$ M(b-a) \le 1 $$
$$ b-a \le \frac{1}{M} $$
Strict inequality ($<$) holds if $p(x)$ is non-zero outside $[a,b]$ or if $p(x) > M$ somewhere in $[a,b]$, but generally the bound is $\le 1/M$.

### Answer 30.3
    
**(a) Calculus Integral**
$$ I = \int_{1}^{3} \left(1 + \frac{x^2}{4}\right) dx $$
$$ I = \left[ x + \frac{x^3}{12} \right]_{1}^{3} $$
$$ I = \left( 3 + \frac{27}{12} \right) - \left( 1 + \frac{1}{12} \right) $$
$$ I = 2 + \frac{26}{12} = 2 + \frac{13}{6} = 2 + 2.166... = 4.166... $$

**(b) Monte Carlo Estimation**
Algorithm:
1. Sample $u \sim U(0,1)$.
2. Transform to interval: $x = 1 + u(3-1) = 1 + 2u$.
3. Evaluate $y = 1 + (x/2)^2$.
4. Estimator $X = (3-1) \cdot y = 2y$.
5. Repeat 100 times, compute average.
Expected Result: The average should be close to 4.17.

### Answer 7.2.9

**Proof:**
Given $A = S \Lambda S^{-1}$ with $S$ orthogonal ($S^{-1} = S^T$).
So $A = S \Lambda S^T$.
Transpose $A$:
$$ A^T = (S \Lambda S^T)^T = (S^T)^T \Lambda^T S^T = S \Lambda S^T $$
(Since $\Lambda$ is diagonal, $\Lambda^T = \Lambda$).
Therefore $A^T = A$, so $A$ must be symmetric.

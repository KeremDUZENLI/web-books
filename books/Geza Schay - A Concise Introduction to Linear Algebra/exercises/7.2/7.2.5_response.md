### Answer 7.2.5

**Matrix:** $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
Eigenvalues (Ex 7.1.5): $\lambda = 1, 2, 3$.
Eigenvectors: $\mathbf{v}_1=(1,0,-1)^T, \mathbf{v}_2=(0,1,0)^T, \mathbf{v}_3=(1,0,1)^T$.
$Q = \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{2} & 0 & 1/\sqrt{2} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1/\sqrt{2} & 0 & 1/\sqrt{2} \end{bmatrix}$.
$\Lambda = \text{diag}(1, 2, 3)$.

$A^{100} = Q \text{diag}(1, 2^{100}, 3^{100}) Q^T$.

Result:
$$ A^{100} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1+3^{100}}{2} & 0 & \frac{3^{100}-1}{2} \\ 0 & 2^{100} & 0 \\ \frac{3^{100}-1}{2} & 0 & \frac{1+3^{100}}{2} \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 7.2.15

**Rank of Symmetric Matrix $AA^T$:**
$\text{Rank}(AA^T) = \text{Rank}(A) = r$.
Since $AA^T$ is size $m \times m$ and has rank $r$ (where $r \le \min(m, n)$), the number of non-zero eigenvalues is $r$.
The number of zero eigenvalues is $m - r$.

**Conjecture:**
The multiplicity of the eigenvalue 0 is $m - r$.

**Proof:**
Symmetric matrices are diagonalizable. The algebraic multiplicity of 0 equals the geometric multiplicity (dimension of Null space).
$$ \dim(Null(AA^T)) = m - \text{Rank}(AA^T) = m - r $$

### Answer 7.2.12

**a. Exponential Solution:**
$\frac{d}{dt} (e^{At} \mathbf{u}_0) = A e^{At} \mathbf{u}_0 = A \mathbf{u}(t)$. Correct.

**b. Diagonalizable:**
$$ e^{At} = \sum \frac{(S\Lambda S^{-1} t)^k}{k!} = S \left(\sum \frac{(\Lambda t)^k}{k!}\right) S^{-1} = S e^{\Lambda t} S^{-1} $$
Entries of the diagonal matrix $e^{\Lambda t}$ are simply $e^{\lambda_i t}$.

**c & d. Circuit Solutions:**
Form the matrix $A$ based on $R, L, C$ values.
Find eigenvalues $\lambda$.
Solution form: $\mathbf{u}(t) = c_1 e^{\lambda_1 t} \mathbf{v}_1 + c_2 e^{\lambda_2 t} \mathbf{v}_2$.
If eigenvalues are complex (underdamped), the solution will oscillate.

### Answer 2.4.19

This exercises demonstrates **Block Multiplication**.

**Partition:**
$A = \begin{bmatrix} A_{11} & A_{12} \end{bmatrix}$ where $A_{11} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A_{12} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = I$.
$B = \begin{bmatrix} B_{11} \\ B_{21} \end{bmatrix}$ where $B_{11} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = O$ and $B_{21} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.

**Product:**
$AB = \begin{bmatrix} A_{11} & A_{12} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} B_{11} \\ B_{21} \end{bmatrix} = [A_{11} B_{11} + A_{12} B_{21}]$.

**Calculate terms:**
1.  $A_{11} B_{11} = A_{11} O = O = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
2.  $A_{12} B_{21} = I B_{21} = B_{21} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.

**Sum:**
$O + B_{21} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.

**Standard Multiplication Check:**
Row 1: $1(0)-2(0)+1(3)+0(1) = 3$. $1(0)-2(0)+1(2)+0(-1) = 2$.
Row 2: $3(0)+4(0)+0(3)+1(1) = 1$. $3(0)+4(0)+0(2)+1(-1) = -1$.
Result $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$. Matches.

### Answer 2.4.20

**Matrices:**
$M = [A \ \ B]$ (Partitioned into columns/blocks).
$N = \begin{bmatrix} C \\ D \end{bmatrix}$ (Partitioned into rows/blocks).
Dimensions: $A$ is $m \times k$, $B$ is $m \times p$. $C$ is $k \times n$, $D$ is $p \times n$.

**Product:**
$(MN)_{ij} = \sum_{r} M_{ir} N_{rj}$.
The summation index $r$ goes over the columns of $M$ (rows of $N$).
The first $k$ values correspond to $A$ and $C$.
The next $p$ values correspond to $B$ and $D$.
$\sum_{r=1}^{k+p} M_{ir} N_{rj} = \sum_{r=1}^k A_{ir} C_{rj} + \sum_{r=1}^p B_{ir} D_{rj}$.
The first sum is the definition of $(AC)_{ij}$.
The second sum is the definition of $(BD)_{ij}$.
Thus $(MN)_{ij} = (AC)_{ij} + (BD)_{ij}$.
So $MN = AC + BD$.

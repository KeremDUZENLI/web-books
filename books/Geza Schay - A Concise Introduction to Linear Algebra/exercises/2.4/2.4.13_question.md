### Exercise 2.4.13

Let $A$ be an $m \times p$ matrix and $B$ a $p \times n$ matrix. Show that the product $AB$ can also be written in the following alternative forms:

a. $AB = a_1 b^1 + a_2 b^2 + \dots + a_p b^p$

b. $AB = \begin{pmatrix} a_1 B \\ a_2 B \\ \vdots \\ a_m B \end{pmatrix}$ or $(AB)_i = \sum_{j=1}^p a_{ij} b^j$

c. $AB = \begin{pmatrix} A b_1 & A b_2 & \dots & A b_n \end{pmatrix}$ or $(AB)^j = \sum_{i=1}^p a^i b_{ij}$

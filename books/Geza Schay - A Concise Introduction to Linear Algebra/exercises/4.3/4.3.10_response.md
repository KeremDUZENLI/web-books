### Answer 4.3.10

**Goal:** Rederive Projection Matrix $P$ onto plane spanned by orthonormal $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}$.
Normal $\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v}$.
**Change of Basis:**
Let $Q = [\mathbf{u} \ \mathbf{v} \ \mathbf{n}]$. This is orthogonal ($Q^T = Q^{-1}$).
In the basis $\{\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{n}\}$, the coordinates of a vector $\mathbf{x}$ are $(c_u, c_v, c_n)^T = Q^T \mathbf{x}$.
**Projection Operation:**
Drop the $\mathbf{n}$ component: $(c_u, c_v, 0)^T$.
This operation in the new basis is matrix $D = \text{diag}(1, 1, 0)$.
**Return to Standard Basis:**
Apply $Q$ to the result.
$P = Q D Q^T$.
$P = [\mathbf{u} \ \mathbf{v} \ \mathbf{n}] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}^T \\ \mathbf{v}^T \\ \mathbf{n}^T \end{bmatrix}$.
$P = [\mathbf{u} \ \mathbf{v} \ \mathbf{0}] \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}^T \\ \mathbf{v}^T \\ \mathbf{n}^T \end{bmatrix}$.
$P = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T + \mathbf{v}\mathbf{v}^T$.
Alternatively, since $I = Q Q^T = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T + \mathbf{v}\mathbf{v}^T + \mathbf{n}\mathbf{n}^T$, we have:
$P = I - \mathbf{n}\mathbf{n}^T$.
This matches Equation 4.124.

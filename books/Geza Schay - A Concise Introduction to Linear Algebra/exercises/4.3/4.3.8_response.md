### Answer 4.3.8

**Axis:** Line through $P(1, 0, 0)$ with direction $\mathbf{u} = (1, 1, 1)$.
Normalize $\mathbf{u}$: $\mathbf{u}_n = (1/\sqrt{3}, 1/\sqrt{3}, 1/\sqrt{3})$.

**Strategy:**
1.  Translate $P$ to origin: $T(-1, 0, 0)$.
2.  Rotate to align $\mathbf{u}_n$ with $z$-axis. (Let this matrix be $R_{align}$).
    * Rotate about $z$ by $-\pi/4$ (align projection $(1,1,0)$ to $x$-axis $(1,0,0)$? No, to $x$ axis). $\phi = 45^\circ$.
    * Rotate about $y$ by $-\arcsin(\sqrt{2/3})$? Or use formula from 4.3.4.
3.  Rotate about $z$ by $\theta$: $R_z(\theta)$.
4.  Inverse Rotate: $R_{align}^T$.
5.  Translate back: $T(1, 0, 0)$.

**Matrix Form:**
$M = T(1,0,0) [R_{align}^T R_z(\theta) R_{align}] T(-1,0,0)$.
(The term in brackets is the rotation matrix about an axis passing through the origin).

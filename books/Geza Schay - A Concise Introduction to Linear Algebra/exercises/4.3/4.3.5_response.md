### Answer 4.3.5

**Rotation about arbitrary axis $\mathbf{p}$:**
Let $M$ be the matrix from Ex 4.3.4 that aligns $\mathbf{p}$ with the $z$-axis.
The rotation $R_{\mathbf{p}}(\theta)$ is given by:
$$ R_{\mathbf{p}}(\theta) = M^{-1} R_z(\theta) M $$
where $R_z(\theta) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta & 0 \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
(Note: If using homogeneous coordinates, extend matrices to $4 \times 4$ with 0s and a 1 in the bottom-right).

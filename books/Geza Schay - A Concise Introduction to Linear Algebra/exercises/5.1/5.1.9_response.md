### Answer 5.1.9

**1. Find Matrix $P$ (Projection onto $x=y$ plane):**
Basis for plane: $\mathbf{a}_1 = (1, 1, 0)^T, \mathbf{a}_2 = (0, 0, 1)^T$.
$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
$A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$. Inverse: $\begin{bmatrix} 1/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
$P = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
$P = \begin{bmatrix} 1/2 & 0 \\ 1/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/2 & 1/2 & 0 \\ 1/2 & 1/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.

**2. Projections of Vectors:**
* $\mathbf{v}_1 = (1, -1, 2)^T$:
    $P \mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5(1-1) \\ 0.5(1-1) \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
* $\mathbf{v}_2 = (1, 2, 3)^T$:
    $P \mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5(1+2) \\ 0.5(1+2) \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1.5 \\ 1.5 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
* $\mathbf{v}_3 = (2, -1, -2)^T$:
    $P \mathbf{v}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5(2-1) \\ 0.5(2-1) \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 \\ 0.5 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.

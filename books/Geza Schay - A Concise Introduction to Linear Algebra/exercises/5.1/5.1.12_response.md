### Answer 5.1.12

Let $Q = I - P$, where $P$ is the projection onto $Col(A)$.

**1. Prove $Q$ is a projection matrix (Symmetric and Idempotent):**
* **Symmetric:** $Q^T = (I - P)^T = I^T - P^T = I - P = Q$. (Since $P$ is symmetric).
* **Idempotent:** $Q^2 = (I - P)(I - P) = I - P - P + P^2$.
    Since $P^2 = P$, this becomes $I - 2P + P = I - P = Q$.
    So $Q$ is a projection matrix.

**2. Prove $Q$ projects onto $Col(A)^\perp$:**
Let $\mathbf{v}$ be any vector. $\mathbf{v} = P\mathbf{v} + (I-P)\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}$.
$\mathbf{p} \in Col(A)$.
Check if $\mathbf{q} \in Col(A)^\perp$.
We check $\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{q} = \mathbf{p}^T \mathbf{q} = (P\mathbf{v})^T ((I-P)\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{v}^T P^T (I-P) \mathbf{v}$.
Since $P$ is symmetric ($P^T=P$) and idempotent ($P(I-P) = P - P^2 = 0$):
The product is 0.
So the image of $Q$ is orthogonal to $Col(A)$.
$Range(Q) = Col(A)^\perp$.
This space is also known as the Left Nullspace of $A$.

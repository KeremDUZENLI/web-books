### Answer 5.1.15

**Given:** $A$ has independent rows.
We want projection onto $Null(A)$.
We know $Row(A)$ and $Null(A)$ are orthogonal complements in $\mathbb{R}^n$.
$Null(A) = Row(A)^\perp$.
Projection onto $Row(A)$ is the projection onto the column space of $A^T$.
Formula for $P_{row}$: $P_R = A^T ( (A^T)^T A^T )^{-1} (A^T)^T = A^T (A A^T)^{-1} A$.
(Note: Columns of $A^T$ are independent, so $(A A^T)^{-1}$ exists).
Projection onto complement ($Null(A)$):
$$ P_{null} = I - P_R = I - A^T (A A^T)^{-1} A $$

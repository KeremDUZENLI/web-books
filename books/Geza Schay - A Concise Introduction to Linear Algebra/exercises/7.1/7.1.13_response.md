### Answer 7.1.13

**Proof:**
Given $A\mathbf{s} = \lambda \mathbf{s}$. Since $A$ is nonsingular, $\lambda \neq 0$.
Multiply by $A^{-1}$:
$A^{-1} A \mathbf{s} = A^{-1} (\lambda \mathbf{s})$.
$I \mathbf{s} = \lambda (A^{-1} \mathbf{s})$.
$\mathbf{s} = \lambda (A^{-1} \mathbf{s})$.
Divide by $\lambda$:
$A^{-1} \mathbf{s} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \mathbf{s}$.
Thus $\mathbf{s}$ is an eigenvector of $A^{-1}$ with eigenvalue $\lambda^{-1}$.

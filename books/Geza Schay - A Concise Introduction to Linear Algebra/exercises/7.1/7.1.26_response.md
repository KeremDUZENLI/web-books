### Answer 7.1.26

**Conjecture:**
For $J_n$ (matrix of all ones size $n$):
1.  Eigenvalues are $n$ (multiplicity 1) and $0$ (multiplicity $n-1$).
2.  Eigenvector for $n$ is $\mathbf{1} = (1, 1, \dots, 1)^T$.
3.  Eigenvectors for $0$ form the subspace where entries sum to 0.

**Proof:**
$J \mathbf{1} = \begin{bmatrix} \sum 1 \\ \vdots \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} n \\ \vdots \end{bmatrix} = n \mathbf{1}$.
Since Rank($J$) = 1 (all rows equal), the dimension of the null space is $n-1$. Thus 0 is an eigenvalue with multiplicity $n-1$.

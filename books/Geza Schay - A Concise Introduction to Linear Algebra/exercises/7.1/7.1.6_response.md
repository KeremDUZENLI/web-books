### Answer 7.1.6

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$
This is upper triangular. Eigenvalues are diagonal entries.
$\lambda = 2$ (multiplicity 3).

**Eigenvectors:**
$(A-2I)\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0}$.
$x_3 = 0$. $x_1, x_2$ are free.
Basis: $\mathbf{s}_1 = (1, 0, 0)^T, \mathbf{s}_2 = (0, 1, 0)^T$.

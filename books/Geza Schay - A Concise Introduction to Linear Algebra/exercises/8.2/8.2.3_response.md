### Answer 8.2.3

**System:**
$$ [A|b] = \begin{bmatrix} 0.002 & 1 & 4 \\ 6 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Partial Pivoting Method:**
1.  **Calculate Scale Factors ($s_i = \max_j |a_{ij}|$):**
    Row 1: $s_1 = \max(|0.002|, |1|) = 1$.
    Row 2: $s_2 = \max(|6|, |-1|) = 6$.

2.  **Calculate Ratios ($r_i = |a_{i1}| / s_i$):**
    Row 1: $r_1 = |0.002| / 1 = 0.002$.
    Row 2: $r_2 = |6| / 6 = 1$.

3.  **Pivot Selection:**
    $\max(r_1, r_2) = 1$ (Row 2).
    Swap Row 1 and Row 2.
    $$ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -1 & 2 \\ 0.002 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

4.  **Elimination:**
    Multiplier $m = 0.002 / 6 \approx 0.000333$.
    $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - m R_1$.
    $a_{22} = 1 - (0.000333)(-1) = 1.000333 \approx 1.00$ (Machine).
    $b_2 = 4 - (0.000333)(2) = 3.999334 \approx 4.00$ (Machine).
    $$ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

5.  **Back Substitution:**
    $x_2 = 4 / 1 = 4$.
    $6 x_1 - 4 = 2 \implies 6x_1 = 6 \implies x_1 = 1$.

**Comparison:**
Result $(1, 4)^T$ matches the exact solution very closely. Partial pivoting avoided the large multiplier, preventing the loss of significant digits in the smaller entries.

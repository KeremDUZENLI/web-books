### Answer 1.3.8

**Given:** $P_0(1, -2, 4)$. Parallel to planes $3x - 4y + 3z = 7$ and $-x + 3y + 4z = 8$.

1.  **Find Direction:**
    Cross product of the two normal vectors: $\mathbf{n}_1 = (3, -4, 3)$ and $\mathbf{n}_2 = (-1, 3, 4)$.
    $$ \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{n}_1 \times \mathbf{n}_2 = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 3 & -4 & 3 \\ -1 & 3 & 4 \end{vmatrix} $$
    $$ = (-16 - 9, -3 - 12, 9 - 4) = (-25, -15, 5) $$
    We can use a simpler parallel vector by dividing by -5: $\mathbf{v}' = (5, 3, -1)$.

2.  **Parametric Form:**
    $$ x = 1 + 5t $$
    $$ y = -2 + 3t $$
    $$ z = 4 - t $$

3.  **Nonparametric Form:**
    $$ \frac{x-1}{5} = \frac{y+2}{3} = \frac{z-4}{-1} $$

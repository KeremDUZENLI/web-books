### Answer 2.5.16

**Definition:** $A^{-n} = (A^{-1})^n$.

**Show $A^{-n} = (A^n)^{-1}$:**
$(A^{-1})^n (A^n) = \underbrace{A^{-1} \dots A^{-1}}_{n} \underbrace{A \dots A}_{n}$.
Middle terms $A^{-1}A = I$ cancel out sequentially from the center.
Result is $I$.
So $(A^{-1})^n$ is the inverse of $A^n$.

**Show $A^{-m}A^{-n} = A^{-(m+n)}$:**
$A^{-m}A^{-n} = (A^{-1})^m (A^{-1})^n$.
By laws of exponents for the matrix $B = A^{-1}$, $B^m B^n = B^{m+n}$.
So $(A^{-1})^{m+n} = A^{-(m+n)}$.

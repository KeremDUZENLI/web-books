### Answer 2.5.9

**a. Left Inverse of $A$ ($3 \times 2$):**
We need $XA = I_2$. Transpose: $A^T X^T = I_2$.
$$ A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 & 2 \\ -1 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$
Solve $[A^T | I]$:
$\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|cc} 2 & 4 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & -1 & 2 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right]$.
1.  $R_2 \leftarrow 2R_2 + R_1 \implies (0, 2, 6 \mid 1, 2)$.
    Scale: $(0, 1, 3 \mid 0.5, 1)$.
2.  $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - 4R_2 \implies (2, 0, -10 \mid -1, -4)$.
    Scale: $(1, 0, -5 \mid -0.5, -2)$.
3.  $X^T = \begin{bmatrix} -0.5 & -2 \\ 0.5 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ (Particular solution with row 3 zero).
    $X = \begin{bmatrix} -0.5 & 0.5 & 0 \\ -2 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.

**b. Right Inverse $Y$:**
Need $AY = I_3$.
Rank $A = 2$. Rank $AY \le 2$. Rank $I_3 = 3$.
Impossible. No right inverse.

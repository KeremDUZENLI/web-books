### Answer 7.4.13

**Proof (Orthogonality of Eigenspaces):**
Let $A$ be Hermitian. Let $A\mathbf{u} = \lambda \mathbf{u}$ and $A\mathbf{v} = \mu \mathbf{v}$ with $\lambda \neq \mu$.
Note $\lambda, \mu$ are real.
Consider $\mathbf{u}^H A \mathbf{v}$.
1.  $\mathbf{u}^H (A \mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{u}^H (\mu \mathbf{v}) = \mu (\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v})$.
2.  $(A \mathbf{u})^H \mathbf{v} = (\lambda \mathbf{u})^H \mathbf{v} = \bar{\lambda} \mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v} = \lambda (\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v})$ (since $\lambda$ is real).
Since $A$ is Hermitian, $\mathbf{u}^H A \mathbf{v} = (A\mathbf{u})^H \mathbf{v}$.
So $\mu (\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v}) = \lambda (\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v})$.
$(\mu - \lambda) (\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v}) = 0$.
Since $\mu \neq \lambda$, we must have $\mathbf{u}^H \mathbf{v} = 0$.
Thus, eigenvectors from different eigenspaces are orthogonal.

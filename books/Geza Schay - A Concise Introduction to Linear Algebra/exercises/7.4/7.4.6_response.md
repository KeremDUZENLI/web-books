### Answer 7.4.6

**Matrix:** $R_\theta = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix}$.

**1. Eigenvalues:**
Characteristic Eq: $\det(R-\lambda I) = (\cos\theta - \lambda)^2 + \sin^2\theta = 0$.
$\cos^2\theta - 2\lambda\cos\theta + \lambda^2 + \sin^2\theta = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 2\lambda\cos\theta + 1 = 0$.
$\lambda = \frac{2\cos\theta \pm \sqrt{4\cos^2\theta - 4}}{2} = \cos\theta \pm \sqrt{-\sin^2\theta} = \cos\theta \pm i\sin\theta = e^{\pm i\theta}$.

**2. Eigenvectors:**
* For $\lambda = e^{i\theta}$:
    $\begin{bmatrix} -i\sin\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & -i\sin\theta \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{v} = 0 \implies i v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = -i v_1$.
    $\mathbf{s}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -i \end{bmatrix}$.
* For $\lambda = e^{-i\theta}$:
    $\mathbf{s}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ i \end{bmatrix}$.

### Answer 7.4.17

**a. Matrix Q:**
Using MATLAB `[Q,R] = qr(A)` provides an orthogonal matrix $Q$.
Since eigenvalues of orthogonal matrices have magnitude 1, and for 3D rotations one eigenvalue is always 1 (the axis), we find:
$\lambda_1 = 1, \lambda_2 = e^{it}, \lambda_3 = e^{-it}$.
The angle $t$ is the rotation angle.
The eigenvector for $\lambda=1$ is the axis of rotation.

**b. Decomposition Proof:**
Let $\mathbf{u}$ be the real eigenvector for $\lambda=1$ (normalized).
Construct an orthogonal basis $S = [\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}]$.
Change basis: $S^T Q S = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & Q_{sub} \end{bmatrix}$.
Since this block preserves orthogonality and determinant, $Q_{sub}$ is a $2 \times 2$ orthogonal matrix with eigenvalues $e^{\pm it}$.
This must be a rotation matrix $\begin{bmatrix} \cos t & -\sin t \\ \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix}$.
Thus $Q = S R S^T$.

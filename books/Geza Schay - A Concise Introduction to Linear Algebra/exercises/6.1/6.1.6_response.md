### Answer 6.1.6

**Proof:**
By Axiom 3 (linearity in the first row), if we multiply the first row by $c$, the determinant is multiplied by $c$.
Let $A$ be the matrix with rows $\mathbf{r}_1, \dots, \mathbf{r}_n$.
$cA$ is the matrix with rows $c\mathbf{r}_1, \dots, c\mathbf{r}_n$.
1.  Factor $c$ from row 1: $det(c\mathbf{r}_1, \dots, c\mathbf{r}_n) = c \cdot det(\mathbf{r}_1, c\mathbf{r}_2, \dots, c\mathbf{r}_n)$.
2.  Factor $c$ from row 2: $= c^2 \cdot det(\mathbf{r}_1, \mathbf{r}_2, c\mathbf{r}_3, \dots)$.
3.  Repeat $n$ times.
    $$ det(cA) = c^n det(A) $$

### Answer 6.1.12

**Skew-Symmetric Matrix ($A^T = -A$):**
$det(A) = det(A^T) = det(-A)$.
From Exercise 6.1.6, $det(-A) = (-1)^n det(A)$.
So, $det(A) = (-1)^n det(A)$.

**For $n=3$:**
$det(A) = -det(A) \implies 2 det(A) = 0 \implies det(A) = 0$.

**General Case:**
If $n$ is **odd**, $det(A) = -det(A) \implies det(A) = 0$.
If $n$ is **even**, $det(A) = det(A)$, which gives no restriction (e.g., $A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$, $det(A)=1$).

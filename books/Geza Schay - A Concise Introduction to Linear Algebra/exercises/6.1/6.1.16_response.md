### Answer 6.1.16

**Proof:**
Functions $1, x, x^2$ are linearly independent if $c_1(1) + c_2(x) + c_3(x^2) = 0$ implies $c_i = 0$ for all $x$.
Substitute three distinct values $a, b, c$:
$$ c_1 + c_2 a + c_3 a^2 = 0 $$
$$ c_1 + c_2 b + c_3 b^2 = 0 $$
$$ c_1 + c_2 c + c_3 c^2 = 0 $$
In matrix form:
$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & a & a^2 \\ 1 & b & b^2 \\ 1 & c & c^2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0} $$
The determinant of this coefficient matrix is the Vandermonde determinant $(b-a)(c-b)(c-a)$.
If $a, b, c$ are distinct, the determinant is non-zero.
Therefore, the only solution is the trivial solution $c_1=c_2=c_3=0$.
Thus, the monomials are linearly independent.

### Answer 6.1.10

**Proof:**
If $A$ and $B$ are similar, there exists an invertible matrix $S$ such that $A = S^{-1} B S$.
Take the determinant:
$$ det(A) = det(S^{-1} B S) $$
$$ det(A) = det(S^{-1}) det(B) det(S) $$
Scalars commute, so rearrange terms:
$$ det(A) = det(B) (det(S^{-1}) det(S)) $$
Using $det(S^{-1}) det(S) = det(S^{-1} S) = det(I) = 1$:
$$ det(A) = det(B) \cdot 1 = det(B) $$

### Exercise 5.2.3

In $\mathbb{R}^4$ find:

a. the projection of the vector $\mathbf{x} = (1, 2, 3, -1)^T$ into the subspace $U$ of Example 5.2.2,

b. the projection of $\mathbf{x}$ into $U^\perp$,

c. a basis for $U^\perp$, and

d. a vector $\mathbf{q}_4$ such that $\{\mathbf{q}_1, \mathbf{q}_2, \mathbf{q}_3, \mathbf{q}_4\}$ is an orthonormal basis for $\mathbb{R}^4$.

*(Note: Example 5.2.2 defines $U$ as the subspace spanned by $\mathbf{a}_1 = (2, 0, -1, 1)^T$, $\mathbf{a}_2 = (1, 1, 0, 1)^T$, and $\mathbf{a}_3 = (1, -3, -1, 3)^T$.)*

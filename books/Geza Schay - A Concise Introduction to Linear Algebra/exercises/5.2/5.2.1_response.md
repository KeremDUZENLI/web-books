### Answer 5.2.1

**Basis Vectors:** $\mathbf{u} = (2, 1, 2)^T$, $\mathbf{v} = (1, 0, -1)^T$.
**Target Vector:** $\mathbf{x} = (2, 3, 4)^T$.

1.  **Check Orthogonality:**
    $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 2(1) + 1(0) + 2(-1) = 2 - 2 = 0$.
    The vectors are orthogonal. We can use the projection formula for orthogonal bases.

2.  **Calculate Projections onto Basis Vectors:**
    * **Onto $\mathbf{u}$:**
        $\|\mathbf{u}\|^2 = 2^2 + 1^2 + 2^2 = 9$.
        $\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 2(2) + 3(1) + 4(2) = 4 + 3 + 8 = 15$.
        $\mathbf{p}_u = \frac{15}{9} \mathbf{u} = \frac{5}{3} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10/3 \\ 5/3 \\ 10/3 \end{bmatrix}$.

    * **Onto $\mathbf{v}$:**
        $\|\mathbf{v}\|^2 = 1^2 + 0^2 + (-1)^2 = 2$.
        $\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 2(1) + 3(0) + 4(-1) = -2$.
        $\mathbf{p}_v = \frac{-2}{2} \mathbf{v} = -1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.

3.  **Total Projection:**
    $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{p}_u + \mathbf{p}_v = \begin{bmatrix} 10/3 - 1 \\ 5/3 + 0 \\ 10/3 + 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7/3 \\ 5/3 \\ 13/3 \end{bmatrix}$.

### Answer 3.1.12

**Proof:**
We are given $\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{p}$ for all $\mathbf{p}$.
Let's choose a specific $\mathbf{p}$. The axiom of the Zero Vector says there exists a $\mathbf{0}$.
Let $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{0}$.
Then $\mathbf{0} + \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
By the identity property of the zero vector ($\mathbf{0} + \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{x}$), we have:
$\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.

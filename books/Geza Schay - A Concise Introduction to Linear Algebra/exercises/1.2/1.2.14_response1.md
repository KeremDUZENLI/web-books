### Answer 1.2.14

**a. Reverse Triangle Inequality:**
$$ ||\mathbf{p}| - |\mathbf{q}|| \leq |\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}| $$
Proof:
Write $\mathbf{p} = (\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}) + \mathbf{q}$.
Apply Triangle Inequality: $|\mathbf{p}| \leq |\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}| + |\mathbf{q}|$.
Rearrange: $|\mathbf{p}| - |\mathbf{q}| \leq |\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}|$.
(Repeat swapping $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ to cover the absolute value).

**b. Equality Condition:**
Equality occurs if the vectors are collinear and point in the same direction, essentially one vector "shortens" the other directly. Specifically, one vector must be a scalar multiple of the other with a non-negative scalar, such that they lie on the same ray from the origin.

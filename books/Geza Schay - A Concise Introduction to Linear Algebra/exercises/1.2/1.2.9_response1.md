### Answer 1.2.9

$\mathbf{p} = (2, -3, 1)$, $\mathbf{q} = (12, 3, 4)$.

1.  **Parallel Component (Projection):**
    $$ \mathbf{p}_{\parallel} = \frac{\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{q}}{|\mathbf{q}|^2} \mathbf{q} $$
    Dot Product: $(2)(12) + (-3)(3) + (1)(4) = 24 - 9 + 4 = 19$.
    Magnitude Squared $|\mathbf{q}|^2$: $144 + 9 + 16 = 169 = 13^2$.
    $\mathbf{p}_{\parallel} = \frac{19}{169}(12, 3, 4)$.

2.  **Perpendicular Component:**
    $$ \mathbf{p}_{\perp} = \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}_{\parallel} $$
    $$ \mathbf{p}_{\perp} = (2, -3, 1) - \frac{19}{169}(12, 3, 4) $$
    (You can simplify the fractions if needed, e.g., $x$-comp: $338/169 - 228/169 = 110/169$).

### Answer 1.2.15

**a. Polar Form:**
In 2D, a vector $\mathbf{p} = (x, y)$.
$x = |\mathbf{p}| \cos \phi$ and $y = |\mathbf{p}| \sin \phi$.
Thus $\mathbf{p} = |\mathbf{p}|(\cos \phi, \sin \phi)$.

**b. Unit Vector:**
$\mathbf{u}_p = \frac{\mathbf{p}}{|\mathbf{p}|}$.
Substituting the result from (a):
$\mathbf{u}_p = \frac{|\mathbf{p}|(\cos \phi, \sin \phi)}{|\mathbf{p}|} = (\cos \phi, \sin \phi)$.

### Answer 1.2.12

**Proof of Cauchy-Schwarz:**

1.  Start with $|\mathbf{p} - \lambda \mathbf{q}|^2 \geq 0$.
2.  Expand: $|\mathbf{p}|^2 - 2\lambda(\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{q}) + \lambda^2 |\mathbf{q}|^2 \geq 0$.
    This is a quadratic $A\lambda^2 + B\lambda + C \geq 0$ where $A = |\mathbf{q}|^2, B = -2(\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q}), C = |\mathbf{p}|^2$.
3.  To find the minimum, assume the derivative is zero (vertex of parabola):
    $2\lambda |\mathbf{q}|^2 - 2(\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q}) = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q}}{|\mathbf{q}|^2}$.
4.  Substitute this optimal $\lambda$ back into the inequality:
    $$ |\mathbf{p}|^2 - 2 \frac{(\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q})^2}{|\mathbf{q}|^2} + \frac{(\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q})^2}{|\mathbf{q}|^2} \geq 0 $$
    $$ |\mathbf{p}|^2 - \frac{(\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q})^2}{|\mathbf{q}|^2} \geq 0 $$
5.  Rearrange: $|\mathbf{p}|^2 |\mathbf{q}|^2 \geq (\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q})^2$.
    Taking the square root gives $|\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q}| \leq |\mathbf{p}| |\mathbf{q}|$.

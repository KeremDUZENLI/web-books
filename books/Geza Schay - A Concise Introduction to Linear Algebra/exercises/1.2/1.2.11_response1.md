### Answer 1.2.11



1.  Set the center of the circle at the origin $O$. Radius is $r$.
2.  Diameter endpoints are $\mathbf{a}$ and $-\mathbf{a}$ (where $|\mathbf{a}| = r$).
3.  Let $P$ be any point on the circle, vector $\mathbf{p}$, with $|\mathbf{p}| = r$.
4.  The lines from $P$ to the diameter ends correspond to vectors:
    $\mathbf{v}_1 = \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}$
    $\mathbf{v}_2 = \mathbf{p} - (-\mathbf{a}) = \mathbf{p} + \mathbf{a}$
5.  Check if they are perpendicular (dot product):
    $$ \mathbf{v}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v}_2 = (\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}) \cdot (\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{a}) $$
    $$ = \mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{a} = |\mathbf{p}|^2 - |\mathbf{a}|^2 $$
6.  Since $|\mathbf{p}| = r$ and $|\mathbf{a}| = r$, the result is $r^2 - r^2 = 0$.
    Zero dot product means a right angle.

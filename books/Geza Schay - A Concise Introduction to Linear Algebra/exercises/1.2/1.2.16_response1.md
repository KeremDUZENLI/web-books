### Answer 1.2.16

**a. Direction Cosines:**
The dot product $\mathbf{u}_p \cdot \mathbf{i}$ is $|\mathbf{u}_p| |\mathbf{i}| \cos \alpha_1 = 1 \cdot 1 \cdot \cos \alpha_1 = \cos \alpha_1$.
Since $\mathbf{u}_p = (u_1, u_2, u_3)$, then $u_1 = \cos \alpha_1, u_2 = \cos \alpha_2, u_3 = \cos \alpha_3$.

**b. Sum of Squares:**
Since $\mathbf{u}_p$ is a unit vector, $| \mathbf{u}_p |^2 = 1$.
$u_1^2 + u_2^2 + u_3^2 = 1 \implies \cos^2 \alpha_1 + \cos^2 \alpha_2 + \cos^2 \alpha_3 = 1$.

**c. Vector Form:**
$\mathbf{p} = |\mathbf{p}| \mathbf{u}_p = |\mathbf{p}| (\cos \alpha_1, \cos \alpha_2, \cos \alpha_3)$.

### Answer 1.2.10

**Proof:**
Expand the squared lengths using the dot product $|\mathbf{v}|^2 = \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{v}$:
1.  $|\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}|^2 = (\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}) \cdot (\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}) = |\mathbf{p}|^2 + 2\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q} + |\mathbf{q}|^2$
2.  $|\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}|^2 = (\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}) \cdot (\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}) = |\mathbf{p}|^2 - 2\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q} + |\mathbf{q}|^2$
3.  Add them:
    $$ \text{Sum} = (|\mathbf{p}|^2 + 2\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q} + |\mathbf{q}|^2) + (|\mathbf{p}|^2 - 2\mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{q} + |\mathbf{q}|^2) $$
    $$ = 2|\mathbf{p}|^2 + 2|\mathbf{q}|^2 $$

**Geometric Interpretation:**
In a parallelogram, the sum of the squares of the diagonals ($|\mathbf{p}+\mathbf{q}|$ and $|\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{q}|$) equals the sum of the squares of the four sides ($2|\mathbf{p}|$ and $2|\mathbf{q}|$).

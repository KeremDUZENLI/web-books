### Answer 4.2.2

**a. $T(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{x}$ ($\mathbf{a} \neq 0$):**
* **Range:** $\mathbb{R}$. (Dot product can be any real number). **Onto.**
* **Kernel:** Plane orthogonal to $\mathbf{a}$ ($\mathbf{a}^\perp$). Dim $n-1$. **Not 1-to-1** (unless $n=1$).

**b. $T(\mathbf{x}) = (A - \lambda I)\mathbf{x}$:**
* **Range:** $Col(A-\lambda I)$. **Onto** iff $\lambda$ is not an eigenvalue.
* **Kernel:** $Null(A-\lambda I)$ (Eigenspace). **1-to-1** iff $\lambda$ is not an eigenvalue.

**c. $T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{b}$:**
* Not linear. If considered as a map:
* **Range:** Affine subspace (Column space of A shifted by b).
* **Kernel:** (Pre-image of 0). Solutions to $A\mathbf{x} = -\mathbf{b}$. May be empty.

**d. $T(\mathbf{x}) = |\mathbf{x}|$:**
* **Range:** $[0, \infty)$. **Not onto** (negative reals missing).
* **Kernel:** $\{\mathbf{0}\}$. **Not 1-to-1** (many vectors have same length).

**e. $T(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{x}^T \mathbf{a}$:** Same as (a).

**f. $T(\mathbf{x}) = (\mathbf{a}^T \mathbf{x})\mathbf{b}$:**
* **Range:** Line spanned by $\mathbf{b}$ (Span$\{\mathbf{b}\}$). Dim 1. **Not onto** (if $m>1$).
* **Kernel:** Hyperplane orthogonal to $\mathbf{a}$ ($\mathbf{a}^\perp$). **Not 1-to-1**.

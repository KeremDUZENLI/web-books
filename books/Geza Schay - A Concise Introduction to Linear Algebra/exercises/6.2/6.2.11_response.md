### Answer 6.2.11

**Proof:**
We know $A \cdot adj(A) = det(A) I$.
Take the determinant of both sides:
$det(A \cdot adj(A)) = det(det(A) I)$.
LHS: $det(A) det(adj(A))$.
RHS: Since $det(A)$ is a scalar $c$, $det(cI) = c^n$.
So RHS = $(det(A))^n$.
Equation: $det(A) det(adj(A)) = (det(A))^n$.
Since $A$ is invertible, $det(A) \neq 0$. Divide by $det(A)$:
$$ det(adj(A)) = (det(A))^{n-1} $$

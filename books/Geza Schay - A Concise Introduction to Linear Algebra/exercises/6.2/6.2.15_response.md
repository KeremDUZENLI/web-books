### Answer 6.2.15

**Equation:**
$$ \begin{vmatrix} y & x^2 & x & 1 \\ y_1 & x_1^2 & x_1 & 1 \\ \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots \end{vmatrix} = 0 $$
1.  **Form:** Expand along row 1.
    $y C_1 + x^2 C_2 + x C_3 + 1 C_4 = 0$.
    $y = -\frac{C_2}{C_1} x^2 - \frac{C_3}{C_1} x - \frac{C_4}{C_1}$.
    This is the form $y = ax^2 + bx + c$.
2.  **Points:**
    Substitute $(x_1, y_1)$ for $(x,y)$. Row 1 equals Row 2. Det = 0.
    Similarly for $(x_2, y_2)$ and $(x_3, y_3)$.
The equation represents the unique parabola passing through the three points.

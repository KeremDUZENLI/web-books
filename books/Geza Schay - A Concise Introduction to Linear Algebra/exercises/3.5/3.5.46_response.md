### Answer 3.5.46

**Conjecture:**
$Nullity(A) = Nullity(A^T A) = Nullity(A A^T)$?
Actually: $Nullity(A^T A) = Nullity(A)$.
$Nullity(A A^T) = Nullity(A^T)$.
Note $A$ is $3 \times 4$.
Rank(A) $\le 3$.
$Nullity(A) = 4 - r$.
$Nullity(A^T) = 3 - r$.
$Nullity(A^T A)$ (4x4) $= 4 - r$.
$Nullity(A A^T)$ (3x3) $= 3 - r$.
Pattern: $A^T A$ shares nullity with $A$. $A A^T$ shares nullity with $A^T$.

### Answer 3.5.17

**Statement:** If cols of $B$ are dependent $\implies$ cols of $AB$ are dependent.
**Proof:**
Cols of $B$ dependent $\implies B\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ for some $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$.
Multiply by $A$: $AB\mathbf{x} = A\mathbf{0} = \mathbf{0}$.
This means the linear combination of columns of $AB$ with coefficients $\mathbf{x}$ is 0.
Since $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$, columns of $AB$ are dependent.

**Converse:** If cols of $AB$ dependent, are cols of $B$ dependent?
**False.** Let $B = I$ (independent), $A = 0$. $AB = 0$ (dependent).
$AB$ can be dependent even if $B$ is independent, if $A$ "kills" the vectors (maps them to zero).

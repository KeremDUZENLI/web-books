### Answer 3.5.18

$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
$U = Span(Col_1, Col_2) = Span((1,0,0)^T, (0,2,0)^T)$. Basis: $\{(1,0,0)^T, (0,1,0)^T\}$. ($xy$-plane).
$V = Span(Col_4, Col_5) = Span((2,0,2)^T, (-1,0,2)^T)$.
Simplify $V$: $(2,0,2) \to (1,0,1)$. $(-1,0,2) + (1,0,1) = (0,0,3) \to (0,0,1)$.
Basis $V$: $\{(1,0,0)^T, (0,0,1)^T\}$. ($xz$-plane).

* **Basis $U$:** $\{(1,0,0)^T, (0,1,0)^T\}$.
* **Basis $V$:** $\{(1,0,0)^T, (0,0,1)^T\}$.
* **Basis $U^\perp$:** Vector orth to $x, y$ axes. $\{(0,0,1)^T\}$.
* **Basis $V^\perp$:** Vector orth to $x, z$ axes. $\{(0,1,0)^T\}$.
* **Basis $U \cap V$:** Intersection of $xy$ and $xz$ planes is $x$-axis. $\{(1,0,0)^T\}$.
* **Basis $U + V$:** $xy$ plane + $xz$ plane spans $\mathbb{R}^3$. $\{(1,0,0)^T, (0,1,0)^T, (0,0,1)^T\}$.

### Answer 3.5.22

**Proof:**
($\Rightarrow$) Direct Sum $\implies U \cap V = \{0\}$.
Suppose $x = u_1 + v_1 = u_2 + v_2$.
Then $u_1 - u_2 = v_2 - v_1$.
LHS $\in U$. RHS $\in V$.
Intersection is 0, so $u_1 - u_2 = 0 \implies u_1 = u_2$.
And $v_2 - v_1 = 0 \implies v_1 = v_2$.
Uniqueness holds.

($\Leftarrow$) Uniqueness $\implies U \cap V = \{0\}$.
Let $w \in U \cap V$.
$w = w + 0$ ( $w \in U, 0 \in V$ ).
$w = 0 + w$ ( $0 \in U, w \in V$ ).
Since decomposition is unique, $w=0$.
Intersection is trivial.

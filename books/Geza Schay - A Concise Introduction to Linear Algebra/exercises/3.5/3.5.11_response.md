### Answer 3.5.11

**Proof:**
Let $\mathbf{x} \in Null(A) \cap Null(B)$.
This means $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ AND $B\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
Stacking these equations:
$$ \begin{bmatrix} A\mathbf{x} \\ B\mathbf{x} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{0} \\ \mathbf{0} \end{bmatrix} \implies \begin{bmatrix} A \\ B \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} $$
Thus $\mathbf{x} \in Null\left(\begin{bmatrix} A \\ B \end{bmatrix}\right)$.
The logic is reversible, so the sets are equal.

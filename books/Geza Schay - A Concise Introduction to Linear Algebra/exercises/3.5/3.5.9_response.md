### Answer 3.5.9

**Proof:**
$U \cap V$ is a subspace of $X$.
We must show $U \cap V \subseteq U+V$.
Let $\mathbf{x} \in U \cap V$.
This implies $\mathbf{x} \in U$ (and $\mathbf{x} \in V$).
Since $\mathbf{0} \in V$, we can write $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{0}$.
Here $\mathbf{x} \in U$ and $\mathbf{0} \in V$.
Thus $\mathbf{x} \in U+V$.
So $U \cap V$ is a subset of $U+V$. Since it is also a vector space itself, it is a subspace.

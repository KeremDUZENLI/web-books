### Answer 3.5.20

**Proof:**
$U+V = Span(U \cup V)$.
1.  $U \subseteq Span(U \cup V)$ and $V \subseteq Span(U \cup V)$, so their sum is contained in the span.
2.  Any vector in $Span(U \cup V)$ is a linear combination $\sum c_i u_i + \sum d_j v_j$.
    $\sum c_i u_i \in U$ and $\sum d_j v_j \in V$.
    So the combination is in $U+V$.
    Thus the sets are equal.

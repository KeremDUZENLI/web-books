### Answer 3.5.16

**Proof:**
1.  **Column Space:** $Col(AB) = \{ AB\mathbf{x} \}$. Let $\mathbf{y} = B\mathbf{x}$. Then $AB\mathbf{x} = A\mathbf{y}$.
    Thus every vector in $Col(AB)$ is in $Col(A)$. $Col(AB) \subseteq Col(A)$.
    Implies $Rank(AB) \le Rank(A)$.
2.  **Row Space:** $Row(AB) = Col((AB)^T) = Col(B^T A^T)$.
    By part 1 logic applied to transpose: $Col(B^T A^T) \subseteq Col(B^T) = Row(B)$.
    Thus $Row(AB) \subseteq Row(B)$.
    Implies $Rank(AB) \le Rank(B)$.

**Conclusion:** $Rank(AB) \le \min(Rank(A), Rank(B))$.
For Nullities:
$Null(B) \subseteq Null(AB)$ (if $B\mathbf{x}=0 \implies AB\mathbf{x}=0$).
$Nullity(AB) \ge Nullity(B)$.

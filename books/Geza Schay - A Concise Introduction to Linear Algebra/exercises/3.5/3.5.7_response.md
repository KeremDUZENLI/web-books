### Answer 3.5.7

**Proof:**
Let $\mathbf{x}$ be a vector in $Row(A) \cap Null(A)$.
1.  Since $\mathbf{x} \in Row(A)$, $\mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{y}$ for some vector $\mathbf{y}$.
2.  Since $\mathbf{x} \in Null(A)$, $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
Substitute (1) into (2):
$A(A^T \mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{0}$.
Multiply on the left by $\mathbf{y}^T$:
$\mathbf{y}^T A A^T \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}^T \mathbf{0} = 0$.
$(A^T \mathbf{y})^T (A^T \mathbf{y}) = 0$.
$\mathbf{x}^T \mathbf{x} = 0$.
$\|\mathbf{x}\|^2 = 0 \implies \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
Thus, the only vector in the intersection is the zero vector.

### Answer 3.5.4

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$
Columns 1, 2, 4 are pivot columns (after scaling row 3). Rank $r=3$.

**a. Dimensions:**
$Row=3, Col=3, Null=2, LeftNull=0$.

**b. Decomposition:**
$\mathbf{x} = (6, 2, 1, 4, 8)^T$.
Note rows are orthogonal:
$r_1 = (1,0,0,2,-1)$, $r_2 = (0,2,0,0,0)$, $r_3 = (0,0,0,2,2)$.
$r_1 \cdot r_2 = 0$. $r_2 \cdot r_3 = 0$.
$r_1 \cdot r_3 = 4 - 2 = 2 \neq 0$. Not orthogonal.
Orthogonalize $r_3$ against $r_1$:
$\mathbf{u}_3 = r_3 - \frac{r_3 \cdot r_1}{r_1 \cdot r_1} r_1 = (0,0,0,2,2) - \frac{2}{6}(1,0,0,2,-1) = (0,0,0,2,2) - (1/3, 0, 0, 2/3, -1/3) = (-1/3, 0, 0, 4/3, 7/3)$.
This is tedious. Let's use $A A^T \mathbf{y} = A \mathbf{x}$.
$A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 6+8-8 \\ 4 \\ 8+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 4 \\ 24 \end{bmatrix}$.
$AA^T = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 4 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$.
Solve $\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 4 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 4 \\ 24 \end{bmatrix}$.
$4y_2 = 4 \implies y_2 = 1$.
$6y_1 + 2y_3 = 6$
$2y_1 + 8y_3 = 24 \implies y_1 + 4y_3 = 12 \implies y_1 = 12 - 4y_3$.
$6(12-4y_3) + 2y_3 = 6 \implies 72 - 24y_3 + 2y_3 = 6 \implies -22y_3 = -66 \implies y_3 = 3$.
$y_1 = 12 - 12 = 0$.
$\mathbf{y} = (0, 1, 3)^T$.
$\mathbf{x}_R = A^T \mathbf{y} = 0 r_1^T + 1 r_2^T + 3 r_3^T$.
$\mathbf{x}_R = (0, 2, 0, 0, 0) + (0, 0, 0, 6, 6) = (0, 2, 0, 6, 6)^T$.
$\mathbf{x}_0 = (6, 2, 1, 4, 8) - (0, 2, 0, 6, 6) = (6, 0, 1, -2, 2)^T$.

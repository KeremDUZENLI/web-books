### Answer 3.3.5

**Goal:** Solve $x_1\mathbf{a}_1 + x_2\mathbf{a}_2 + x_3\mathbf{a}_3 = \mathbf{b}$.
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 4 & 0 & 7 \\ 2 & -3 & 5 & 16 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 & -3 \end{array} \right] $$

**Reduction:**
1.  Swap $R_1, R_3$: $(1, 2, -1 \mid -3)$.
2.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1$: $(0, -7, 7 \mid 22)$.
3.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 4R_1$: $(0, -4, 4 \mid 19)$.
4.  Observe $R_2$ and $R_3$.
    $R_2$: $-7x_2 + 7x_3 = 22 \implies x_2 - x_3 = -22/7$.
    $R_3$: $-4x_2 + 4x_3 = 19 \implies x_2 - x_3 = -19/4$.
    Contradiction: $-22/7 \neq -19/4$.

**Result:**
The system is inconsistent. $\mathbf{b}$ cannot be written as a linear combination of these vectors.

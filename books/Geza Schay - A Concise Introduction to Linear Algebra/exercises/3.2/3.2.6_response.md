### Answer 3.2.6

**Set:** $U=\{x \in \mathbb{R}^3 \mid x_{1}=x_{2} \text{ and } x_{3}=0\}$.

**Decision: Yes, this is a subspace.**

**Proof:**
This set is the **intersection** of two subspaces ($x_1=x_2$ and $x_3=0$).
1.  $\mathbf{0} = (0,0,0)$ satisfies both.
2.  If $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}$ satisfy both, their sum satisfies both.
    $(u_1+v_1) = (u_2+v_2)$ and $(u_3+v_3) = 0+0=0$.
3.  Scalar multiples satisfy both conditions.

*Geometric Interpretation:* This is the line $x=y$ in the $xy$-plane.

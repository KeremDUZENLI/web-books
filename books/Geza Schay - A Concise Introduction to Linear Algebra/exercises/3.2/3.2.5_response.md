### Answer 3.2.5

**Set:** $U=\{x \in \mathbb{R}^3 \mid x_{1}=x_{2} \text{ or } x_{3}=0\}$.

**Decision: No, this is not a subspace.**

**Proof (Counterexample for Addition):**
$U$ is the union of two subspaces (a plane $P_1: x_1=x_2$ and a plane $P_2: x_3=0$).
Let $\mathbf{u} = (1, 1, 1)$. In $U$ because $x_1=x_2$.
Let $\mathbf{v} = (0, 1, 0)$. In $U$ because $x_3=0$.
$\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = (1, 2, 1)$.
Check conditions:
1. $x_1 = x_2$? $1 = 2$. False.
2. $x_3 = 0$? $1 = 0$. False.
Since neither condition holds, the sum is not in $U$.

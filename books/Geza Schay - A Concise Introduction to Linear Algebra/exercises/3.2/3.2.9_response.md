### Answer 3.2.9

**Proof that Intersection is a Subspace:**

Let $W = U \cap V$.
1.  **Zero Vector:** Since $U$ and $V$ are subspaces, $\mathbf{0} \in U$ and $\mathbf{0} \in V$. Thus $\mathbf{0} \in W$.
2.  **Closure under Addition:** Let $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in W$.
    Then $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in U$ (sum is in $U$) and $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in V$ (sum is in $V$).
    Therefore, $\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y} \in U \cap V = W$.
3.  **Closure under Scalar Multiplication:** Let $\mathbf{x} \in W$.
    Then $\mathbf{x} \in U$ (so $c\mathbf{x} \in U$) and $\mathbf{x} \in V$ (so $c\mathbf{x} \in V$).
    Therefore, $c\mathbf{x} \in W$.

$W$ satisfies all conditions.

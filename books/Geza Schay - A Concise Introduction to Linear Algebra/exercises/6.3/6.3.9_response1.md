### Answer 6.3.9

**Transformation:** $T(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{x}$.

**a. Linearity:**
$\mathbf{a} \times (\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{y}$.
$\mathbf{a} \times (c\mathbf{x}) = c(\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{x})$.
(Properties of cross product). Yes, linear.

**b. Matrix $[T]$:**
Let $\mathbf{a} = (a_1, a_2, a_3)$.
$\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{x} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ x_1 & x_2 & x_3 \end{vmatrix}$.
$x$-comp: $-a_3 x_2 + a_2 x_3$.
$y$-comp: $a_3 x_1 - a_1 x_3$.
$z$-comp: $-a_2 x_1 + a_1 x_2$.
$$ [T] = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -a_3 & a_2 \\ a_3 & 0 & -a_1 \\ -a_2 & a_1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$
This is a **skew-symmetric** matrix.

**c. Null Space:**
$T(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{0} \implies \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
This happens if $\mathbf{x}$ is parallel to $\mathbf{a}$ (or $\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0}$).
$Null(T) = \{ t\mathbf{a} \mid t \in \mathbb{R} \}$.
Geometrically: The line passing through origin in direction $\mathbf{a}$.

**d. Rank:**
Dimension of Domain = 3. Dim(Null) = 1.
Rank = $3 - 1 = 2$. (Assuming $\mathbf{a} \neq \mathbf{0}$).

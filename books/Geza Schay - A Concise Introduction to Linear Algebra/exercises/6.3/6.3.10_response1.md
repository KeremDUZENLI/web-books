### Answer 6.3.10

**Coriolis Force:** $\mathbf{F} = 2m\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{\omega}$.
$\mathbf{\omega}$ is Earth's angular velocity vector, pointing North (along axis).
$\mathbf{v}$ is velocity of air mass.

**Northern Hemisphere:**
$\mathbf{\omega}$ points up/North out of the ground (roughly).
Imagine air moving towards a low pressure center ($L$).
1.  **Velocity North:** $\mathbf{v}$ North. $\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{\omega} \approx 0$ (parallel, but $\omega$ has vertical component). Consider horizontal plane. Vertical component of $\omega$ is "out of page".
    $\mathbf{v}$ (up page) $\times$ $\mathbf{\omega}$ (out of page) $\to$ Force Right (East).
2.  **Velocity South:** $\mathbf{v}$ South. $\mathbf{v}$ (down) $\times$ $\mathbf{\omega}$ (out) $\to$ Force Right (West).
3.  **Result:** Moving air is deflected to the **right**.
    Air rushing inward to a Low pressure center deflects right, creating a **Counter-Clockwise** spiral.

**Southern Hemisphere:**
Effective vertical component of $\mathbf{\omega}$ is "into the ground" (South Pole).
$\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{\omega}_{down}$ deflects to the **left**.
Air rushing inward deflects left, creating a **Clockwise** spiral.

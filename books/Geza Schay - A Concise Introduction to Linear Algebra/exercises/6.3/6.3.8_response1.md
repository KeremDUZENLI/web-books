### Answer 6.3.8

**Identity:** $\mathbf{v} = (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}) \times (\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{d})$.
This vector is perpendicular to $\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}$ (so it lies in the plane of $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}$) AND perpendicular to $\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{d}$ (so it lies in the plane of $\mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d}$).
Thus $\mathbf{v}$ is the intersection line of the two planes.

**1. Expansion in $\mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d}$ (treating $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ as a single vector $\mathbf{u}$):**
$\mathbf{u} \times (\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{d}) = (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{d})\mathbf{c} - (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{c})\mathbf{d}$.
$\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{d} = (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}) \cdot \mathbf{d} = det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{d})$.
$\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{c} = (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}) \cdot \mathbf{c} = det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c})$.
So $\mathbf{v} = [det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{d})]\mathbf{c} - [det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c})]\mathbf{d}$. (Matches second formula).

**2. Expansion in $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}$ (treating $\mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{d}$ as $\mathbf{w}$):**
$(\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}) \times \mathbf{w} = - \mathbf{w} \times (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b})$.
$= - [ (\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{b})\mathbf{a} - (\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{a})\mathbf{b} ]$.
$= (\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{a})\mathbf{b} - (\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{b})\mathbf{a}$.
$\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{a} = (\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{d}) \cdot \mathbf{a} = det(\mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d}, \mathbf{a}) = det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d})$.
$\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{b} = (\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{d}) \cdot \mathbf{b} = det(\mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d}, \mathbf{b}) = det(\mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d})$.
So $\mathbf{v} = [det(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d})]\mathbf{b} - [det(\mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}, \mathbf{d})]\mathbf{a}$. (Matches first formula).

### Answer 6.3.1

**Vectors:** $\mathbf{u}=(1,-1,0)^T, \mathbf{v}=(1,2,0)^T$.

**1. Cross Product:**
Since both vectors are in the $xy$-plane ($z=0$), the cross product will point in the $z$-direction.
$$ \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 1 & -1 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & 0 \end{vmatrix} = \mathbf{i}(0) - \mathbf{j}(0) + \mathbf{k}(2 - (-1)) = 3\mathbf{k} $$
$$ \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} = (0, 0, 3)^T $$
Magnitude $|\mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v}| = 3$.

**2. Elementary Geometry:**
The area of a parallelogram is Base $\times$ Height.
Base length $|\mathbf{u}| = \sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{2}$.
Height is the projection of $\mathbf{v}$ onto the vector orthogonal to $\mathbf{u}$. Or simply use the determinant of the 2D matrix formed by $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}$:
Area $= |\det \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}| = |2 - (-1)| = |3| = 3$.
Alternatively, angle $\theta$. $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 1-2 = -1$.
$\cos \theta = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{5}} = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{10}}$.
$\sin \theta = \sqrt{1 - 1/10} = 3/\sqrt{10}$.
Area $= |\mathbf{u}| |\mathbf{v}| \sin \theta = \sqrt{2}\sqrt{5} \frac{3}{\sqrt{10}} = \sqrt{10}\frac{3}{\sqrt{10}} = 3$.
Matches.

### Answer 8.1.6

**1. Solve $L\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{b}$ (Forward Substitution):**
$L$ is $m \times m$. $\mathbf{b}$ is $m \times 1$.
Ops: $1 + 2 + \dots + (m-1) \approx m^2/2$.

**2. Solve $U\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{c}$ (Back Substitution):**
Assume $A$ is square $n \times n$ for a unique solution.
$U$ is $n \times n$.
Ops: $1 + 2 + \dots + n \approx n^2/2$.

**Total for solving (given L, U):**
$\approx n^2$ long operations (multiplications/divisions).
(Much faster than the factorization step of $n^3/3$).

### Answer 7.3.2

**Equation:** $7x_1^2 + 48x_1x_2 - 7x_2^2 = 25$.
**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 24 \\ 24 & -7 \end{bmatrix} $$

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    $\lambda^2 - \text{tr}(A)\lambda + \det(A) = 0$. Trace=0. Det=$-49 - 576 = -625$.
    $\lambda^2 - 625 = 0 \implies \lambda = \pm 25$.
    $\lambda_1 = 25, \lambda_2 = -25$.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**

    - $\lambda_1 = 25$: $\begin{bmatrix} -18 & 24 \\ 24 & -32 \end{bmatrix} \implies -3x_1 + 4x_2 = 0 \implies \mathbf{v}_1 = (4, 3)^T$.
      Direction: $\mathbf{u}_1 = (0.8, 0.6)^T$.
    - $\lambda_2 = -25$: Orthogonal to $\mathbf{v}_1$. $\mathbf{v}_2 = (-3, 4)^T$.
      Direction: $\mathbf{u}_2 = (-0.6, 0.8)^T$.

3.  **Lengths/Equation:**
    $25y_1^2 - 25y_2^2 = 25 \implies y_1^2 - y_2^2 = 1$.
    - This is a hyperbola opening along the $y_1$ axis (direction $(4,3)$).
    - Semi-axis length $a=1, b=1$.

### Answer 7.3.6

**Equation:** $2xy + 2xz + 2yz = 1$.
**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$
This is $J - I$.
Eigenvalues of $J$ are $3, 0, 0$.
Eigenvalues of $A$ are $3-1=2$ and $0-1=-1$ (multiplicity 2).

**Standard Form:**
$2y_1^2 - 1y_2^2 - 1y_3^2 = 1$.

**Description:**
A **hyperboloid of two sheets**? Let's check signs. (+, -, -).
Plane sections $y_1 = c$ (for $|c|$ large) are circles ($y_2^2 + y_3^2 = R^2$).
Axis of symmetry is the eigenvector for $\lambda=2$, which is $(1,1,1)^T$.
The surface consists of two separate sheets opening along the axis $x=y=z$.

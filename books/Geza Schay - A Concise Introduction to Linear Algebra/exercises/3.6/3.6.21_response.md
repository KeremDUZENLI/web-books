**Basis Matrix $A$:**
$$ P_A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 4 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$
We want coordinates relative to $A$, so we solve $P_A [\mathbf{x}]_A = \mathbf{x}$.
$[\mathbf{x}]_A = P_A^{-1} \mathbf{x}$.
Find $P_A^{-1}$:
Note block structure or simple rows.
Row 4 implies $-x_2 = y_4 \implies x_2 = -y_4$.
Row 3 implies $2x_3 = y_3 \implies x_3 = 0.5 y_3$.
Row 1: $x_1 + x_3 = y_1 \implies x_1 = y_1 - 0.5 y_3$.
Row 2: $3x_1 + 4x_2 + x_4 = y_2 \implies x_4 = y_2 - 3x_1 - 4x_2 = y_2 - 3(y_1 - 0.5y_3) - 4(-y_4)$.
Inverse:
$$ P_A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -0.5 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.5 & 0 \\ -3 & 1 & 1.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 3.6.9

**Matrices:** $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 4 \\ 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$, $B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 0 & -2 \\ 7 & 11 \end{bmatrix}$.
These matrices represent bases for a 2D subspace in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
We solve $A S = B$.
Augmented $[A|B]$:
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{cc|cc} 1 & 0 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & 0 & -2 \\ 3 & -1 & 7 & 11 \end{array} \right] $$
1.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1$: $(0, 4 \mid -4, -8)$.
2.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1$: $(0, -1 \mid 1, 2)$.
3.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2/4$: $(0, 1 \mid -1, -2)$.
4.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_2$: $(0, 0 \mid 0, 0)$. System is consistent.
    $[I \mid S] \implies S = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ -1 & -2 \end{bmatrix}$.

**a. Transition $A \to B$:**
$$ S_{A \to B} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ -1 & -2 \end{bmatrix} $$

**b. Transition $B \to A$:**
$$ S_{B \to A} = S^{-1} = \frac{1}{-4 - (-3)} \begin{bmatrix} -2 & -3 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ -1 & -2 \end{bmatrix} $$
(It is its own inverse).

### Answer 3.6.7

**Basis $A$:** $\mathbf{a}_1 = \mathbf{e}_3, \mathbf{a}_2 = \mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{a}_3 = \mathbf{e}_2$.
$$ P_A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

**a. Transition Standard $\to A$:**
$S = P_A^{-1} = P_A^T$ (Permutation matrix is orthogonal).
$$ S = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

**b. Components of $\mathbf{x}=(3,4,5)^T$:**
$$ [\mathbf{x}]_A = S \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

**c. Representation $M_A$:**
$M = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
$M_A = P_A^{-1} M P_A = S M S^T$.
$M S^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
$S (M S^T) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
(Essentially permutes rows and columns: 1->2, 2->3, 3->1).

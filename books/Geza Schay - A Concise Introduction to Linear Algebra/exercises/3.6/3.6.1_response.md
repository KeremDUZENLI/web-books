### Answer 3.6.1

**Basis $A$:** $\mathbf{a}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{a}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.

**a. Transition Matrix $S$ (Standard $\to A$):**
Let $P_A = [\mathbf{a}_1 \ \mathbf{a}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
The relationship between coordinates is $\mathbf{x}_{std} = P_A \mathbf{x}_A$.
To find $\mathbf{x}_A$ from $\mathbf{x}_{std}$, we need $\mathbf{x}_A = P_A^{-1} \mathbf{x}_{std}$.
Thus, the transition matrix $S$ from the standard basis to $A$ is $P_A^{-1}$.
Determinant of $P_A$: $1(1) - (-2)(2) = 5$.
$$ S = P_A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2 & 0.4 \\ -0.4 & 0.2 \end{bmatrix} $$

**b. Components of $\mathbf{x}=(3,5)^T$ in basis $A$:**
$$ [\mathbf{x}]_A = S \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2 & 0.4 \\ -0.4 & 0.2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} $$
$$ [\mathbf{x}]_A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 + 2.0 \\ -1.2 + 1.0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2.6 \\ -0.2 \end{bmatrix} $$
Check: $2.6(1,2) - 0.2(-2,1) = (2.6+0.4, 5.2-0.2) = (3, 5)$. Correct.

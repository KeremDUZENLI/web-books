# Chapter 13: Frontiers

---

## Main Idea

- This concluding chapter surveys advanced topics that could influence the future of VR, specifically addressing the "forgotten senses" (touch, smell, and taste) which are currently less developed than vision and hearing.

- It explores the potential of robotic interfaces for telepresence (acting as physical avatars) and the ultimate goal of brain-machine interfaces that facilitate direct neural interaction.

---

## Key Principles & Guidelines

**The Somatosensory System**

- **Definition:** The system responsible for the sense of touch, proprioception, temperature, and pain, mediated by receptors in the skin (epidermis, dermis) and body rather than a single concentrated organ.

- **Design Implication:** Unlike vision and hearing, which utilize organs concentrated on the head, the somatosensory system is distributed across the entire body, making it significantly harder to "co-opt" or stimulate effectively with current VR hardware.

**Chemical Senses**

- **Definition:** The senses of smell (olfaction) and taste (gustation), which rely on the detection of chemical compounds.

- **Design Implication:** Developing "displays" for these senses requires engineering methods to generate and deliver chemicals, a distinct challenge from the wave-based physics of light and sound.

**Telepresence**

- **Definition:** The use of robots as surrogates or "physical avatars" in the real world, controlled through a VR interface.

- **Design Implication:** This technology extends VR beyond virtual worlds, allowing users to explore real-world environments remotely while maintaining a sense of presence.

**Brain-Machine Interfaces (BMI)**

- **Definition:** Systems that enable direct communication between the human brain and external machines, potentially bypassing sensory organs (e.g., retinal implants) or controlling devices via neural signals.

- **Design Implication:** This represents the ultimate level of human augmentation, theoretically leading to scenarios like the "brain in a vat" where reality is simulated directly at the neural level.

---

## Practical Use

**Haptic Interfaces**

- Engineering technologies designed to stimulate the somatosensory system, providing tactile and force feedback to simulate the weight, texture, and resistance of virtual objects.

**Physical Avatars**

- Robots that serve the same function in the real world as digital avatars do in the virtual world, enabling remote interaction and exploration.

- **Given:** $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{Q}\mathbf{D}\mathbf{Q}^T$ (where $\mathbf{Q}$ is orthogonal).

- **Proof:** Multiply $\mathbf{A}$ by the $i$-th column of $\mathbf{Q}$ (call it $\mathbf{v}$).

  $$
  \mathbf{A}\mathbf{v} = (\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{D}\mathbf{Q}^T) \mathbf{v}
  $$

  Since $\mathbf{Q}$ is orthogonal, $\mathbf{Q}^T \mathbf{v}$ results in the standard basis vector $\mathbf{e}_i$ (0 everywhere, 1 at index $i$).
  $\mathbf{D} \mathbf{e}_i$ picks out the diagonal value $\lambda_i$.
  $\mathbf{Q} (\lambda_i \mathbf{e}_i) = \lambda_i \mathbf{v}$.

- **Result:** $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{v} = \lambda_i \mathbf{v}$, so $\mathbf{v}$ is an eigenvector.

- **Goal:** Compute the average distance from the center ($r$) on a unit disk.

- **Measure:** We must integrate over Area ($dA$), not just the radius. In polar coordinates, $dA = r \, dr \, d\phi$.

- **Normalization:** The area of the unit disk is $\pi R^2 = \pi(1)^2 = \pi$.

- **Integral:**

  $$
  \text{Avg} = \frac{1}{\text{Area}} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^1 r \cdot (r \, dr \, d\phi) = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} d\phi \int_0^1 r^2 \, dr
  $$

- **Calculation:**

  $$
  \frac{1}{\pi} (2\pi) \left[ \frac{r^3}{3} \right]_0^1 = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3}
  $$

- **Result:** $2/3$. (Note: This is larger than $1/2$ because there is more area in the outer rings of a disk than near the center).

- **Concept:** An affine matrix has the form $\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{L} & \mathbf{t} \\ \mathbf{0} & 1 \end{bmatrix}$, where $\mathbf{L}$ is any $3 \times 3$ linear transformation (scale, shear, rotation).

- **Inversion:** We need a matrix $\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{A} & \mathbf{b} \\ \mathbf{c} & d \end{bmatrix}$ such that the product is Identity.

  - The bottom row $\mathbf{0} \cdot \mathbf{A} + 1 \cdot \mathbf{c}$ must equal $\mathbf{0}$, so $\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{0}$.

  - $\mathbf{0} \cdot \mathbf{b} + 1 \cdot d$ must equal $1$, so $d = 1$.

- **Conclusion:** The inverse also has $\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{0} & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ as the bottom row, preserving the affine structure.

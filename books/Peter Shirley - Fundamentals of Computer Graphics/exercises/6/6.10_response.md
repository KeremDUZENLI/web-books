- **Goal:** Find matrix $\mathbf{M}$ such that $\mathbf{M}\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{a}$, $\mathbf{M}\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{b}$, $\mathbf{M}\mathbf{w}=\mathbf{c}$.

- **Concept:** We can view this as a change of basis. We want to map the frame $[\mathbf{u} \mathbf{v} \mathbf{w}]$ to $[\mathbf{a} \mathbf{b} \mathbf{c}]$.

- **Formula:**

  $$
  \mathbf{M} = \mathbf{B}_{dest} \mathbf{B}_{source}^{-1} =
  \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{a} & \mathbf{b} & \mathbf{c} & \mathbf{0} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}
  \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u} & \mathbf{v} & \mathbf{w} & \mathbf{0} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}^T
  $$

- **Simplified:** Since $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}$ are orthonormal, their inverse is their transpose.

  $$
  \mathbf{M} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  | & | & | & 0 \\
  \mathbf{a} & \mathbf{b} & \mathbf{c} & 0 \\
  | & | & | & 0 \\
  0 & 0 & 0 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  \begin{bmatrix}
  - & \mathbf{u} & - & 0 \\
  - & \mathbf{v} & - & 0 \\
  - & \mathbf{w} & - & 0 \\
  0 & 0 & 0 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  $$

- **Goal:** Show that multiplying the viewport and orthographic matrices ($M_{vp} M_{orth}$) creates the general windowing matrix.

- **Explanation:**

  1. **$M_{orth}$:** Maps the volume $[l, r] \times [b, t] \times [n, f]$ to the canonical cube $[-1, 1]^3$.

  2. **$M_{vp}$:** Maps the canonical cube to screen coordinates $[0, n_x] \times [0, n_y]$.

  3. **The Product:** Multiplying them creates a single matrix that linearly maps the source bounds directly to the pixel bounds.

- **Resulting Matrix:**

  $$
  \mathbf{M}_{window} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \frac{n_x}{r-l} & 0 & 0 & -\frac{n_x l}{r-l} \\\\
  0 & \frac{n_y}{t-b} & 0 & -\frac{n_y b}{t-b} \\\\
  0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\\\
  0 & 0 & 0 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  $$

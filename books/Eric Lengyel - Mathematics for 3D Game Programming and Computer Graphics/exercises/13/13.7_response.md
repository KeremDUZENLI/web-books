### Answer 13.7

**1. System Analysis**
Mass $M$ hangs (accelerates down). Mass $m$ slides up the incline.
Common acceleration $a$.
Forces on $M$: Gravity $Mg$ (down), Tension $T$ (up).
$Ma = Mg - T \implies T = M(g-a)$.

Forces on $m$:
Gravity parallel: $mg \sin \theta$ (down incline).
Friction: $\mu_K N$ (opposing motion, down incline).
Normal Force $N = mg \cos \theta$.
Tension $T$ (up incline).
$ma = T - mg \sin \theta - \mu_K (mg \cos \theta)$.

**2. Combine Equations**
Substitute $T = M(g-a)$ into $m$'s equation:
$ma = [M(g-a)] - mg \sin \theta - \mu_K mg \cos \theta$
$ma = Mg - Ma - mg \sin \theta - \mu_K mg \cos \theta$
$ma + Ma = Mg - mg \sin \theta - \mu_K mg \cos \theta$
$a(M + m) = g [ M - m(\sin \theta + \mu_K \cos \theta) ]$

**3. Solve for $a$**
$$a = g \frac{M - m(\sin \theta + \mu_K \cos \theta)}{M + m}$$

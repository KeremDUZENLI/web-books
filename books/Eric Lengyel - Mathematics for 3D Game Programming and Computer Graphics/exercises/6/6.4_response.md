### Answer 6.4

**1. Area Definition:**
The area of a triangle with vertices $A, B, C$ is $\frac{1}{2} ||(B-A) \times (C-A)||$.
For triangle T ($P_0, P_1, P_2$), Area $A = \frac{1}{2} ||(P_1 - P_0) \times (P_2 - P_0)||$.

**2. Subtriangle Areas:**
Consider $w_2$ (associated with $P_2$). It corresponds to subtriangle $U_2$ formed by $P, P_0, P_1$.
Area $A_2 = \frac{1}{2} ||(P_1 - P_0) \times (P - P_0)||$.

**3. Substitute P:**
$P = w_0 P_0 + w_1 P_1 + w_2 P_2$.
$P - P_0 = w_1(P_1 - P_0) + w_2(P_2 - P_0)$ (since $w_0 = 1 - w_1 - w_2$).

**4. Compute Cross Product for $A_2$:**
$$(P_1 - P_0) \times (P - P_0) = (P_1 - P_0) \times [w_1(P_1 - P_0) + w_2(P_2 - P_0)]$$
$$= w_1 [(P_1 - P_0) \times (P_1 - P_0)] + w_2 [(P_1 - P_0) \times (P_2 - P_0)]$$
The term $(P_1 - P_0) \times (P_1 - P_0)$ is zero.
$$= w_2 [(P_1 - P_0) \times (P_2 - P_0)]$$

**5. Ratio:**
$$A_2 = \frac{1}{2} ||w_2 ((P_1 - P_0) \times (P_2 - P_0))|| = w_2 \cdot (\text{Area of T})$$
$$w_2 = \frac{\text{Area}(U_2)}{\text{Area}(T)}$$
(The same logic applies to $w_0$ and $w_1$).

### Answer 6.6

Function: $f(x, y, z) = 2x^2 + 3y^2 - z$.
Point: $P = (-1, 2, 14)$.

**1. Calculate Gradient $\nabla f$:**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 4x$$
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 6y$$
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial z} = -1$$
$$\nabla f = \langle 4x, 6y, -1 \rangle$$

**2. Evaluate at P:**
$$x = -1 \implies 4(-1) = -4$$
$$y = 2 \implies 6(2) = 12$$
$$z = 14 \implies -1$$
$$N_{unnormalized} = \langle -4, 12, -1 \rangle$$

**3. Normalize:**
$$||N|| = \sqrt{(-4)^2 + 12^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 144 + 1} = \sqrt{161}$$
$$N = \langle \frac{-4}{\sqrt{161}}, \frac{12}{\sqrt{161}}, \frac{-1}{\sqrt{161}} \rangle$$

### Answer 5.3

Let $\theta$ be the angle between the line direction vector $V$ and the vector $(Q - S)$.

**1. Geometric interpretation of cross product magnitude:**
$$||(Q - S) \times V|| = ||Q - S|| \, ||V|| \sin \theta$$

**2. Geometric interpretation of distance:**
The distance $d$ is the length of the orthogonal projection of $(Q - S)$ onto the plane perpendicular to $V$. In the triangle formed by $S$, $Q$, and the projection of $Q$ onto the line, $d$ is the opposite side to angle $\theta$.
$$d = ||Q - S|| \sin \theta$$

**3. Substitute from (1):**
From step 1, we have:
$$||Q - S|| \sin \theta = \frac{||(Q - S) \times V||}{||V||}$$

**4. Result:**
$$d = \frac{||(Q - S) \times V||}{||V||}$$

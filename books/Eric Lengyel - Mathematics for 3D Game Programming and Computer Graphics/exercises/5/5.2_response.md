### Answer 5.2

We want to find $t$ such that the distance between $P(t)$ and $Q$ is minimized. This occurs when the vector from $P(t)$ to $Q$ is perpendicular to the line direction $V$.

**1. Vector from Line to Point:**
$$W = Q - P(t) = Q - (S + tV) = (Q - S) - tV$$

**2. Orthogonality Condition:**
$$W \cdot V = 0$$
$$((Q - S) - tV) \cdot V = 0$$
$$(Q - S) \cdot V - t(V \cdot V) = 0$$

**3. Solve for t:**
$$t(V \cdot V) = (Q - S) \cdot V$$
$$t = \frac{(Q - S) \cdot V}{V \cdot V} = \frac{(Q - S) \cdot V}{||V||^2}$$

### Answer 2.4

We use the definitions involving the angle $\alpha$ between vectors $V$ and $W$.

**1. Dot Product Definition:**
[cite_start]$$(V \cdot W)^2 = (||V|| ||W|| \cos \alpha)^2 = ||V||^2 ||W||^2 \cos^2 \alpha$$ [cite: 340]

**2. Cross Product Magnitude Definition:**
[cite_start]$$||V \times W||^2 = (||V|| ||W|| \sin \alpha)^2 = ||V||^2 ||W||^2 \sin^2 \alpha$$ [cite: 462]

**3. Sum:**
$$(V \cdot W)^2 + ||V \times W||^2 = ||V||^2 ||W||^2 \cos^2 \alpha + ||V||^2 ||W||^2 \sin^2 \alpha$$
$$= ||V||^2 ||W||^2 (\cos^2 \alpha + \sin^2 \alpha)$$
Since $\cos^2 \alpha + \sin^2 \alpha = 1$:
$$= ||V||^2 ||W||^2$$
$$= V^2 W^2$$
(Note: $V^2$ denotes $P \cdot P$ or $||P||^2$) [cite_start][cite: 391, 654].

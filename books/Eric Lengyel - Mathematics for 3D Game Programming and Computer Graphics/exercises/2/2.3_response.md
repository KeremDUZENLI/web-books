### Answer 2.3

Let vertices be $V_1 = (1, 2, 3)$, $V_2 = (-2, 2, 4)$, $V_3 = (7, -8, 6)$.
[cite_start]Area $A = \frac{1}{2} ||(V_2 - V_1) \times (V_3 - V_1)||$[cite: 482].

**1. Compute edge vectors:**
$$P = V_2 - V_1 = \langle -2-1, 2-2, 4-3 \rangle = \langle -3, 0, 1 \rangle$$
$$Q = V_3 - V_1 = \langle 7-1, -8-2, 6-3 \rangle = \langle 6, -10, 3 \rangle$$

**2. Compute Cross Product $P \times Q$:**
$$P \times Q = \begin{vmatrix} i & j & k \\ -3 & 0 & 1 \\ 6 & -10 & 3 \end{vmatrix}$$
$$x = (0)(3) - (1)(-10) = 10$$
$$y = (1)(6) - (-3)(3) = 6 + 9 = 15$$
$$z = (-3)(-10) - (0)(6) = 30$$
$$P \times Q = \langle 10, 15, 30 \rangle$$

**3. Compute Magnitude:**
$$||P \times Q|| = \sqrt{10^2 + 15^2 + 30^2} = \sqrt{100 + 225 + 900} = \sqrt{1225}$$
$$\sqrt{1225} = 35$$

**4. Compute Area:**
$$A = \frac{1}{2} (35) = 17.5$$

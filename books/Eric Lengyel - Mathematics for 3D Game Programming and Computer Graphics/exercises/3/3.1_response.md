### Answer 3.1

**(a)**
Formula for $2 \times 2$: $ad - bc$.
$$det = (2)(\frac{1}{2}) - (7)(-3)$$
$$det = 1 - (-21) = 22$$

**(b)**
Expand along the first row.
$$det = 0 - 0 + 1 \begin{vmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{vmatrix}$$
$$det = 1(0 - 1) = -1$$

**(c)**
Expand along the third row (containing zeros).
$$det = 1 \begin{vmatrix} \frac{1}{2} & \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2} \\ -\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2} & \frac{1}{2} \end{vmatrix}$$
$$det = (\frac{1}{2})(\frac{1}{2}) - (\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2})(-\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2})$$
$$det = \frac{1}{4} - (-\frac{5}{4}) = \frac{6}{4} = \frac{3}{2}$$

**(d)**
Observe that Row 3 is exactly $2 \times$ Row 1.
$$R_3 = \langle 10, 14, 2 \rangle = 2 \langle 5, 7, 1 \rangle = 2 R_1$$
Since the rows are linearly dependent, the determinant is zero.
$$det = 0$$

### Answer 3.2

**(a)**
The inverse of a diagonal matrix consists of the reciprocals of the diagonal entries.
$$A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{3} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{4} \end{bmatrix}$$

**(b)**
Use Gauss-Jordan elimination on $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 2 & | & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 0 & 8 & | & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
1. $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 2 & | & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 8 & | & -3 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$
2. $R_2 \leftarrow \frac{1}{2}R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 0 & \frac{1}{2} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 8 & | & -3 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$
3. $R_3 \leftarrow \frac{1}{8}R_3$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 0 & \frac{1}{2} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -\frac{3}{8} & 0 & \frac{1}{8} \end{bmatrix}$
4. $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_3$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & \frac{3}{8} & \frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{8} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -\frac{3}{8} & 0 & \frac{1}{8} \end{bmatrix}$

$$A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{3}{8} & \frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{8} \\ -\frac{3}{8} & 0 & \frac{1}{8} \end{bmatrix}$$

**(c)**
This is a rotation matrix about the Y-axis. Orthogonal matrices have inverses equal to their transpose.
$$A^{-1} = A^T = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & 0 & \sin \theta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin \theta & 0 & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix}$$

**(d)**
Using cofactor expansion or Gauss-Jordan. Determinant $det = 2(2(2) - 3(3)) = 2(4-9) = -10$.
Matrix of cofactors $C$:
$C_{11} = -5, C_{12} = -22, C_{13} = 23$
$C_{21} = 0, C_{22} = 4, C_{23} = -6$
$C_{31} = 0, C_{32} = -6, C_{33} = 4$
Adjugate is $C^T$:
$$A^{-1} = -\frac{1}{10} \begin{bmatrix} -5 & 0 & 0 \\ -22 & 4 & -6 \\ 23 & -6 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} & 0 & 0 \\ \frac{11}{5} & -\frac{2}{5} & \frac{3}{5} \\ -\frac{23}{10} & \frac{3}{5} & -\frac{2}{5} \end{bmatrix}$$

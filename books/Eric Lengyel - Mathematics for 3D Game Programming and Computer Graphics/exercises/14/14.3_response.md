### Answer 14.3

Density function: $\rho(z) = 1 + \frac{z}{h}$.
Volume element (cylindrical): $dV = r dr d\theta dz$.

**1. Calculate Total Mass (M):**
$$M = \int_V \rho(z) dV = \int_0^h \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^R (1 + \frac{z}{h}) r dr d\theta dz$$
Integrate $r$: $\int_0^R r dr = \frac{R^2}{2}$.
Integrate $\theta$: $\int_0^{2\pi} d\theta = 2\pi$.
Integrate $z$: $\int_0^h (1 + \frac{z}{h}) dz = [z + \frac{z^2}{2h}]_0^h = h + \frac{h}{2} = \frac{3h}{2}$.
$$M = (2\pi)(\frac{R^2}{2})(\frac{3h}{2}) = \frac{3}{2} \pi R^2 h$$

**2. Calculate Center of Mass ($C_z$):**
$$C_z = \frac{1}{M} \int_V z \rho(z) dV$$
Integral part: $\int_0^h \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^R z(1 + \frac{z}{h}) r dr d\theta dz$.
Integrate $z$: $\int_0^h (z + \frac{z^2}{h}) dz = [\frac{z^2}{2} + \frac{z^3}{3h}]_0^h = \frac{h^2}{2} + \frac{h^2}{3} = \frac{5h^2}{6}$.
Integral result: $(2\pi)(\frac{R^2}{2})(\frac{5h^2}{6}) = \frac{5}{6} \pi R^2 h^2$.

**3. Final Result:**
$$C_z = \frac{\frac{5}{6} \pi R^2 h^2}{\frac{3}{2} \pi R^2 h} = \frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{2}{3} h = \frac{5}{9} h$$
Due to symmetry, $C_x = 0$ and $C_y = 0$.
$$C = \langle 0, 0, \frac{5h}{9} \rangle$$

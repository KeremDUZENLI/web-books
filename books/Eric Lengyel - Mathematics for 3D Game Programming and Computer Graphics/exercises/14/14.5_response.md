### Answer 14.5

The capsule consists of a central elliptical cylinder (mass $m_{cyl}$) and two elliptical domes (mass $m_{dome}$ each).
Let the total density be $\rho = m / V_{total}$.

**1. Central Cylinder ($h$, semiaxes $a, b$):**
Mass $m_{cyl} = \rho \pi a b h$.
Inertia tensor about origin (Equation 14.72 generalized for ellipse):
$$I_{cyl} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{4}m_{cyl}b^2 + \frac{1}{12}m_{cyl}h^2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{4}m_{cyl}a^2 + \frac{1}{12}m_{cyl}h^2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{4}m_{cyl}(a^2+b^2) \end{bmatrix}$$

**2. Domes ($a, b, c$):**
Mass of one dome $m_{dome} = \frac{2}{3} \rho \pi a b c$.
Inertia tensor of a dome about its own center of mass (derived from Eq 14.105):
$$I_{dome}^{CM} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{5}m_{dome}b^2 + \frac{10}{320}m_{dome}c^2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{5}m_{dome}a^2 + \frac{10}{330}m_{dome}c^2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{5}m_{dome}(a^2+b^2) \end{bmatrix}$$
*Note: The constants $\frac{10}{320}$ etc. in source Eq 14.105 appear specific to the derivation. Using $I_{xx} = m(\frac{1}{5}b^2 + C c^2)$ form.*

**3. Parallel Axis Theorem:**
The domes are shifted by $s = \pm (h/2 + \frac{3}{8}c)$ along the z-axis.
Shift term $J = m_{dome}(s_z^2 E - s \otimes s) = m_{dome} \text{diag}(s_z^2, s_z^2, 0)$.
$s_z = \frac{h}{2} + \frac{3}{8}c$.

**4. Total Inertia:**
$$I_{total} = I_{cyl} + 2(I_{dome}^{CM} + J)$$
Sum components:
$$I_{xx} = I_{cyl,xx} + 2(I_{dome,xx}^{CM} + m_{dome}s_z^2)$$
$$I_{yy} = I_{cyl,yy} + 2(I_{dome,yy}^{CM} + m_{dome}s_z^2)$$
$$I_{zz} = I_{cyl,zz} + 2I_{dome,zz}^{CM}$$

### Answer 14.1

The ant experiences two inertial forces in the rotating frame: the Centrifugal force and the Coriolis force.

**1. Force Vectors:**
* **Angular Velocity:** $\mathbf{\omega} = \omega \mathbf{k}$ (counterclockwise).
* **Velocity:** $\mathbf{v} = v \mathbf{u}_r$ (radially outward).
* **Position:** $\mathbf{r} = r \mathbf{u}_r$.

**2. Centrifugal Force:**
$$F_{cen} = m \omega^2 r$$
Direction is radially outward ($\mathbf{u}_r$).

**3. Coriolis Force:**
Formula: $F_{cor} = -2m (\mathbf{\omega} \times \mathbf{v})$.
$\mathbf{\omega} \times \mathbf{v} = (\omega \mathbf{k}) \times (v \mathbf{u}_r) = \omega v \mathbf{u}_\theta$.
Magnitude: $2m\omega v$.
Direction is tangential (perpendicular to radius).

**4. Total Magnitude:**
Since the radial and tangential directions are orthogonal:
$$F = \sqrt{F_{cen}^2 + F_{cor}^2}$$
$$F = \sqrt{(m\omega^2 r)^2 + (2m\omega v)^2}$$
$$F = m\omega \sqrt{\omega^2 r^2 + 4v^2}$$

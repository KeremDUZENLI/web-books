### Answer 14.4

We compute the moment of inertia about the z axis: $I_{zz} = \int_V r^2 \rho dV$.
Volume of annulus: $V = \pi h (R_2^2 - R_1^2)$.
Density $\rho = \frac{m}{V} = \frac{m}{\pi h (R_2^2 - R_1^2)}$.

**1. Setup Integral:**
$$I_{zz} = \rho \int_0^h \int_0^{2\pi} \int_{R_1}^{R_2} r^2 (r dr d\theta dz)$$
$$I_{zz} = \rho \int_0^h dz \int_0^{2\pi} d\theta \int_{R_1}^{R_2} r^3 dr$$

**2. Evaluate:**
$$\int_{R_1}^{R_2} r^3 dr = [\frac{r^4}{4}]_{R_1}^{R_2} = \frac{1}{4}(R_2^4 - R_1^4)$$
$$I_{zz} = \rho (h)(2\pi) \frac{1}{4}(R_2^4 - R_1^4) = \frac{1}{2} \pi h \rho (R_2^2 - R_1^2)(R_2^2 + R_1^2)$$

**3. Substitute Mass:**
$$I_{zz} = \frac{1}{2} \pi h \left( \frac{m}{\pi h (R_2^2 - R_1^2)} \right) (R_2^2 - R_1^2)(R_2^2 + R_1^2)$$
$$I_{zz} = \frac{1}{2} m (R_1^2 + R_2^2)$$

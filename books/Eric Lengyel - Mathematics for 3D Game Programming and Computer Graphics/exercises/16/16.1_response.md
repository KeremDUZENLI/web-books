### Answer 16.1

To extend the Jacobi method from $3 \times 3$ to $n \times n$, we must iterate through every unique off-diagonal pair $(p, q)$ where $p < q$ and apply the rotation that annihilates $M_{pq}$.

**Algorithm Modification:**

1.  **Initialize:** Set $R$ to the identity matrix.
2.  **Sweeps:** Perform a fixed number of sweeps (e.g., 32) or iterate until off-diagonal magnitude is negligible.
3.  **Iteration:**
    Loop `p` from `0` to `n-2`:
        Loop `q` from `p+1` to `n-1`:
            If $|M_{pq}| > \epsilon$:
                a. Calculate $u = \frac{M_{pp} - M_{qq}}{2M_{pq}}$.
                b. Calculate $t = \text{sgn}(u)(\sqrt{u^2+1} - |u|)$. (Use special case if $u \to \infty$).
                c. Calculate $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{t^2+1}}$ and $s = ct$.
                d. Update diagonal entries:
                   $M_{pp}' = M_{pp} - tM_{pq}$
                   $M_{qq}' = M_{qq} + tM_{pq}$
                   $M_{pq}' = 0$
                e. Update off-diagonal entries $M_{ip}, M_{iq}$ for $i \neq p, q$:
                   $M_{ip}' = cM_{ip} - sM_{iq}$
                   $M_{iq}' = sM_{ip} + cM_{iq}$
                f. Accumulate eigenvectors in $R$ (rows $p$ and $q$).

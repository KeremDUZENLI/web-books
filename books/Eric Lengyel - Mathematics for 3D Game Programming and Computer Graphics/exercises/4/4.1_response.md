### Answer 4.1

We need matrices for $\theta = 30^\circ$.
$\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$
$\sin(30^\circ) = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5$

**1. Rotation about X-axis ($R_x$)**
$$R_x(30^\circ) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos 30^\circ & -\sin 30^\circ \\ 0 & \sin 30^\circ & \cos 30^\circ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{bmatrix}$$

**2. Rotation about Y-axis ($R_y$)**
$$R_y(30^\circ) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos 30^\circ & 0 & \sin 30^\circ \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin 30^\circ & 0 & \cos 30^\circ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & 0 & \frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & 0 & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{bmatrix}$$

**3. Rotation about Z-axis ($R_z$)**
$$R_z(30^\circ) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos 30^\circ & -\sin 30^\circ & 0 \\ \sin 30^\circ & \cos 30^\circ & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} & 0 \\ \frac{1}{2} & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

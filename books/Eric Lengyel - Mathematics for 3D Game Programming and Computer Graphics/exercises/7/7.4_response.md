### Answer 7.4

We define the halfway vector $H = \frac{L+V}{||L+V||}$.
We must show $(N \cdot H)^m = [\frac{(N \cdot L + N \cdot V)^2}{2(L \cdot V + 1)}]^{m/2}$.

**1. Expand the dot product $N \cdot H$:**
$$N \cdot H = N \cdot \frac{L+V}{||L+V||} = \frac{N \cdot L + N \cdot V}{||L+V||}$$

**2. Calculate the magnitude squared $||L+V||^2$:**
$$||L+V||^2 = (L+V) \cdot (L+V) = L \cdot L + 2(L \cdot V) + V \cdot V$$
Since $L$ and $V$ are unit vectors, $L \cdot L = 1$ and $V \cdot V = 1$.
$$||L+V||^2 = 1 + 2(L \cdot V) + 1 = 2 + 2(L \cdot V) = 2(1 + L \cdot V)$$

**3. Substitute magnitude back into the expression for $(N \cdot H)$:**
$$N \cdot H = \frac{N \cdot L + N \cdot V}{\sqrt{2(1 + L \cdot V)}}$$

**4. Raise to the power $m$:**
$$(N \cdot H)^m = \left( \frac{N \cdot L + N \cdot V}{\sqrt{2(1 + L \cdot V)}} \right)^m$$
$$(N \cdot H)^m = \frac{(N \cdot L + N \cdot V)^m}{(2(1 + L \cdot V))^{m/2}}$$

**5. Rearrange to match the target form:**
$$(N \cdot H)^m = \left[ \frac{(N \cdot L + N \cdot V)^2}{2(1 + L \cdot V)} \right]^{m/2}$$
This matches the required expression.

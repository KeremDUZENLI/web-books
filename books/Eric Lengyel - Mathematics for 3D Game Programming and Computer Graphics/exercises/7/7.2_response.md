### Answer 7.2

Given:
Spot light at $P=(0,0,10)$, direction $R=(0,0,-1)$.
Attenuation constants $k_c=1, k_l=0, k_q=0$. (No distance attenuation).
Spot exponent $p=8$.
Color $C_0 = 1$ (white). Target intensity $C = 0.5$ (50% gray).

**1. Spot Light Formula:**
Since distance attenuation is 1, the formula simplifies to:
$$C = (\max\{-R \cdot L, 0\})^p C_0$$
$$0.5 = (-R \cdot L)^8 (1)$$
$$(-R \cdot L)^8 = 0.5$$

**2. Geometry:**
Point $Q$ lies on the x-y plane, so $Q=(x,y,0)$.
Vector to light $L_{vec} = P - Q = (0,0,10) - (x,y,0) = (-x, -y, 10)$.
Distance $D = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + 10^2}$. Let $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$.
Normalized light vector $L = \frac{(-x, -y, 10)}{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}}$.
Spot direction $R = (0,0,-1) \implies -R = (0,0,1)$.

**3. Dot Product:**
$$-R \cdot L = \langle 0,0,1 \rangle \cdot \frac{\langle -x, -y, 10 \rangle}{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}} = \frac{10}{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}}$$

**4. Solve for radius $r$:**
$$\left( \frac{10}{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}} \right)^8 = 0.5$$
$$\frac{10}{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}} = (0.5)^{1/8}$$
$$\frac{\sqrt{r^2 + 100}}{10} = (0.5)^{-1/8} = 2^{1/8}$$
$$\sqrt{r^2 + 100} = 10 \cdot 2^{1/8}$$
$$r^2 + 100 = 100 \cdot 2^{1/4}$$
$$r^2 = 100 (2^{0.25} - 1)$$
$$r = 10 \sqrt{2^{0.25} - 1}$$
$$r \approx 10 \sqrt{1.1892 - 1} \approx 10 \sqrt{0.1892} \approx 4.35 \text{ meters}$$

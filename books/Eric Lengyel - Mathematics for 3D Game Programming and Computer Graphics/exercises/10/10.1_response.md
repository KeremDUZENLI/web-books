### Answer 10.1

We seek to modify the projection matrix $M'_{infinite}$ such that the resulting normalized depth $d'$ is offset by $\delta$ relative to the original depth $d$.
$$d' = d + \delta$$

**1. Analyze the Depth Function**
The normalized depth $d$ produced by a projection matrix is given by the perspective division of the clip-space coordinates $P'_z$ and $P'_w$:
$$d = \frac{P'_z}{P'_w}$$
For the standard infinite projection (assuming eye-space $w_{eye}=1$), $P'_w = -P_{z_{eye}}$.
Let $M_{33}$ (0-indexed $M_{22}$) be the (3,3) entry and $M_{34}$ be the (3,4) entry.
$$P'_z = M_{33} P_z + M_{34}$$
$$d = \frac{M_{33} P_z + M_{34}}{-P_z} = -M_{33} - \frac{M_{34}}{P_z}$$

**2. Apply the Offset**
We require the new depth $d'$ to satisfy:
$$d' = d + \delta$$
$$-M'_{33} - \frac{M'_{34}}{P_z} = \left( -M_{33} - \frac{M_{34}}{P_z} \right) + \delta$$

**3. Solve for the New Entry**
To satisfy this for all $P_z$, we equate the constant terms:
$$-M'_{33} = -M_{33} + \delta$$
$$M'_{33} = M_{33} - \delta$$

**4. Substitute $M_{infinite}^{\prime}$ Value**
From Equation (10.10), the (3,3) entry is $M_{33} = \epsilon - 1$.
$$M'_{33} = (\epsilon - 1) - \delta$$
$$M'_{33} = \epsilon - 1 - \delta$$

(Note: Depending on the specific definition of "offset" in Section 9.1—e.g., if it implies $d' = d - \delta$—the sign of $\delta$ would be reversed).

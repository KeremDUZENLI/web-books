- **Proof:** If a symmetric matrix $\mathbf{A}$ has distinct eigenvalues, its eigenvectors are orthogonal.

  $$
  \lambda_1 (\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y}) = (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}) \cdot \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{x} \cdot (\mathbf{A}^T \mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{x} \cdot (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{x} \cdot (\lambda_2 \mathbf{y}) = \lambda_2 (\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y})
  $$

  $(\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)(\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y}) = 0$.
  Since $\lambda_1 \neq \lambda_2$, the dot product $\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y}$ must be zero.

- **Goal:** Create a viewport matrix where pixel $(0,0)$ is at the top-left (common in image processing) rather than bottom-left (standard OpenGL).

- **Standard $y$ mapping:** Maps $y \in [-1, 1]$ to $[0, n_y]$.

- **Inverted $y$ mapping:** We want $y = +1$ (top) to map to pixel $0$, and $y = -1$ (bottom) to map to pixel $n_y$.

  - This requires flipping the sign of the scale factor: scale by $-\frac{n_y}{2}$.

  - The translation remains the center of the image: $\frac{n_y - 1}{2}$.

- **Matrix:**

  $$
  \mathbf{M}_{vp}' =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \frac{n_x}{2} & 0 & 0 & \frac{n_x - 1}{2} \\
  0 & -\frac{n_y}{2} & 0 & \frac{n_y - 1}{2} \\
  0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
  0 & 0 & 0 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  $$

- **$|AB| = |A||B|$:** Geometrically, the determinant measures volume change. Applying transformation $\mathbf{A}$ then $\mathbf{B}$ scales volume by factor $|A|$ then $|B|$. The total scale is the product.

- **$|A^{-1}| = 1/|A|$:** Since $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{A}^{-1} = \mathbf{I}$ and $|\mathbf{I}| = 1$, using the product rule gives $|A| |A^{-1}| = 1$, so $|A^{-1}| = 1/|A|$.

- **$|A^T| = |A|$:** Transposing a matrix swaps rows and columns (changing the axes of the parallelepiped) but does not change the volume enclosed .

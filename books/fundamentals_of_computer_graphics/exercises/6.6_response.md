- **Given:** A matrix $\mathbf{M} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{R} & \mathbf{t} \\ \mathbf{0} & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ representing rotation $\mathbf{R}$ followed by translation $\mathbf{t}$.

- **Inverse Logic:** To undo the operation, we must **translate back** first, then **rotate back**.

  - Inverse Translation: $-\mathbf{t}$

  - Inverse Rotation: $\mathbf{R}^T$ (since rotation matrices are orthogonal, inverse = transpose).

- **Formula:**

  $$
  \mathbf{M}^{-1} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \mathbf{R}^T & -\mathbf{R}^T \mathbf{t} \\
  \mathbf{0} & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  $$

- **Explanation:** Multiplying $\mathbf{M}^{-1}\mathbf{M}$ results in the identity matrix .

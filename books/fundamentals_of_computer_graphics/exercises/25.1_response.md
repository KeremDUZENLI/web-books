- **Given:** A BRDF $\rho(\theta_i) = C \cos^a \theta_i$.

- **Goal:** Find $C$ such that energy is conserved (total reflectance $\le 1$).

- **Reflectance Integral:** The directional hemispherical reflectance $R(\theta_i)$ is the integral of the BRDF times the cosine of the outgoing angle over the hemisphere.

  $$
  R(\theta_i) = \int_{\Omega} \rho \cos \theta_o \, d\omega_o = \int_{\Omega} (C \cos^a \theta_i) \cos \theta_o \, d\omega_o
  $$

- **Integration:**

  Since the BRDF terms depend only on $\theta_i$, they move out of the integral:

  $$
  R(\theta_i) = C \cos^a \theta_i \int_{\Omega} \cos \theta_o \, d\omega_o = C \cos^a \theta_i (\pi)
  $$

- **Normalization:** To conserve energy for all angles, the maximum reflectance (at $\theta_i = 0$) must be 1.

  $$
  C \pi (1)^a = 1 \implies C = \frac{1}{\pi}
  $$

- **Result:** $C = 1/\pi$

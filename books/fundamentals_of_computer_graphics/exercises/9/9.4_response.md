- **Incorrect Definition:** $(a \star b)[i] = \sum_{j} a[j]b[i + j]$ (This is actually **correlation**, not convolution).

- **Test for Commutativity:** Does $a \star b = b \star a$?

- **Counterexample:**

  Let vector $a = [1, 2]$ (where $a[0]=1, a[1]=2$) and $b = [0, 3]$ (where $b[0]=0, b[1]=3$).

  **Case 1 ($a \star b$ using incorrect formula at index 0):**

  $$
  (a \star b)[0] = a[0]b[0] + a[1]b[1] = (1)(0) + (2)(3) = 6
  $$

  **Case 2 ($b \star a$ using incorrect formula at index 0):**

  $$
  (b \star a)[0] = b[0]a[0] + b[1]a[1] = (0)(1) + (3)(2) = 6
  $$

  _Wait, index 0 matches. Let's look at index -1:_

  $$
  (a \star b)[-1] = a[0]b[-1] + a[1]b[0] = (1)(0) + (2)(0) = 0
  $$

  $$
  (b \star a)[-1] = b[0]a[-1] + b[1]a[0] = (0)(0) + (3)(1) = 3
  $$

- **Result:** $0 \neq 3$. The operation is **not commutative** without the "flip" ($i-j$). The flip is required for the associative and commutative properties that make convolution useful for filtering.

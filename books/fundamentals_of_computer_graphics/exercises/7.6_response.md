- **Matrix:** $\mathbf{P} = \begin{bmatrix} n & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & n & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & n+f & -fn \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$

- **Inverse Check:** Multiply $\mathbf{P}$ by the given $\mathbf{P}^{-1}$:

  $$
  \mathbf{P}^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} f & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & f & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & fn \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & n+f \end{bmatrix}
  $$

- **Multiplication:** The result is a diagonal matrix scaled by a factor (effectively identity in homogeneous coordinates), or exactly identity if the scalar $1/fn$ is included.

- **Result:** The inverse form undoes the projection, mapping screen space back to the view frustum.

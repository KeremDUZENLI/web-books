- **Goal:** Show $|\mathbf{a}\mathbf{b}\mathbf{c}| = (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}) \cdot \mathbf{c}$.

- **Geometric Meaning:**

  1. $\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}$ creates a vector perpendicular to the base with length equal to the **base area**.

  2. Dotting this with $\mathbf{c}$ computes $\text{Area} \times (\text{projection of } \mathbf{c} \text{ onto normal})$, which is **Base Area** $\times$ **Height**.

- **Result:** This equals the volume of the parallelepiped, which is the definition of the determinant .

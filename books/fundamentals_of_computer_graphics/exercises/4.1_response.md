- **Given:**

  - Ray origin $\mathbf{e} = (1, 1, 1)$

  - Ray direction $\mathbf{d} = (-1, -1, -1)$

  - Sphere center $\mathbf{c} = (0, 0, 0)$, Radius $R = 1$

- **Equation:** substitute the ray equation $\mathbf{p}(t) = \mathbf{e} + t\mathbf{d}$ into the implicit sphere equation $\|\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{c}\|^2 - R^2 = 0$.

  $$
  \begin{aligned}
  (1-t)^2 + (1-t)^2 + (1-t)^2 - 1 &= 0 \\
  3(1-t)^2 &= 1 \\
  (1-t) &= \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \\
  t &= 1 \mp \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}
  \end{aligned}
  $$

- **Result:** The intersection parameters are $\displaystyle t_1 = 1 - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \approx 0.423$ and $\displaystyle t_2 = 1 + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \approx 1.577$. Since both are positive, the ray hits the sphere twice in front of the camera .

- **Goal:** Create a BRDF that reflects light back toward the source (like a stop sign), is reciprocal, and conserves energy.

- **Geometry:** Retroreflection occurs when the view vector $\mathbf{k}_o$ aligns with the light vector $\mathbf{k}_i$. The angle between them is 0.

- **Phong Inspiration:** The Phong model uses $(\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{k}_o)^p$ to create a lobe around the reflection vector. We want a lobe around the _incident_ vector.

- **Proposed Model:** Use the dot product of the light and view vectors:

  $$
  \rho(\mathbf{k}_i, \mathbf{k}_o) = C (\mathbf{k}_i \cdot \mathbf{k}_o)^p
  $$

- **Properties:**

  1.  **Retroreflection:** Maximized when $\mathbf{k}_i = \mathbf{k}_o$ (vectors aligned).

  2.  **Reciprocity:** The dot product is commutative ($\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = \mathbf{b} \cdot \mathbf{a}$), so the condition is met.

  3.  **Conservation:** $C$ must be chosen (like the Phong normalization factor $\frac{p+2}{2\pi}$) to ensure the integral over the hemisphere $\le 1$.

- **Result:** $\rho = C (\mathbf{k}_i \cdot \mathbf{k}_o)^p$

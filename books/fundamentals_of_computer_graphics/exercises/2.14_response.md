- **Given:** A point $\mathbf{p}$ and a triangle with vertices $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}$.

- **Concept:** Barycentric coordinates $(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ allow you to express $\mathbf{p} = \alpha \mathbf{a} + \beta \mathbf{b} + \gamma \mathbf{c}$.

- **Procedure:**

  1.  Compute the total area of triangle $ABC$ (using the cross product magnitude).

  2.  $\alpha = \text{Area}(P, B, C) / \text{TotalArea}$

  3.  $\beta = \text{Area}(P, A, C) / \text{TotalArea}$

  4.  $\gamma = 1 - \alpha - \beta$

- **Check:** If $0 \le \alpha, \beta, \gamma \le 1$, the point is inside the triangle .

### Answer 7.3.1

**Equation:** $13x_1^2 - 8x_1x_2 + 7x_2^2 = 30$.
**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 13 & -4 \\ -4 & 7 \end{bmatrix} $$

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    $\det(A-\lambda I) = (13-\lambda)(7-\lambda) - 16 = \lambda^2 - 20\lambda + 91 - 16 = \lambda^2 - 20\lambda + 75 = 0$.
    $(\lambda - 15)(\lambda - 5) = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = 5, \lambda_2 = 15$.

2.  **Eigenvectors (Directions of Axes):**
    * $\lambda_1 = 5$: $(A-5I)\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -4 \\ -4 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = 0 \implies 2x_1 - x_2 = 0 \implies \mathbf{v}_1 = (1, 2)^T$.
        Normalized direction: $\mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(1, 2)^T$.
    * $\lambda_2 = 15$: $(A-15I)\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & -4 \\ -4 & -8 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = 0 \implies x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \implies \mathbf{v}_2 = (-2, 1)^T$.
        Normalized direction: $\mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(-2, 1)^T$.

3.  **Lengths of Semi-axes:**
    Standard form in principal coordinates $y_1, y_2$: $5y_1^2 + 15y_2^2 = 30$.
    $\frac{y_1^2}{6} + \frac{y_2^2}{2} = 1$.
    * Major axis (along $\mathbf{u}_1$): Length $2\sqrt{6}$. (Semi-axis $a=\sqrt{6}$).
    * Minor axis (along $\mathbf{u}_2$): Length $2\sqrt{2}$. (Semi-axis $b=\sqrt{2}$).

**Graph Sketch:** An ellipse rotated so the major axis is along the line $y=2x$.

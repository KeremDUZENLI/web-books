### Answer 7.3.4

**Equation:** $3x_1^2 + 3x_2^2 + 3x_3^2 - 2x_1x_2 - 2x_1x_3 - 2x_2x_3 = 4$.
**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -1 & -1 \\ -1 & 3 & -1 \\ -1 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    Notice $A = 4I - J$ (where $J$ is all-ones).
    Eigenvalues of $J$ are $3, 0, 0$.
    Eigenvalues of $A$ are $4-3=1$ and $4-0=4$ (multiplicity 2).
    $\lambda_1 = 1, \lambda_2 = 4, \lambda_3 = 4$.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**
    * $\lambda_1 = 1$: $\mathbf{v}_1 = (1, 1, 1)^T$ (From symmetry or $J\mathbf{x}=3\mathbf{x}$).
    * $\lambda = 4$: Plane orthogonal to $(1,1,1)$. E.g., $\mathbf{v}_2 = (1, -1, 0)^T, \mathbf{v}_3 = (1, 1, -2)^T$.

3.  **Standard Form:**
    $1y_1^2 + 4y_2^2 + 4y_3^2 = 4 \implies \frac{y_1^2}{4} + y_2^2 + y_3^2 = 1$.

**Description:** An **oblate spheroid** (ellipsoid of revolution). It is flattened. The axis of symmetry is the line $x_1=x_2=x_3$. The cross-section perpendicular to this axis is a circle.

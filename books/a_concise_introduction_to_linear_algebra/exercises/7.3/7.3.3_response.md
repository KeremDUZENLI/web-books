### Answer 7.3.3

**Equation:** $2x_1^2 + 4x_1x_2 - x_2^2 = 12$.
**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2 \\ 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    $(2-\lambda)(-1-\lambda) - 4 = \lambda^2 - \lambda - 2 - 4 = \lambda^2 - \lambda - 6 = 0$.
    $(\lambda - 3)(\lambda + 2) = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = 3, \lambda_2 = -2$.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**
    * $\lambda_1 = 3$: $\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \implies -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \implies \mathbf{v}_1 = (2, 1)^T$.
    * $\lambda_2 = -2$: Orthogonal. $\mathbf{v}_2 = (-1, 2)^T$.

3.  **Standard Form:**
    $3y_1^2 - 2y_2^2 = 12 \implies \frac{y_1^2}{4} - \frac{y_2^2}{6} = 1$.
    Hyperbola opening along $\mathbf{v}_1 = (2,1)$.
    Semi-axes: $a=2, b=\sqrt{6}$.

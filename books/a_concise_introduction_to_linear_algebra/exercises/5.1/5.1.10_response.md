### Answer 5.1.10

**1. Orthogonal Complement:**
The plane is $x - y = 0$. Normal vector is $\mathbf{n} = (1, -1, 0)^T$.
The orthogonal complement is the line spanned by $\mathbf{n}$.
Line through origin with direction $(1, -1, 0)$.

**2. Projection Matrix $Q$:**
$Q$ projects onto the line spanned by $\mathbf{a} = (1, -1, 0)^T$.
Using formula from 5.1.8:
$\|\mathbf{a}\|^2 = 2$.
$$ Q = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & -0.5 & 0 \\ -0.5 & 0.5 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

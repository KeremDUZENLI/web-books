### Answer 5.1.8

**Goal:** Projection onto line spanned by $\mathbf{a} = (a, b, c)^T$.
Formula: $P = \frac{\mathbf{a}\mathbf{a}^T}{\|\mathbf{a}\|^2}$.
Denominator: $\|\mathbf{a}\|^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2$.
Numerator: $\mathbf{a}\mathbf{a}^T = \begin{bmatrix} a^2 & ab & ac \\ ab & b^2 & bc \\ ac & bc & c^2 \end{bmatrix}$.

$$ P = \frac{1}{a^2+b^2+c^2} \begin{bmatrix} a^2 & ab & ac \\ ab & b^2 & bc \\ ac & bc & c^2 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Comparison with Corollary 1.2.1:**
Corollary 1.2.1 gives the projection of vector $\mathbf{x}$ onto $\mathbf{a}$ as $\text{proj}_{\mathbf{a}} \mathbf{x} = \frac{\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{a}}{\|\mathbf{a}\|^2} \mathbf{a}$.
Note that $P \mathbf{x} = \frac{\mathbf{a}\mathbf{a}^T \mathbf{x}}{\|\mathbf{a}\|^2} = \frac{\mathbf{a}(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{x})}{\|\mathbf{a}\|^2}$.
This is exactly the matrix form of the vector formula.

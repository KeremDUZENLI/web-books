### Answer 3.6.2

**Basis $A$:** $\mathbf{a}_1=(1,1,2)^T, \mathbf{a}_2=(0,-2,1)^T, \mathbf{a}_3=(1,1,0)^T$.

**a. Transition Standard $\to A$:**
Let $P = [\mathbf{a}_1 \ \mathbf{a}_2 \ \mathbf{a}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
The transition matrix $S$ is $P^{-1}$.
Using Gaussian elimination on $[P|I]$:
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|ccc} 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \xrightarrow{RREF} \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 & -0.5 & 0.5 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2.5 & -0.5 & -1 \end{array} \right] $$
$$ S = \begin{bmatrix} -0.5 & 0.5 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 & 0 \\ 2.5 & -0.5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

**b. Transition $A \to$ Standard:**
This is simply the matrix $P$ constructed from the basis vectors.
$$ P_{A \to std} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

**c. Components of $\mathbf{x}=(3,4,5)^T$:**
$$ [\mathbf{x}]_A = S \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} -0.5 & 0.5 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 & 0 \\ 2.5 & -0.5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1.5 + 2 + 5 \\ 3 - 4 + 0 \\ 7.5 - 2 - 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5.5 \\ -1 \\ 0.5 \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 3.6.6

**Standard Matrix:** $M = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
**Basis $A$:** Obtained by rotation $\theta$.
$$ P_A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix} $$
**Representation $M_A$:**
The formula is $M_A = P_A^{-1} M P_A$.
Since $P_A$ is a rotation matrix (orthogonal), $P_A^{-1} = P_A^T$.
$$ M_A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & \sin \theta \\ -\sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix} $$
$$ = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & 0 \\ -\sin \theta & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix} $$
$$ M_A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos^2 \theta & -\sin \theta \cos \theta \\ -\sin \theta \cos \theta & \sin^2 \theta \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 3.6.13

**Representation $M_{A,B}$:**
This matrix maps coordinates relative to basis $A$ to coordinates relative to basis $B$.
Let $\mathbf{x}$ be a vector. $\mathbf{x} = P_A [\mathbf{x}]_A$.
The linear map is $\mathbf{y} = M \mathbf{x} = M P_A [\mathbf{x}]_A$.
We need $[\mathbf{y}]_B$.
$\mathbf{y} = P_B [\mathbf{y}]_B \implies [\mathbf{y}]_B = P_B^{-1} \mathbf{y}$.
Substitute $\mathbf{y}$:
$$ [\mathbf{y}]_B = P_B^{-1} (M P_A [\mathbf{x}]_A) $$
Thus, the matrix representation is:
$$ M_{A,B} = P_B^{-1} M P_A $$

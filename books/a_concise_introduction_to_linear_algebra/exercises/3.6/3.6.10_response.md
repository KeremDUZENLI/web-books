### Answer 3.6.10

**Goal:** Find basis $C = \{\mathbf{c}_1, \mathbf{c}_2\}$ for $V$ such that $\mathbf{c}_1 = \mathbf{a}_1$ and $\mathbf{c}_2 \perp \mathbf{a}_1$.
This is the Gram-Schmidt process on the basis $A = \{\mathbf{a}_1, \mathbf{a}_2\}$.
$\mathbf{a}_1 = (1, 2, 3)^T$. $\mathbf{a}_2 = (0, 4, -1)^T$.
1.  $\mathbf{c}_1 = \mathbf{a}_1 = (1, 2, 3)^T$.
2.  $\mathbf{c}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \text{proj}_{\mathbf{c}_1} \mathbf{a}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \frac{\mathbf{a}_2 \cdot \mathbf{c}_1}{\|\mathbf{c}_1\|^2} \mathbf{c}_1$.
    $\mathbf{a}_2 \cdot \mathbf{c}_1 = 0 + 8 - 3 = 5$.
    $\|\mathbf{c}_1\|^2 = 1 + 4 + 9 = 14$.
    $\mathbf{c}_2 = (0, 4, -1)^T - \frac{5}{14}(1, 2, 3)^T = (-5/14, 4 - 10/14, -1 - 15/14)^T$.
    $\mathbf{c}_2 = (-5/14, 46/14, -29/14)^T$.
    For simplicity, scale by 14: $\mathbf{c}'_2 = (-5, 46, -29)^T$.
    
**Basis:** $\{ (1, 2, 3)^T, (-5, 46, -29)^T \}$.

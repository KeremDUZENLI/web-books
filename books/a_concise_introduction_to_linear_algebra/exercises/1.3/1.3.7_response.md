### Answer 1.3.7

**Given:** $P_0(5, 4, -8)$. Line is parallel to plane $3x - 4y + 3z = 7$ and the $xy$-plane ($z=0$).

1.  **Find Direction:**
    The line is parallel to both planes, so it is perpendicular to both of their normal vectors.
    * Normal to first plane: $\mathbf{n}_1 = (3, -4, 3)$.
    * Normal to $xy$-plane: $\mathbf{n}_2 = (0, 0, 1)$ (the $z$-axis).
    The line's direction $\mathbf{v}$ is the cross product $\mathbf{n}_1 \times \mathbf{n}_2$.
    $$ \mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 3 & -4 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = (-4)\mathbf{i} - (3)\mathbf{j} + (0)\mathbf{k} = (-4, -3, 0) $$

2.  **Parametric Form:**
    $$ x = 5 - 4t $$
    $$ y = 4 - 3t $$
    $$ z = -8 $$

3.  **Nonparametric Form:**
    $$ \frac{x-5}{-4} = \frac{y-4}{-3}, \quad z = -8 $$

### Answer 1.3.10

**Derivation:**
A line through $\mathbf{b}$ with direction towards $\mathbf{a}$ is:
$\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{b} + t(\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b})$.
Distribute $t$:
$\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{b} + t\mathbf{a} - t\mathbf{b}$
$\mathbf{p} = t\mathbf{a} + (1-t)\mathbf{b}$.

**Geometric Meaning:**
This is a weighted average of the positions $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.
* At $t=0$, $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{b}$.
* At $t=1$, $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{a}$.
* At $t=0.5$, $\mathbf{p}$ is the midpoint.
$t$ represents the "weight" or influence of point $A$, and $(1-t)$ is the influence of point $B$.

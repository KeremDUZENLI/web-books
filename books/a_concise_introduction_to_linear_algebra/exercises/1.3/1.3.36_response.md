### Answer 1.3.36

**Logic:**
Let $D(t) = |\overrightarrow{QP}|^2 = |\mathbf{p}_0 + t\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{q}|^2$.
To minimize distance, we minimize squared distance.
Take derivative with respect to $t$ and set to 0.
$\frac{d}{dt} [ (\mathbf{p}_0 - \mathbf{q} + t\mathbf{v}) \cdot (\mathbf{p}_0 - \mathbf{q} + t\mathbf{v}) ] = 0$.
Using product rule on dot product:
$2 (\mathbf{p}_0 - \mathbf{q} + t\mathbf{v}) \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$.
The term in parentheses is exactly the vector $\overrightarrow{QP}$.
So, $2 (\overrightarrow{QP} \cdot \mathbf{v}) = 0$.
$\overrightarrow{QP} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$.
This means the vector connecting $Q$ to the optimal point $P$ is orthogonal to the line direction $\mathbf{v}$.

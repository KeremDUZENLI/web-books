### Answer 4.2.12

**Subspace $K$:** x-axis ($Span\{\mathbf{e}_1\}$).
**Complement:** A subspace $W$ such that $K \oplus W = \mathbb{R}^3$.
Conditions: $K \cap W = \{0\}$ and $K + W = \mathbb{R}^3$.
$W$ must be a plane not containing the x-axis.
**Example 1:** $W_1 = Span\{\mathbf{e}_2, \mathbf{e}_3\}$ (yz-plane).
**Example 2:** $W_2 = Span\{\mathbf{e}_2, \mathbf{e}_1 + \mathbf{e}_3\}$? No, contains part of x?
Try $W_2 = Span\{(1,1,0), (1,0,1)\}$.
Any vector in $K$ is $(a,0,0)$.
If $(a,0,0) = c_1(1,1,0) + c_2(1,0,1) = (c_1+c_2, c_1, c_2)$, then $c_1=0, c_2=0 \implies a=0$.
So intersection is 0.
$W_2$ is a plane distinct from $yz$-plane.
Both are valid complements. Complement is not unique.

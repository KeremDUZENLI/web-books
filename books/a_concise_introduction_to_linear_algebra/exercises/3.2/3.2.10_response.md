### Answer 3.2.10

**Counterexample:**
Let $U$ be the x-axis in $\mathbb{R}^2$: spanned by $(1,0)$.
Let $V$ be the y-axis in $\mathbb{R}^2$: spanned by $(0,1)$.
$W = U \cup V$ consists of all vectors on the axes.
$\mathbf{u} = (1, 0) \in W$.
$\mathbf{v} = (0, 1) \in W$.
Sum $\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = (1, 1)$.
$(1, 1)$ is not on the x-axis and not on the y-axis. It is not in $W$.
So $U \cup V$ is **not** a subspace.

**Condition for Subspace:**
$U \cup V$ is a subspace if and only if **$U \subseteq V$ or $V \subseteq U$**.
(Proof: If neither is subset of other, pick $u \in U \setminus V$ and $v \in V \setminus U$. Then $u+v$ is in neither, failing closure).

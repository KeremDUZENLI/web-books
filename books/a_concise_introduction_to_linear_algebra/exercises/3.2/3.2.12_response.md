### Answer 3.2.12

**Set:** $W = \{ \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n \mid \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{a} = 0 \}$.

**Decision: Yes, this is a subspace.**

**Proof:**
1.  **Zero Vector:** $\mathbf{0} \cdot \mathbf{a} = 0$. So $\mathbf{0} \in W$.
2.  **Closure under Addition:** Let $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in W$.
    $(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y}) \cdot \mathbf{a} = \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{a} + \mathbf{y} \cdot \mathbf{a} = 0 + 0 = 0$.
    So $\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y} \in W$.
3.  **Closure under Scalar Multiplication:** Let $\mathbf{x} \in W$.
    $(c\mathbf{x}) \cdot \mathbf{a} = c(\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{a}) = c(0) = 0$.
    So $c\mathbf{x} \in W$.


*Geometric Interpretation:* This is the plane (or hyperplane) passing through the origin, normal to vector $\mathbf{a}$.

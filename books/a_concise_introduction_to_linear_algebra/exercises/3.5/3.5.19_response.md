### Answer 3.5.19

$A$ is $4 \times 4$. $U = Span(C_1, C_2, C_3)$. $V = Span(C_2, C_3, C_4)$.
$C_1 = (1,0,0,0)^T$. $C_2 = (0,2,0,0)^T$. $C_3 = (1,0,1,0)^T$. $C_4 = (1,0,2,1)^T$.
* **Basis $U$:** $C_1, C_2, C_3$ are independent. Basis $\{e_1, e_2, e_1+e_3\}$. Simplified: $\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}$. (Hyperplane $x_4=0$).
* **Basis $V$:** $C_2, C_3, C_4$.
    $C_2 \to e_2$.
    $C_3 = e_1 + e_3$.
    $C_4 = e_1 + 2e_3 + e_4$.
    Independent? Yes. Basis $\{e_2, e_1+e_3, e_1+2e_3+e_4\}$.
* **$U \cap V$:**
    Vectors in $U$ have $x_4=0$.
    Vectors in $V$: $c_1 e_2 + c_2(e_1+e_3) + c_3(e_1+2e_3+e_4)$.
    4th component is $c_3$. For intersection, need $c_3=0$.
    Then $v \in Span(e_2, e_1+e_3)$.
    This span is exactly $V \cap \{x_4=0\}$.
    Basis $U \cap V$: $\{ (0,1,0,0)^T, (1,0,1,0)^T \}$.
* **$U + V$:**
    Contains $e_1, e_2, e_3$ (from U) and $e_1+2e_3+e_4$ (from V).
    Thus contains $e_4$. Spans $\mathbb{R}^4$. Basis: Standard basis.

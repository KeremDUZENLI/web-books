### Answer 3.5.10

**Proof:**
$Col(A)$ is the set of all linear combinations of columns of $A$.
$Col(B)$ is the set of all linear combinations of columns of $B$.
$Col(A) + Col(B) = \{ A\mathbf{x} + B\mathbf{y} \}$.
This can be rewritten as block multiplication:
$$ \begin{bmatrix} A & B \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} \\ \mathbf{y} \end{bmatrix} $$
This represents a linear combination of the columns of the augmented matrix $[A \ B]$.
Thus $Col(A) + Col(B) = Col([A \ B])$.

### Answer 3.5.31

**Goal:** $Null(A) = Col(B)$.
This means $A x = 0 \iff x \in Col(B)$.
Rows of $A$ must span the orthogonal complement of $Col(B)$.
$Row(A) = Col(B)^\perp = Null(B^T)$.
Compute $Null(B^T)$. $B = [1,1,1; 0,2,0; 0,0,0; 2,1,0]$.
$B^T = [1,0,0,2; 1,2,0,1; 1,0,0,0]$.
RREF of $B^T$ leads to basis for null space: $A = [0, 0, 1, 0]$.
Check: $Col(B)$ vectors have 3rd component 0. $Ax = x_3$. $x_3=0$ matches.

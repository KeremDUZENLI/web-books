### Answer 2.5.1

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Determinant Check (Optional but quick):**
$det(A) = 2(-1) - 3(4) = -2 - 12 = -14 \neq 0$. Invertible.

**Method 1: Formula for $2 \times 2$**
$$ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} $$
$$ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{-14} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & -3 \\ -4 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 3/14 \\ 4/14 & -2/14 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 3/14 \\ 2/7 & -1/7 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Method 2: Gauss-Jordan on $[A|I]$**
1.  $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|cc} 2 & 3 & 1 & 0 \\ 4 & -1 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right]$
2.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1$: $(0, -7 \mid -2, 1)$.
3.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 / (-7)$: $(0, 1 \mid 2/7, -1/7)$.
4.  $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - 3R_2$:
    RHS: $(1 - 3(2/7), 0 - 3(-1/7)) = (1/7, 3/7)$.
    $R_1$: $(2, 0 \mid 1/7, 3/7)$.
5.  $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 / 2$: $(1, 0 \mid 1/14, 3/14)$.

**Result:**
$$ A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 3/14 \\ 2/7 & -1/7 \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 2.5.20

**Show $(ABC)^{-1} = C^{-1}B^{-1}A^{-1}$:**
Multiply $(ABC)$ by the proposed inverse:
$(ABC) (C^{-1}B^{-1}A^{-1}) = AB (C C^{-1}) B^{-1} A^{-1}$.
$= AB (I) B^{-1} A^{-1} = A (B B^{-1}) A^{-1}$.
$= A (I) A^{-1} = A A^{-1} = I$.
Since the product is $I$, the proposed matrix is indeed the inverse.

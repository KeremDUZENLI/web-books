### Answer 2.5.10

**a. $2 \times 3$ and $3 \times 2$:**
* A $2 \times 3$ matrix (more cols than rows) can have a **right** inverse if Rank = 2 (Full Row Rank). It cannot have a left inverse.
* A $3 \times 2$ matrix (more rows than cols) can have a **left** inverse if Rank = 2 (Full Col Rank). It cannot have a right inverse.

**b. General $m \times n$:**
* Right Inverse exists $\iff$ Rank = $m$ (Full Row Rank). Requires $n \ge m$.
* Left Inverse exists $\iff$ Rank = $n$ (Full Column Rank). Requires $m \ge n$.

**c. Uniqueness:**
* Right inverse is unique $\iff$ $n = m$ (Square and invertible). If $n > m$, there are free variables, so infinite right inverses.
* Left inverse is unique $\iff$ $n = m$. If $m > n$, infinite left inverses.

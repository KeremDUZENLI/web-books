### Answer 2.5.7

We need invertible $A, B$ such that $A+B$ is singular.

**Example:**
$A = I = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$. (Invertible).
$B = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$. (Invertible, determinant is -1).
$A \neq -B$ (since $1 \neq -(-1)$).
$A+B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$. (Determinant is 0, singular).

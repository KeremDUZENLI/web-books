### Answer 2.5.8

**a. Find Right Inverse of $A$ ($2 \times 3$):**
$$ [A|I] = \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|cc} 2 & 0 & 4 & 1 & 0 \\ 4 & -1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] $$
1.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1 \implies (0, -1, -7 \mid -2, 1)$.
    Scale $R_1$: $(1, 0, 2 \mid 0.5, 0)$.
    Scale $R_2$: $(0, 1, 7 \mid 2, -1)$.
    $$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|cc} 1 & 0 & 2 & 0.5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 7 & 2 & -1 \end{array} \right] $$
2.  General Solution for columns of $X$:
    $x_3$ is free ($t$).
    $x_2 = 2 - 7t$ (for col 1 RHS $2$), or $-1 - 7t$ (for col 2 RHS $-1$).
    $x_1 = 0.5 - 2t$ (for col 1), or $0 - 2t$ (for col 2).
    Let's pick $x_3 = 0$ for simplicity (Particular Right Inverse).
    $X = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & 0 \\ 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
    Check: $AX = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 4 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & 0 \\ 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$. Works.

**b. Left Inverse $Y$ ($3 \times 2$):**
We need $YA = I_{3}$.
Rank of $A$ is 2. The product $YA$ will have rank at most 2.
$I_3$ has rank 3.
Impossible. No left inverse exists.

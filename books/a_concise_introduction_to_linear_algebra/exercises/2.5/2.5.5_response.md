### Answer 2.5.5

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 5 \\ 4 & -1 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 & 5 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Reduction:**
1.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1 \implies (0, -7, -7 \mid -2, 1, 0)$.
2.  $R_3 \leftarrow 2R_3 - 3R_1 \implies (0, -5, -5 \mid -3, 0, 2)$.
3.  Observe $R_2$ and $R_3$:
    $R_2 = -7(0, 1, 1)$.
    $R_3 = -5(0, 1, 1)$.
    LHS of rows are proportional.
    $5R_2 - 7R_3 \implies (0, 0, 0 \mid \dots)$.

**Conclusion:**
The matrix is **singular** (not invertible). A row of zeros appears on the LHS.

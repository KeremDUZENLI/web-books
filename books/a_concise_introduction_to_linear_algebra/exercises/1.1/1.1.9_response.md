### Answer 1.1.9

**Centroid:** $\mathbf{p} = \frac{1}{3}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c})$.
**Median:** Consider the median from vertex $\mathbf{a}$ to the midpoint of the opposite side $\mathbf{m} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c})$.
We want to show $\mathbf{p}$ lies on the line segment connecting $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{m}$ at a specific ratio.
A point on this segment is $(1-t)\mathbf{a} + t\mathbf{m}$.
We check if $\mathbf{p}$ corresponds to $t = \frac{2}{3}$ (2/3 of the way from vertex, or 1/3 from midpoint).
$(1 - \frac{2}{3})\mathbf{a} + \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{m} = \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{a} + \frac{2}{3}(\frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c}))$
$= \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{a} + \frac{1}{3}(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c}) = \frac{1}{3}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c})$.
This equals $\mathbf{p}$.
Since $t=2/3$, the centroid is $2/3$ of the way from the vertex, which implies it is $1/3$ of the way from the midpoint (side) to the vertex.

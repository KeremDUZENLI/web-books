### Answer 1.1.5

**Diagram Description:**
1.  Draw two vectors $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ starting from the origin $O$.
2.  Draw the sum $\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}$ using the parallelogram rule.
3.  Extend the vector $\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}$ to double its length. This is $2(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q})$.
4.  Separately, extend $\mathbf{p}$ to $2\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ to $2\mathbf{q}$.
5.  Complete the parallelogram for $2\mathbf{p}$ and $2\mathbf{q}$.
6.  Observe that the diagonal of the larger parallelogram coincides exactly with the doubled diagonal of the smaller one. Thus $2(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}) = 2\mathbf{p} + 2\mathbf{q}$.

### Answer 1.1.10

**Centroid:** $\mathbf{p} = \frac{1}{4}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c} + \mathbf{d})$.
**Face Centroid:** Consider the face $BCD$. Its centroid is $\mathbf{f} = \frac{1}{3}(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{c} + \mathbf{d})$.
We check the line segment from the opposite vertex $\mathbf{a}$ to $\mathbf{f}$.
Parametric equation: $(1-t)\mathbf{f} + t\mathbf{a}$.
We want to see if $\mathbf{p}$ lies on this line.
$\mathbf{p} = \frac{1}{4}\mathbf{a} + \frac{3}{4}(\frac{\mathbf{b}+\mathbf{c}+\mathbf{d}}{3}) = \frac{1}{4}\mathbf{a} + \frac{3}{4}\mathbf{f}$.
This corresponds to $t=1/4$.
So $\mathbf{p}$ is $1/4$ of the way from the face centroid $\mathbf{f}$ to the vertex $\mathbf{a}$.

### Answer 3.4.1

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 & 4 \\ 2 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Row Reduction to Echelon Form:**
1.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1$: $(0, -7, 1)$.
2.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 2R_1$: $(0, -7, 1)$.
3.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_2$: $(0, 0, 0)$.
    $$ U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & -7 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

**1. Basis for Row Space ($Row(A)$):**
The nonzero rows of $U$ form a basis.
$$ \mathcal{B}_{row} = \{ (1, 3, 1), (0, -7, 1) \} $$

**2. Basis for Column Space ($Col(A)$):**
Identify pivot columns in $U$: Columns 1 and 2.
The corresponding columns from the **original** matrix $A$ form a basis.
$$ \mathcal{B}_{col} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\} $$

**3. Basis for Null Space ($Null(A)$):**
Solve $Ux = 0$.
$x_3$ is a free variable ($t$).
$-7x_2 + x_3 = 0 \implies 7x_2 = t \implies x_2 = t/7$.
$x_1 + 3x_2 + x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 + 3t/7 + t = 0 \implies x_1 = -10t/7$.
Vector: $t(-10/7, 1/7, 1)$. To simplify, let $t=7$.
$$ \mathcal{B}_{null} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} -10 \\ 1 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} $$

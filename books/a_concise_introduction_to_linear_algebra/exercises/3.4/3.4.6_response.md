### Answer 3.4.6

**Matrix Ex 3.4.1:** $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 & 4 \\ 2 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
**Echelon Form $U$:** $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & -7 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.

**Check Columns of $U$:**
Col 1: $(1, 0, 0)^T$. Col 2: $(3, -7, 0)^T$.
These span the plane $z=0$.
The actual column space of $A$ contains vector $(1, 3, 2)^T$ (Col 1 of A).
$(1, 3, 2)^T$ has a nonzero $z$-component, so it is **not** in the span of the first two columns of $U$.
Thus, columns of $U$ do not form a basis for $Col(A)$.

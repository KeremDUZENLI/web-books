### Answer 6.3.6

**Proof:**
Consider a tetrahedron with vertices $O, A, B, C$.
Edges vectors from origin: $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}$.
The four face areas vectors (outward) are:
1.  Face $OAB$: $\frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} \times \mathbf{a})$. (Order ensures outward direction, assuming right-hand rule $OA \to OB$ is in). Wait, let's fix orientation. $\frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b})$ points "up" if $\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}$ in plane.
    Standard definition: Sum of outward normals area vectors is 0 for any closed polyhedron.
    Explicit check for tetrahedron:
    $\mathbf{n}_1 = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b})$.
    $\mathbf{n}_2 = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{b} \times \mathbf{c})$.
    $\mathbf{n}_3 = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{c} \times \mathbf{a})$.
    $\mathbf{n}_4 = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{c}-\mathbf{a}) \times (\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a})$ (Back face).
    Expand $\mathbf{n}_4$: $\frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{a})$.
    $= \frac{1}{2} (-\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{c} - \mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b})$.
    Sum:
    $\frac{1}{2} [ (\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}) + (\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{c}) + (\mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{a}) - (\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{c}) - (\mathbf{c}\times\mathbf{a}) - (\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}) ]$.
    All terms cancel. Sum $= \mathbf{0}$.

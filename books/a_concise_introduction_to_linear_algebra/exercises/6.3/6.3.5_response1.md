### Answer 6.3.5

**Geometric Proof:**

The volume of a parallelepiped defined by vectors $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}$ is given by Area of Base $\times$ Height.
Let the base be spanned by $\mathbf{v}$ and $\mathbf{w}$.
Area of Base $A = |\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w}|$.
The vector $\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w}$ is perpendicular to the base.
The height $h$ of the parallelepiped is the projection of $\mathbf{u}$ onto the normal $\mathbf{n}$.
$h = |\mathbf{u} \cdot \frac{\mathbf{n}}{|\mathbf{n}|}|$.
Volume $V = A \cdot h = |\mathbf{n}| \cdot |\mathbf{u} \cdot \frac{\mathbf{n}}{|\mathbf{n}|}| = |\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{n}|$.
Substitute $\mathbf{n}$:
$V = |\mathbf{u} \cdot (\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w})|$.
This is the absolute value of the scalar triple product.

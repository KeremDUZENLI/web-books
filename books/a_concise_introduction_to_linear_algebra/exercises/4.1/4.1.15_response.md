### Answer 4.1.15

**Condition:** Maps orthogonal lines to orthogonal lines.
Let $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}$ be orthogonal. Then $T\mathbf{u} \cdot T\mathbf{v} = 0$.
This characterizes **conformal maps** (scaled rotations/reflections) combined with stretches?
Actually, if it holds for *all* pairs, it must be a **similarity transformation**: $T(\mathbf{x}) = c Q \mathbf{x}$ where $Q$ is orthogonal and $c$ is scalar.
Matrix form: $\begin{bmatrix} a & -b \\ b & a \end{bmatrix}$ or $\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ b & -a \end{bmatrix}$. (Scaled rotation or reflection).

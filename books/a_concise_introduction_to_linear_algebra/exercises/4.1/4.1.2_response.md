### Answer 4.1.2

**Definition:** $T(\mathbf{x}) = \sum x_{Ai} T(\mathbf{a}_i)$.
This defines $T$ based on its action on a basis $\{\mathbf{a}_i\}$.
Let $\mathbf{x} = \sum x_{Ai} \mathbf{a}_i$ and $\mathbf{y} = \sum y_{Ai} \mathbf{a}_i$.
Then $\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y} = \sum (x_{Ai} + y_{Ai}) \mathbf{a}_i$.
$T(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y}) = \sum (x_{Ai} + y_{Ai}) T(\mathbf{a}_i) = \sum x_{Ai} T(\mathbf{a}_i) + \sum y_{Ai} T(\mathbf{a}_i) = T(\mathbf{x}) + T(\mathbf{y})$.
Similarly for scalars.
Thus $T$ is linear.

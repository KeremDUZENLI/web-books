### Answer 4.1.7

**Transformation:** $T(\mathbf{x}) = (\mathbf{a}^T \mathbf{x})\mathbf{b}$.
Note $\mathbf{a}^T \mathbf{x}$ is a scalar.
$\mathbf{b}(\mathbf{a}^T \mathbf{x}) = (\mathbf{b}\mathbf{a}^T)\mathbf{x}$ (Associativity of matrix mult).
The matrix representing $T$ is the outer product $\mathbf{b}\mathbf{a}^T$.
If $\mathbf{a} \in \mathbb{R}^n, \mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{R}^m$, then $[T]$ is $m \times n$.
$[T] = \mathbf{b} \otimes \mathbf{a}$.

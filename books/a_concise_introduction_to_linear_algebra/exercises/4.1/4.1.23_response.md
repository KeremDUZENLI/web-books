### Answer 4.1.23

**a. Linearity:**
Convolution is linear.
$\text{conv}(f, p+q) = \text{conv}(f, p) + \text{conv}(f, q)$.
$\text{conv}(f, cp) = c \text{conv}(f, p)$.
Follows from polynomial multiplication properties.

**b. Matrix:**
This is the **Toeplitz matrix** (or convolution matrix).
For $f = [f_0, f_1, f_2]$ and $p \in \mathcal{P}_4$ (length 5 vec):
Output length $3+5-1 = 7$.
Matrix size $7 \times 5$.
Columns are shifted versions of $f$.
Col 1: $[f_0, f_1, f_2, 0, \dots]^T$.
Col 2: $[0, f_0, f_1, f_2, 0 \dots]^T$.
Fill with coefficients of $f$ along diagonals.

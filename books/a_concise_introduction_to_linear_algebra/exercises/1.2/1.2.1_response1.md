### Answer 1.2.1

Given $\mathbf{p} = (5, 5)$ and $\mathbf{q} = (1, -7)$.

**a. Vector Arithmetic**
* **Sum:** $\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q} = (5+1, 5-7) = (6, -2)$
* **Difference:** $\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} = (5-1, 5-(-7)) = (4, 12)$

**b. Geometric Representation**
* Draw $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ from the origin.
* $\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}$ is the main diagonal of the parallelogram formed by $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$.
* $\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}$ is the other diagonal (connecting the tip of $\mathbf{q}$ to the tip of $\mathbf{p}$).

**c. Lengths (Magnitudes)**
* $|\mathbf{p}| = \sqrt{5^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2} \approx 7.07$
* $|\mathbf{q}| = \sqrt{1^2 + (-7)^2} = \sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2} \approx 7.07$
* $|\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}| = \sqrt{6^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{40} = 2\sqrt{10} \approx 6.32$
* $|\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}| = \sqrt{4^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{160} = 4\sqrt{10} \approx 12.65$

**d. Pythagorean Check**
* $|\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}|^2 = 40$
* $|\mathbf{p}|^2 + |\mathbf{q}|^2 = 50 + 50 = 100$
* **Conclusion:** No, $40 \neq 100$. (This equality only holds if $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ are orthogonal/perpendicular).

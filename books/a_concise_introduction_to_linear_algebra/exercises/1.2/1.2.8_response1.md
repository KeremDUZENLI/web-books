### Answer 1.2.8

Points: $A(1,3), B(2,4), C(4,-1)$.
Vectors:
$\mathbf{p} = \overrightarrow{AB} = B - A = (1, 1)$
$\mathbf{q} = \overrightarrow{BC} = C - B = (2, -5)$

**a. Orthogonal Projection of $\mathbf{p}$ onto $\mathbf{q}$:**
$$ \text{proj}_\mathbf{q} \mathbf{p} = \frac{\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{q}}{|\mathbf{q}|^2} \mathbf{q} $$
$\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{q} = 2 - 5 = -3$.
$|\mathbf{q}|^2 = 2^2 + (-5)^2 = 29$.
Projection $= \frac{-3}{29}(2, -5) = (-6/29, 15/29)$.

**b. Distance of $A$ from line $BC$:**
This is the magnitude of the perpendicular component (the altitude).
$\mathbf{p}_{\perp} = \mathbf{p} - \text{proj}_\mathbf{q} \mathbf{p} = (1, 1) - (-6/29, 15/29) = (35/29, 14/29)$.
Distance $= \sqrt{(35/29)^2 + (14/29)^2} = \frac{7}{29}\sqrt{5^2+2^2} = \frac{7\sqrt{29}}{29} = \frac{7}{\sqrt{29}} \approx 1.3$.

**c. Area of Triangle:**
Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{Base} \times \text{Height}$.
Base $= |\mathbf{q}| = \sqrt{29}$.
Height $= 7/\sqrt{29}$.
Area $= \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{29} \cdot \frac{7}{\sqrt{29}} = 3.5$.

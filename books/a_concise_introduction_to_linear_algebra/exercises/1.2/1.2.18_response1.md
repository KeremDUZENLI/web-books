### Answer 1.2.18

1.  **Unit Vectors:**
    $\mathbf{e}_a = (\cos \alpha, \sin \alpha)$
    $\mathbf{e}_b = (\cos \beta, \sin \beta)$

2.  **Dot Product (Coordinate Definition):**
    $\mathbf{e}_a \cdot \mathbf{e}_b = \cos \alpha \cos \beta + \sin \alpha \sin \beta$.

3.  **Dot Product (Geometric Definition):**
    $\mathbf{e}_a \cdot \mathbf{e}_b = |\mathbf{e}_a| |\mathbf{e}_b| \cos \theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between them.
    Magnitudes are 1. The angle between them is simply the difference $\alpha - \beta$.
    So, $\mathbf{e}_a \cdot \mathbf{e}_b = \cos (\alpha - \beta)$.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    $\cos (\alpha - \beta) = \cos \alpha \cos \beta + \sin \alpha \sin \beta$.

### Answer 1.2.3

Let the vertices be vectors $\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}, \mathbf{r}$.

1.  **Midpoints:**
    * Midpoint of side $PQ$: $\mathbf{m}_1 = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q})$
    * Midpoint of side $PR$: $\mathbf{m}_2 = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{r})$

2.  **Vector connecting midpoints:**
    $$ \overrightarrow{M_1 M_2} = \mathbf{m}_2 - \mathbf{m}_1 $$
    $$ = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{r}) - \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{p} + \mathbf{q}) $$
    $$ = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{q}) $$

3.  **Third Side:**
    The vector for the third side $QR$ is $\overrightarrow{QR} = \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{q}$.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    $\overrightarrow{M_1 M_2} = \frac{1}{2} \overrightarrow{QR}$.
    Since one is a scalar multiple of the other, they are **parallel**. The factor $\frac{1}{2}$ means the segment is **half the length** of the third side.

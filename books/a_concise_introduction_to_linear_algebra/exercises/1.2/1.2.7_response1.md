### Answer 1.2.7

The regular octahedron vertices are the centers of the cube faces:
Top: $(0.5, 0.5, 1)$, Bottom: $(0.5, 0.5, 0)$, Front: $(0.5, 0, 0.5)$, etc.
For simplicity, shift origin to center of cube. Vertices are at $(\pm 1, 0, 0), (0, \pm 1, 0), (0, 0, \pm 1)$ scaled by $0.5$. Let's use standard basis vectors $\pm \mathbf{e}_1, \pm \mathbf{e}_2, \pm \mathbf{e}_3$.

**Edges of Octahedron:**
An edge connects adjacent vertices, e.g., $\mathbf{e}_1$ to $\mathbf{e}_2$.
Vector $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{e}_2 - \mathbf{e}_1 = (-1, 1, 0)$.

**Angles between edges:**
1.  **Edges meeting at a vertex (e.g., top vertex):**
    Edge 1 vector: $(-1, 1, 0)$ (from $\mathbf{e}_1$ to $\mathbf{e}_2$)
    Edge 2 vector: $(-1, 0, 1)$ (from $\mathbf{e}_1$ to $\mathbf{e}_3$)
    $\cos \theta = \frac{1 + 0 + 0}{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{2} \implies 60^\circ$.
    (The faces are equilateral triangles).

2.  **Opposite edges:** They are parallel ($0^\circ$ or $180^\circ$).

### Answer 2.4.8

**Matrices:**
$A$ ($2 \times 3$).
$B$ ($4 \times 2$).

**1. Product $AB$:**
Dimensions: $(2 \times 3) \times (4 \times 2)$.
Inner dimensions (3 and 4) do not match.
**$AB$ is not defined.**

**2. Product $BA$:**
Dimensions: $(4 \times 2) \times (2 \times 3) \to (4 \times 3)$.
$$ BA = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 \\ 2 & 2 \\ 1 & -3 \\ -2 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 5 \\ 1 & -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$
Row 1: $3(2)-4(1)=2, \quad 3(3)-4(-2)=17, \quad 3(5)-4(3)=3$.
Row 2: $2(2)+2(1)=6, \quad 2(3)+2(-2)=2, \quad 2(5)+2(3)=16$.
Row 3: $1(2)-3(1)=-1, \quad 1(3)-3(-2)=9, \quad 1(5)-3(3)=-4$.
Row 4: $-2(2)+5(1)=1, \quad -2(3)+5(-2)=-16, \quad -2(5)+5(3)=5$.
$$ BA = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 17 & 3 \\ 6 & 2 & 16 \\ -1 & 9 & -4 \\ 1 & -16 & 5 \end{bmatrix} $$

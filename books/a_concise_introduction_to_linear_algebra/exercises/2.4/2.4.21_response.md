### Answer 2.4.21

**Matrices:**
$M = \begin{bmatrix} A & B \\ C & D \end{bmatrix}$, $N = \begin{bmatrix} E & F \\ G & H \end{bmatrix}$.

**Block Multiplication:**
Treat the blocks like scalars, provided dimensions allow multiplication.
1.  **Top-Left Block:** (Row 1 of M) $\times$ (Col 1 of N) = $AE + BG$.
2.  **Top-Right Block:** (Row 1 of M) $\times$ (Col 2 of N) = $AF + BH$.
3.  **Bottom-Left Block:** (Row 2 of M) $\times$ (Col 1 of N) = $CE + DG$.
4.  **Bottom-Right Block:** (Row 2 of M) $\times$ (Col 2 of N) = $CF + DH$.

**Result:**
$$ \begin{bmatrix} AE + BG & AF + BH \\ CE + DG & CF + DH \end{bmatrix} $$
This works because standard matrix multiplication is a sum of products, and partitioning simply groups those sums.

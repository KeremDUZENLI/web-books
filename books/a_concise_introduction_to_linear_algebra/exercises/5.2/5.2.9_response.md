### Answer 5.2.9

**Proof:**
Let $Q = [\mathbf{q}_1 \ \mathbf{q}_2 \ \dots \ \mathbf{q}_n]$.
Since $Q$ is orthogonal, $Q Q^T = I$.
Write out the matrix multiplication $Q Q^T$ in terms of columns of $Q$ and rows of $Q^T$.
$Q^T$ has rows $\mathbf{q}_1^T, \dots, \mathbf{q}_n^T$.
The product $Q Q^T$ is the sum of the outer products of the columns of $Q$ with the corresponding rows of $Q^T$:
$$ Q Q^T = \sum_{i=1}^n \mathbf{q}_i \mathbf{q}_i^T $$
Since $Q Q^T = I$, we have:
$$ \sum_{i=1}^n \mathbf{q}_i \mathbf{q}_i^T = I $$

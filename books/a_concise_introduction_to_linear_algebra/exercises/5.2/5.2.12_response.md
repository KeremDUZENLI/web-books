### Answer 5.2.12

**Plane $S$:** Span of $\mathbf{a}=(1, 2, 0)^T, \mathbf{b}=(1, 3, 5)^T$.

**a. Matrices:**
Easier to find normal $\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}$.
$\mathbf{n} = (10, -5, 1)^T$. $\|\mathbf{n}\|^2 = 100+25+1 = 126$.
Projection onto Normal ($P_{norm}$):
$P_{N} = \frac{1}{126} \begin{bmatrix} 100 & -50 & 10 \\ -50 & 25 & -5 \\ 10 & -5 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
Projection onto Plane ($P_S$):
$P_S = I - P_N$.

**c. Decomposition of $\mathbf{x}=(2, 3, 4)^T$:**
Component orthogonal to $S$ (parallel to $\mathbf{n}$):
$\mathbf{x}_{norm} = P_N \mathbf{x} = \frac{\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{n}}{126} \mathbf{n}$.
$\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{n} = 20 - 15 + 4 = 9$.
$\mathbf{x}_{norm} = \frac{9}{126} (10, -5, 1) = \frac{1}{14} (10, -5, 1)$.
Component in $S$:
$\mathbf{x}_S = \mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}_{norm} = (2, 3, 4) - (10/14, -5/14, 1/14) = (18/14, 47/14, 55/14)$.

**d. Distance:**
Distance = $\|\mathbf{x}_{norm}\| = \frac{1}{14} \sqrt{100+25+1} = \frac{\sqrt{126}}{14} = \frac{3\sqrt{14}}{14} \approx 0.8$.
Alternative: $\frac{|\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{n}|}{\|\mathbf{n}\|} = \frac{9}{\sqrt{126}}$.

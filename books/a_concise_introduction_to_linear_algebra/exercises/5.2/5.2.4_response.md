### Answer 5.2.4

**Yes, orthogonal matrices preserve angles.**

**Proof:**
The angle $\theta$ between vectors $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ is defined by:
$$ \cos \theta = \frac{\langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \rangle}{\|\mathbf{x}\| \|\mathbf{y}\|} $$
Let $Q$ be an orthogonal matrix ($Q^T Q = I$).
Consider the transformed vectors $Q\mathbf{x}$ and $Q\mathbf{y}$.
1.  **Preservation of Dot Product:**
    $\langle Q\mathbf{x}, Q\mathbf{y} \rangle = (Q\mathbf{x})^T (Q\mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{x}^T Q^T Q \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{x}^T I \mathbf{y} = \langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \rangle$.
2.  **Preservation of Norm:**
    $\|Q\mathbf{x}\| = \sqrt{\langle Q\mathbf{x}, Q\mathbf{x} \rangle} = \sqrt{\langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{x} \rangle} = \|\mathbf{x}\|$.

Substituting these into the cosine formula:
$$ \cos \theta' = \frac{\langle Q\mathbf{x}, Q\mathbf{y} \rangle}{\|Q\mathbf{x}\| \|Q\mathbf{y}\|} = \frac{\langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \rangle}{\|\mathbf{x}\| \|\mathbf{y}\|} = \cos \theta $$
Thus, the angle is preserved.

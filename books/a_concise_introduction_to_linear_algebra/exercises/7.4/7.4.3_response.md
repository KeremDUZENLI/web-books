### Answer 7.4.3



**Vectors:** $\mathbf{x}=\begin{bmatrix}2e^{i\pi/4}\\ 2i\end{bmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{y}=\begin{bmatrix}e^{i\pi/4}\\ e^{-i\pi/4}\end{bmatrix}$.
Note: $2i = 2e^{i\pi/2}$.

**a. Conjugates:**
* $\mathbf{x}^H = [2e^{-i\pi/4}, -2i]$.
* $\mathbf{y}^H = [e^{-i\pi/4}, e^{i\pi/4}]$.

**b. Lengths:**
* $|\mathbf{x}|^2 = |2e^{i\pi/4}|^2 + |2i|^2 = 4 + 4 = 8 \implies |\mathbf{x}| = \sqrt{8}$.
* $|\mathbf{y}|^2 = |e^{i\pi/4}|^2 + |e^{-i\pi/4}|^2 = 1 + 1 = 2 \implies |\mathbf{y}| = \sqrt{2}$.

**c. Inner Products:**
* $\mathbf{x}^H \mathbf{y} = 2e^{-i\pi/4} e^{i\pi/4} + (-2i)e^{-i\pi/4} = 2 - 2i(\cos(-\pi/4) + i\sin(-\pi/4))$.
    $= 2 - 2i(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}) = 2 - i\sqrt{2} - \sqrt{2} = (2-\sqrt{2}) - i\sqrt{2}$.
* $\mathbf{y}^H \mathbf{x} = \overline{\mathbf{x}^H \mathbf{y}} = (2-\sqrt{2}) + i\sqrt{2}$.

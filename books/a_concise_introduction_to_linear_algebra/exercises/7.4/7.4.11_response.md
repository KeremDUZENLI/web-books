### Answer 7.4.11

**Proof:**
Let $U = e^{itA}$. Given $A$ is Hermitian ($A^H = A$).
$U^H = (e^{itA})^H = e^{-it A^H}$ (since $(e^X)^H = e^{X^H}$ and $\overline{it} = -it$ for real $t$).
$U^H = e^{-it A}$.
$U^H U = e^{-itA} e^{itA} = e^{(-itA + itA)} = e^0 = I$.
(We can combine exponents because $A$ commutes with itself).
Thus $U$ is unitary.

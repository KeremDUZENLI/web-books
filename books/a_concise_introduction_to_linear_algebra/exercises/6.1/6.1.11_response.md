### Answer 6.1.11

**Proof:**
We know $AB$ is invertible $\iff det(AB) \neq 0$.
Using the product rule: $det(AB) = det(A) det(B)$.
Since determinants are real scalars:
$det(A) det(B) \neq 0$ if and only if $det(A) \neq 0$ AND $det(B) \neq 0$.
$det(A) \neq 0 \iff A$ is invertible.
$det(B) \neq 0 \iff B$ is invertible.
Thus, $AB$ is invertible $\iff$ both $A$ and $B$ are invertible.

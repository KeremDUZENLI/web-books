### Answer 6.1.18

**Code Analysis:**
1.  `x = 1:6`: Creates ordered vector.
2.  `y = randperm(6)`: Creates a random permutation of 1..6.
3.  `A, B`: Transposed Vandermonde matrices.
4.  `P = B\A`: Since $B$ and $A$ involve the same set of numbers just permuted, $P$ essentially maps the order of $y$ to $x$. $P$ will be a **Permutation Matrix**.
5.  `det(P)`: The determinant of a permutation matrix is $+1$ (even permutation) or $-1$ (odd permutation). It signifies the parity of the permutation `y`.

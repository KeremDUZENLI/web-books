### Answer 6.1.9

**Proof:**
Let $A$ be an invertible matrix. Then $A A^{-1} = I$.
Take the determinant of both sides:
$$ det(A A^{-1}) = det(I) $$
Using the multiplicative property of determinants (Theorem 6.1.9):
$$ det(A) det(A^{-1}) = 1 $$
Since $A$ is invertible, $det(A) \neq 0$. Dividing by $det(A)$ gives:
$$ det(A^{-1}) = \frac{1}{det(A)} $$

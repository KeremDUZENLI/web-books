### Answer 7.1.2

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = 2I $$

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    Since $A$ is diagonal, the eigenvalues are the diagonal entries.
    $\lambda_1 = 2, \quad \lambda_2 = 2$.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**
    $(A - 2I)\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \implies 0\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
    Every non-zero vector in $\mathbb{R}^2$ is an eigenvector.
    Basis: $\mathbf{e}_1 = (1, 0)^T, \mathbf{e}_2 = (0, 1)^T$.

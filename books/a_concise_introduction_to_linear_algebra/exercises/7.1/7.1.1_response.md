### Answer 7.1.1

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$

1.  **Characteristic Equation:**
    $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 2-\lambda & 3 \\ 3 & 2-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (2-\lambda)^2 - 9 = 0$.
    $(2-\lambda)^2 = 9 \implies 2-\lambda = \pm 3$.
    $\lambda_1 = -1, \quad \lambda_2 = 5$.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**
    * For $\lambda_1 = -1$: Solve $(A + I)\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
        $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 3 \\ 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies x_1 + x_2 = 0$.
        $\mathbf{s}_1 = (1, -1)^T$.
    * For $\lambda_2 = 5$: Solve $(A - 5I)\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
        $\begin{bmatrix} -3 & 3 \\ 3 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies -3x_1 + 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = x_2$.
        $\mathbf{s}_2 = (1, 1)^T$.

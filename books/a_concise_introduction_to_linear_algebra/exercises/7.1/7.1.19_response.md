### Answer 7.1.19

**Proof:**
Suppose $A\mathbf{x} = \lambda_1 \mathbf{x}$ and $A\mathbf{x} = \lambda_2 \mathbf{x}$ with $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$.
Subtracting the equations:
$\mathbf{0} = (\lambda_1 - \lambda_2) \mathbf{x}$.
Since $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$, the scalar factor must be zero.
$\lambda_1 - \lambda_2 = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = \lambda_2$.
Thus, a vector cannot belong to two *different* eigenvalues.

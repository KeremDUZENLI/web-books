### Answer 7.1.15

**Matrix:** $A = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T$ where $\|\mathbf{u}\| = 1$.

1.  **Apply to $\mathbf{u}$:**
    $A\mathbf{u} = (\mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T)\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{u}(\mathbf{u}^T\mathbf{u}) = \mathbf{u}(1) = 1\mathbf{u}$.
    $\mathbf{u}$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue $\lambda_1 = 1$.

2.  **Apply to $\mathbf{v} \perp \mathbf{u}$:**
    $A\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{u}^T\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{u}(0) = \mathbf{0} = 0\mathbf{v}$.
    Any vector orthogonal to $\mathbf{u}$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue $\lambda = 0$.

**Results:**
* Eigenvalue $1$, Eigenspace spanned by $\mathbf{u}$ (Line).
* Eigenvalue $0$, Eigenspace is $\mathbf{u}^\perp$ (Hyperplane).

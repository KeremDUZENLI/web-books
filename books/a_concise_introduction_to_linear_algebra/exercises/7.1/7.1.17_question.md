### Exercise 7.1.17

A row vector $\mathbf{s}^{T}$ is called a left eigenvector of a matrix $A$ belonging to the eigenvalue $\lambda$ if the equation $\mathbf{s}^{T}A=\lambda \mathbf{s}^{T}$ holds. Show that $\mathbf{s}^{T}$ is a left eigenvector of a matrix $A$ belonging to the eigenvalue $\lambda$ if and only if $\mathbf{s}$ is an eigenvector of $A^{T}$ belonging to the eigenvalue $\lambda$.

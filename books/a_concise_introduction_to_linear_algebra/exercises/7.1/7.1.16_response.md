### Answer 7.1.16

**Projection Matrix $P$:** Satisfies $P^2 = P$.
Let $P\mathbf{x} = \lambda \mathbf{x}$.
$P^2 \mathbf{x} = P(\lambda \mathbf{x}) = \lambda P\mathbf{x} = \lambda^2 \mathbf{x}$.
Also $P^2 \mathbf{x} = P\mathbf{x} = \lambda \mathbf{x}$.
So $\lambda^2 \mathbf{x} = \lambda \mathbf{x}$. Since $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$, $\lambda^2 = \lambda \implies \lambda(\lambda-1) = 0$.
**Eigenvalues:** $0$ and $1$.
**Eigenspaces:**
* $\lambda=1$: $Range(P)$ (vectors projected onto themselves).
* $\lambda=0$: $Null(P)$ (vectors orthogonal to the projection space, assuming orthogonal projection).

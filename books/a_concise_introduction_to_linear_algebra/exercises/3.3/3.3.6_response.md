### Answer 3.3.6

**Goal:** Solve for $\mathbf{b}=(4,7,0)^T$. Matrix $A$ is same as Ex 3.3.5.
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 4 & 0 & 4 \\ 2 & -3 & 5 & 7 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 & 0 \end{array} \right] $$

**Reduction:**
1.  Swap $R_1, R_3$: $(1, 2, -1 \mid 0)$.
2.  $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1$: $(0, -7, 7 \mid 7)$.
3.  $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 4R_1$: $(0, -4, 4 \mid 4)$.
4.  $R_2$: $-7(x_2 - x_3) = 7 \implies x_2 - x_3 = -1$.
    $R_3$: $-4(x_2 - x_3) = 4 \implies x_2 - x_3 = -1$.
    Consistent! Infinite solutions.
    Let $x_3 = t$. Then $x_2 = t - 1$.
    $x_1 + 2(t-1) - t = 0 \implies x_1 + 2t - 2 - t = 0 \implies x_1 = 2 - t$.

**Result:**
Many combinations exist, e.g., (setting $t=0$): $\mathbf{b} = 2\mathbf{a}_1 - \mathbf{a}_2$.

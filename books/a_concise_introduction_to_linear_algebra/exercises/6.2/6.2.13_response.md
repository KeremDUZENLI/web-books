### Answer 6.2.13

**Derivation:**
The area of a triangle with vertices at the origin, $\mathbf{u}$, and $\mathbf{v}$ is $\frac{1}{2} | \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} |$ (in 2D, determinant of $2 \times 2$).
Translate the triangle so $(c_1, c_2)$ is at the origin.
Vertices become $(a_1-c_1, a_2-c_2)$ and $(b_1-c_1, b_2-c_2)$.
Area $= \frac{1}{2} | (a_1-c_1)(b_2-c_2) - (a_2-c_2)(b_1-c_1) |$.
Now expand the $3 \times 3$ determinant given:
$\frac{1}{2} \begin{vmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & 1 \\ b_1 & b_2 & 1 \\ c_1 & c_2 & 1 \end{vmatrix}$.
Row ops: $R_1-R_3, R_2-R_3$:
$\frac{1}{2} \begin{vmatrix} a_1-c_1 & a_2-c_2 & 0 \\ b_1-c_1 & b_2-c_2 & 0 \\ c_1 & c_2 & 1 \end{vmatrix}$.
Expand col 3: $\frac{1}{2} (1) \begin{vmatrix} a_1-c_1 & a_2-c_2 \\ b_1-c_1 & b_2-c_2 \end{vmatrix}$.
This matches the geometric area formula.

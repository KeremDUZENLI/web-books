### Answer 6.2.18

**Derivation:**
Volume of tetrahedron is $\frac{1}{6}$ Volume of parallelepiped defined by edges from one vertex.
Edges from $\mathbf{d}$: $\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{a}-\mathbf{d}, \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{b}-\mathbf{d}, \mathbf{w} = \mathbf{c}-\mathbf{d}$.
Volume $= \frac{1}{6} | det([\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}]) |$.
This $3 \times 3$ determinant is exactly the result of expanding the given $4 \times 4$ determinant after subtracting the last row from the first three.
$\begin{vmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 & 1 \\ \dots & \dots & \dots & 1 \\ d_1 & d_2 & d_3 & 1 \end{vmatrix} \xrightarrow{R_i-R_4} \begin{vmatrix} a_1-d_1 & a_2-d_2 & a_3-d_3 & 0 \\ \dots & \dots & \dots & 0 \\ d_1 & d_2 & d_3 & 1 \end{vmatrix}$.
Expand Col 4: $1 \cdot det(3 \times 3)$.
Matches formula.

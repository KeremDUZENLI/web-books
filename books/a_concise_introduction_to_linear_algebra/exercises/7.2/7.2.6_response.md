### Answer 7.2.6

**Matrix:**
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} $$
This is an upper triangular matrix with eigenvalues on the diagonal: 1, 1, 2, 2.
We can compute $A^4$ by direct multiplication or block arithmetic.
$$ A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$
$$ A^4 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 15 \\ 0 & 1 & 15 & 15 \\ 0 & 0 & 16 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 16 \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 7.2.4

**Matrix:** $A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.

1.  **Diagonalize:**
    Char Eq: $(3-\lambda)^2 - 4 = 0 \implies 3-\lambda = \pm 2$.
    $\lambda_1 = 5, \lambda_2 = 1$.
    Eigenvectors: $\lambda=5 \to (1,1)^T$. $\lambda=1 \to (1,-1)^T$.
    Normalize for orthogonal $Q$: $Q = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
    $A = Q \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} Q^T$.

2.  **$A^{100}$:**
    $$ A^{100} = Q \begin{bmatrix} 5^{100} & 0 \\ 0 & 1^{100} \end{bmatrix} Q^T = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5^{100} & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$
    $$ = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 5^{100}+1 & 5^{100}-1 \\ 5^{100}-1 & 5^{100}+1 \end{bmatrix} $$

3.  **$A^{1/2}$:**
    Same logic with $\sqrt{5}$ and $\sqrt{1}=1$.
    $$ A^{1/2} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{5}+1 & \sqrt{5}-1 \\ \sqrt{5}-1 & \sqrt{5}+1 \end{bmatrix} $$

### Answer 7.2.11

**System:** $\mathbf{x}_{k+1} = A \mathbf{x}_k$ with $A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.8 & 0.4 \\ -0.8 & 2.0 \end{bmatrix}$.

1.  **Eigenvalues:**
    $\det(A-\lambda I) = \lambda^2 - 2.8\lambda + 1.92$.
    Roots: $\lambda_1 = 1.6, \lambda_2 = 1.2$.
    Both $> 1$, so population grows indefinitely.

2.  **Eigenvectors:**
    $\lambda=1.6 \implies \mathbf{s}_1 = (1, 2)^T$.
    $\lambda=1.2 \implies \mathbf{s}_2 = (1, 1)^T$.

3.  **Solution:**
    $\mathbf{x}_0 = (1000, 1000)^T$.
    Decompose: $\mathbf{x}_0 = 0 \cdot \mathbf{s}_1 + 1000 \cdot \mathbf{s}_2$.
    (The initial state is exactly an eigenvector).
    $$ \mathbf{x}_k = 1000 (1.2)^k \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} $$
    As $k \to \infty$, both populations grow at a rate of $20\%$ per year, maintaining a 1:1 ratio.

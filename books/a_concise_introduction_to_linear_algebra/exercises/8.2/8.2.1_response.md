### Answer 8.2.1

**System:**
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 0.002 & 1 & 4 \\ 6 & -1 & 2 \end{array} \right] $$

**Hypothetical Machine:** Assumes standard floating point representation with limited precision (e.g., 3 significant digits).

**Step 1: Elimination without Pivoting**
Multiplier $m = 6 / 0.002 = 3000$.
$R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3000 R_1$.
New $a_{22} = -1 - 3000(1) = -3001$.
New $b_2 = 2 - 3000(4) = 2 - 12000 = -11998$.

**Machine Representation (3 digits):**
$-3001 \to -0.300 \times 10^4$.
$-11998 \to -0.120 \times 10^5$.

Equation for $x_2$: $-3000 x_2 = -12000 \implies x_2 = 4$.
Solve for $x_1$: $0.002 x_1 + 4 = 4 \implies 0.002 x_1 = 0 \implies x_1 = 0$.

**Result:** $\mathbf{x}_{machine} \approx (0, 4)^T$.

**Exact Solution:**
$0.002 x_1 + x_2 = 4$
$6 x_1 - x_2 = 2$
Add equations: $6.002 x_1 = 6 \implies x_1 \approx 0.9997$.
$x_2 = 6(0.9997) - 2 \approx 3.998$.
**Exact:** $\mathbf{x} \approx (1, 4)^T$.

**Discrepancy Explanation:**
The small pivot $0.002$ caused a large multiplier ($3000$). When subtracting rows, the large numbers swamped the small significant digits of the original row 2 ($-1$ and $2$). This loss of precision led to a wildly incorrect $x_1$ value. This is a classic example of instability due to a small pivot.

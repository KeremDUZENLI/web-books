### Answer 11.8
**(a)** 3.
**(b)** $Av = \lambda v \implies ||Av|| = |\lambda| ||v||$. Rotation preserves length ($||Av|| = ||v||$), so $|\lambda|=1$. Real roots $\lambda \in \{1, -1\}$.
**(c)** Characteristic polynomial is cubic. Complex roots appear in conjugate pairs. Roots are either $\{r_1, r_2, r_3\}$ (3 real) or $\{r_1, z, \bar{z}\}$ (1 real).
**(d)** $\det(A) = 1$.
Case 1 Real: $r_1 z \bar{z} = 1 \implies r_1 |z|^2 = 1$. Since $|z|^2 > 0$, $r_1$ must be positive. $r_1=1$.
Case 3 Real: $r_1 r_2 r_3 = 1$. Eigenvalues are $\pm 1$. Possibilities: $\{1, 1, 1\}$ or $\{1, -1, -1\}$. In all cases, at least one is 1.
**(e)** Since 1 is an eigenvalue, there exists $v \neq 0$ such that $Av = 1v = v$. This is the axis.

### Answer 11.9
**(a)**
$S = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -c & b \\ c & 0 & -a \\ -b & a & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
Inverse mapping: $\omega = \begin{bmatrix} -S_{23} \\ S_{13} \\ -S_{12} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix}$.

**(b)**
$S \omega = \omega \times \omega = 0 = 0 \omega$.
Since $\omega \neq 0$ (for non-zero S), 0 is an eigenvalue.
$\det(S) = \prod \lambda_i = 0 \cdot \lambda_2 \cdot \lambda_3 = 0$.

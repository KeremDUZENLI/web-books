**Implementation Plan:**

2.  **Ball:** Draw a red circle.
    * Position $P(u) = (x_0 + u \cdot \Delta x, y_0 + u \cdot \Delta y)$.
3.  **Shadow:** Draw a shape (Ellipse/Square/Airplane) in gray.
    * Shadow Position determines depth perception.
    * **Case 1 (Diagonal Motion):** Shadow moves diagonally with the ball.
    * **Case 2 (Levitation):** Shadow stays fixed on the floor while ball moves "up".
4.  **Slider:** Controls parameter $u$ (0 to 1).

**Result:**
Changing the shadow shape does *not* significantly break the illusion of depth; the brain prioritizes the *existence* and *motion* of the shadow over its geometric accuracy. An airplane shadow under a sphere still effectively places the sphere in 3D space.

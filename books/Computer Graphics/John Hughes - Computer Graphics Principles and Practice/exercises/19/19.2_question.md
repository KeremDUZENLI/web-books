### Exercise 19.2
    
One favorite filter is the Gaussian, defined by $g(x,y)=Ce^{-\pi(x^{2}+y^{2})}$. It has three important properties. First, it is its own Fourier transform. Second, it's circularly symmetric: Its value on the circle of radius r around the origin is constant. Third, it's a product of two one-dimensional filters. Do the algebra to show the second and third properties.

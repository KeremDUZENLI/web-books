### Answer 19.1
    
**Method 1 (Endpoint-inclusive):** $u_i = -0.5 + \frac{i}{N-1}$
**Method 2 (Cell-centered):** $u_i = -0.5 + \frac{i + 0.5}{N}$

**(a) N = 3**
* **Method 1:** $i \in \{0, 1, 2\}$
    $u = \{-0.5, 0.0, 0.5\}$
* **Method 2:** Spacing $1/3$.
    $u = \{-0.5 + 1/6, -0.5 + 3/6, -0.5 + 5/6\} = \{-1/3, 0, 1/3\}$

**(b) N = 2**
* **Method 1:** $i \in \{0, 1\}$
    $u = \{-0.5, 0.5\}$
* **Method 2:** Spacing $1/2$.
    $u = \{-0.25, 0.25\}$

**N = 1**
* **Method 1:** Division by zero (undefined). If defined by limit, usually $0$.
* **Method 2:** $u = \{0\}$

**(c) Consistency Check**
We want the subset of samples to align.
* **Method 2** (Cell-centered) preserves spatial alignment. For 200 points on $[-1, 1]$, the interval is $2/200 = 0.01$. The indices $i=50$ to $149$ correspond to the range $[-0.5, 0.5]$.
    Example: $u_{50} = -1 + (50.5)/100 = -0.495$.
    For the narrower problem ($N=100$ on $[-0.5, 0.5]$), $u_0 = -0.5 + 0.5/100 = -0.495$.
    The sample locations match.
    
* Method 1 does not align. 200 points on $[-1, 1]$ places samples at $-1, -1 + 2/199 \dots$. 100 points on $[-0.5, 0.5]$ places samples at $-0.5, -0.5 + 1/99 \dots$. These grids are disjoint except at specific coincidences.

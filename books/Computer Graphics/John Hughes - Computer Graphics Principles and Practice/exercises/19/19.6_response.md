### Answer 19.6
    
Function: $f(x) = \text{sinc}(x)$.
Fourier Transform: $\mathcal{F}(f) = \text{box}(\omega)$ on $[-\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}]$.

**Band-limiting:**
We apply a low-pass filter to cut off frequencies $|\omega| > \frac{1}{4}$.
The resulting spectrum is $\text{box}(\omega)$ on $[-\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{4}]$.

**Resultant Signal:**
We know $\text{box}_{[-A, A]}(\omega) \leftrightarrow 2A \text{sinc}(2Ax)$.
Here $A = 1/4$, so total width is $1/2$.
$$
g(x) = 2(1/4) \text{sinc}(2(1/4)x) = \frac{1}{2} \text{sinc}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
$$
The signal is broadened (stretched) by a factor of 2 and its amplitude halved.

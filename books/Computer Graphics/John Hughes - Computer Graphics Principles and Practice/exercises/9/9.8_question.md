### Exercise 9.8
Consider the interval $[p,q]$ where $p\ne q$. If we define $\alpha(x)=\frac{x-p}{q-p}$ and $\beta(x)=\frac{x-q}{p-q}$ then $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are called the barycentric coordinates of x.
(a) Show that for $x\in[p,q]$, both $\alpha(x)$ and $\beta(x)$ are between 0 and 1.
(b) Show that $\alpha(x)+\beta(x)=1$.
(c) Clearly $\alpha$ and $\beta$ can be defined on the rest of the real line, and these definitions depend on p and q; if we call them $\alpha_{pq}$ and $\beta_{pq},$ then we can, for another interval $[p^{\prime},q^{\prime}]$, define corresponding barycentric coordinates. How are $\alpha_{pq}(x)$ and $\alpha_{p^{\prime},q^{\prime}}(x)$ related?

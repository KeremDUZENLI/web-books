### Answer 9.7
**(a) Algorithm:**
1.  Calculate total length $L$. Determine step $\delta = L/k$.
2.  Traverse the original polyline edges. Maintain a `current_dist` accumulator.
3.  Whenever `current_dist` + `edge_length` $\ge$ next target step $i\delta$, interpolate the point on the edge and increment target step.

**(b) Gap Bound:**
Let the shortest edge length be $s$. For any edge of length $l$, assign $k_{edge} = \lfloor l/s \rfloor$ segments. The gap size is $g = l/k_{edge}$.
Since $k_{edge} \le l/s$, $g \ge s$.
Since $k_{edge} > l/s - 1$, and assuming $l \ge s$, the ratio $l/k_{edge}$ is bounded. Specifically, if $l \approx s$, $k=1$ and $g=l$. If $l$ is large, $g \approx s$. The maximum gap is less than $2s$.

**(c) Max-Min Ratio:**
With $C L / s$ points (where $C > 1$), we have a budget to place $\approx C$ times more points. We can choose integers $k_i$ such that gaps are approximately $s/C$. As $C \to \infty$, the discretization error decreases, and the ratio of the largest gap to the smallest gap approaches 1.

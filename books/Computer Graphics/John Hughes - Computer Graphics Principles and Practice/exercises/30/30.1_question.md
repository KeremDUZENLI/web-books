### Exercise 30.1
    
A friend picks three positive numbers A, B, and C and then draws the function graph shown in Figure 30.11. You don't know the values A, B, and C. You get to ask your friend one question of the form "What is the value of $f(s)$?" for some particular s. Given these constraints, you are to estimate the integral of f over [0, 3]. Your approach is to flip a three-sided coin that lands on side $i=1, 2,$ or 3. You ask your friend, "What's $f(i-\frac{1}{2})$?" You multiply his answer by three and use this as an estimate of the integral. The value produced is evidently a random variable, X.

(a) What's the expected value $\mu=E[X]$?
(b) What's the variance of $X$? Express your answer in terms of A, B, C, and $\mu$.
(c) Under what conditions on A, B, and C is the variance zero? Under what conditions is it large compared to $\mu$?

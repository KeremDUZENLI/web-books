### Answer 30.4
    
**(a) Using Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**
Given $f(b) = \int_{a}^{b} p_W(r) dr$.
By the FTC, the derivative of an integral with respect to its upper limit is the integrand evaluated at that limit.
$$ f'(b) = p_W(b) $$

**(b) Using Algebraic Expression**
Given $f(b) = b^2 - a^2$.
Differentiating with respect to $b$ (treating $a$ as constant):
$$ f'(b) = \frac{d}{db}(b^2 - a^2) = 2b $$

**(c) Conclusion**
Equating the results from (a) and (b):
$$ p_W(b) = 2b $$
Thus, $p_W(r) = 2r$ for $r \in [0,1]$.

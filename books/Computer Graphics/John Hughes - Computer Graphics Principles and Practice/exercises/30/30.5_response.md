### Answer 30.5
    
**(a) Exact Integral**
$$ I = \int_0^1 x^2 dx = \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_0^1 = \frac{1}{3} $$

**(b) Stochastic Integration (Uniform)**
Estimator $Y = (1-0) \cdot X^2 = X^2$ where $X \sim U(0,1)$.
Repeat for varying $n$.

**(c) Error Plot**
Error roughly follows $1/\sqrt{n}$.

**(d) Function $f(x) = \cos^2(x)e^{-20x}$**
Exact integral (approx): dominated by exponential decay near 0.
$\int_0^1 \cos^2(x)e^{-20x} dx \approx \int_0^\infty 1 \cdot e^{-20x} dx = 1/20 = 0.05$. (Very rough).
Accurate calculation yields a small value.

**(e) Importance Sampling**
PDF proportional to $s(x) = e^{-20x}$.
Normalization constant $C = \int_0^1 e^{-20x} dx = [\frac{e^{-20x}}{-20}]_0^1 = \frac{1 - e^{-20}}{20} \approx \frac{1}{20}$.
PDF $p(x) = \frac{e^{-20x}}{C} \approx 20e^{-20x}$.
Inversion sampling:
CDF $P(x) = \int_0^x 20e^{-20t} dt = 1 - e^{-20x} = u$.
$e^{-20x} = 1-u$.
$x = -\ln(1-u)/20$. Since $u \sim U(0,1)$, $1-u$ is also uniform, so $x = -\ln(u)/20$ works.
Estimator:
$$ Y = \frac{f(x)}{p(x)} = \frac{\cos^2(x)e^{-20x}}{\frac{e^{-20x}}{C}} = C \cos^2(x) \approx \frac{\cos^2(x)}{20} $$

**(f) Comparison**
**Yes**, this produces much better estimates.
* **Reason:** The function $f(x)$ decays extremely rapidly due to $e^{-20x}$. A uniform sampler spends most of its time ($x > 0.2$) sampling where the function is effectively zero, contributing nothing to the estimate but increasing variance (many 0s, few large values near 0).
* The importance sampler concentrates samples near $x=0$ where the "action" is. The estimator $C \cos^2(x)$ is bounded and smooth (since $\cos^2(x) \approx 1$ near 0), leading to very low variance.

### Answer 23.5
    
**Analysis of Loop Subdivision:**
1.  **Splitting:** In Loop subdivision, every triangular face $(v_1, v_2, v_3)$ is split into 4 subtriangles.
2.  **New Vertices:** A new vertex ("edge vertex") is inserted on every edge of the original mesh.
3.  **Connectivity:**
    * Each new edge vertex connects to the two endpoints of the original edge.
    * Each new edge vertex also connects to the two *other* new edge vertices generated within the same original face (to form the central subtriangle).
    * Since an interior edge is shared by exactly two faces, the new vertex on that edge connects to:
        * 2 original endpoints.
        * 2 new vertices in the left face.
        * 2 new vertices in the right face.
    * Total connections = $2 + 2 + 2 = 6$.

**Conclusion:**
Every newly introduced vertex lies on an edge shared by two triangles. It connects to the two endpoints of that edge and the four other new edge vertices arising from the two adjacent triangles. Thus, every new vertex has exactly 6 neighbors (valence 6).

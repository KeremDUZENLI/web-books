### Answer 28.8
    
**Calculation:**
1.  **Blackbody Spectrum:** Use Planck's Law to determine the spectral radiance $L_e(\lambda, T)$ in units of $W \cdot sr^{-1} \cdot m^{-3}$ (or per nm).
    $L_e(\lambda, T) = \frac{2hc^2}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{e^{hc/\lambda k_B T} - 1}$.
2.  **Integration:** Compute the total luminance $L_v$ (in nits, or $cd/m^2$) by integrating the spectral radiance against the luminous efficiency function $V(\lambda)$.
    $$ L_v = 683 \int_{380 nm}^{780 nm} L_e(\lambda, T) V(\lambda) d\lambda $$
    (The factor 683 $lm/W$ converts radiant watts to luminous lumens at 555 nm).
3.  **Scaling:** The problem states the total radiance is 18 $W m^{-2} sr^{-1}$. The Planck formula gives a specific total radiance $R_{total} = \int L_e(\lambda, T) d\lambda$.
    Calculate the ratio $k = 18 / R_{total}$.
    The final answer is $k \cdot L_v$ nits.

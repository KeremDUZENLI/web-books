### Answer 18.2
    
(a) The definition of convolution is $(f * g_a)(t_0) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)g_a(t_0 - x) dx$.
The function $g_a(t_0 - x)$ is non-zero (equal to $\frac{1}{2a}$) only when $|t_0 - x| < a$, which is equivalent to $t_0 - a < x < t_0 + a$.
Therefore:
$$
(f * g_a)(t_0) = \int_{t_0-a}^{t_0+a} f(x) \left(\frac{1}{2a}\right) dx = \frac{1}{2a} \int_{t_0-a}^{t_0+a} f(x) dx
$$
This matches the definition of $U(a)$ in Equation 18.26.

(b) The sample of $f$ at $t_0$ is defined as the limit of the average value over a shrinking interval around $t_0$.
Using the result from (a):
$$
\text{Sample at } t_0 = \lim_{a \to 0} U(a) = \lim_{a \to 0} (f * g_a)(t_0)
$$

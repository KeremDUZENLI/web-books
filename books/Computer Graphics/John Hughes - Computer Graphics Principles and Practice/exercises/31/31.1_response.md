### Answer 31.1
    
**(a) Path Tracing Cost**
Let $r$ be the attenuation rate (termination probability). The probability of a path having length $k$ (number of segments) is proportional to $(1-r)^{k-1}$.
Alternatively, consider the expected number of rays generated per primary ray.
1.  Primary ray: 1 (always traced).
2.  Secondary ray: probability $(1-r)$.
3.  Tertiary ray: probability $(1-r)^2$.
The expected number of rays per pixel is the sum of the geometric series:
$$ E[\text{rays}] = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (1-r)^k = \frac{1}{1 - (1-r)} = \frac{1}{r} $$
For $N$ primary rays, the total number of rays traced is $N/r$.
Each step involves one ray trace (cost A) and typically one BRDF evaluation to determine the weight/contribution (cost B).
**Total Time:**
$$ T_{path} \approx \frac{N}{r} (A + B) $$

**(b) Bidirectional Path Tracing Cost**
We trace $N/2$ eye paths and $N/2$ light paths.
1.  **Tracing Cost:**
    * Eye paths: $\frac{N/2}{r}$ segments.
    * Light paths: $\frac{N/2}{r}$ segments.
    * Trace Cost: $\frac{N}{r}(A + B)$.
2.  **Connection Cost:**
    Bidirectional path tracing connects every vertex of the eye path to every vertex of the light path.
    * Average vertices per eye path: $1/r$.
    * Average vertices per light path: $1/r$.
    * Connections per path pair: $(1/r) \times (1/r) = 1/r^2$.
    * Number of pairs: $N/2$ (assuming 1-to-1 pairing of eye/light paths).
    * Cost per connection: A visibility ray cast (A) plus BRDF evaluations at both the eye-path vertex and the light-path vertex (2B).
    * Connection Cost: $\frac{N}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{r^2} \cdot (A + 2B)$.
**Total Time:**
$$ T_{bi} \approx \frac{N}{r}(A + B) + \frac{N}{2r^2}(A + 2B) $$

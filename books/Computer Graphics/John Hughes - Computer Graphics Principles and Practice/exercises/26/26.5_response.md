### Answer 26.5
    
An ideal Lambertian reflector reflects all incoming energy ($\text{albedo } \rho = 1$) and distributes it uniformly in all directions.
The outgoing radiance $L_o$ is constant for all outgoing directions.
The total outgoing radiant exitance $M$ is related to $L_o$ by $M = \pi L_o$.
For a perfect reflector, outgoing power equals incoming power, so $M = E$ (Irradiance).
Thus $L_o = E / \pi$.
The definition of BRDF is $f_r = \frac{L_o}{E}$.
Substituting $L_o$:
$f_r = \frac{E/\pi}{E} = \frac{1}{\pi}$.
The BRDF would be the constant function $f_r(\omega_i, \omega_o) = \frac{1}{\pi}$ (units $sr^{-1}$).

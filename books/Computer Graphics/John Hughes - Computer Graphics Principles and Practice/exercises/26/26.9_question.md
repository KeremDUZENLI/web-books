### Exercise 26.9
    
We said that "A 'disk' consisting of all points on the unit sphere whose spherical distance from a point P is less than r (where $r<\pi$) has solid angle measure $2\pi(1-cos(r))$." But you'd expect, for small r, that the solid angle would contain an $r^{2}$ factor, because the formula for the area of a disk in the plane contains an $r^{2}$ factor. Reconcile the two by recalling the Taylor series for $cos(r)$ at $r=0.$

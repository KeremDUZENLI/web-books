### Answer 26.7
    
The 4000 K bulb emits more power in the invisible portion.
**Reasoning:**
1.  **Spectrum Distribution:** According to Planck's Law (and Wien's displacement law), the peak wavelength of emission shifts to shorter wavelengths (higher frequencies) as temperature increases.
    * 4000 K peak is in the infrared (invisible).
    * 6500 K peak is in the visible spectrum (approx 440nm-500nm).
2.  **Efficiency:** The 6500 K source radiates a much larger fraction of its total energy in the visible spectrum compared to the 4000 K source, which radiates the vast majority of its energy as invisible infrared heat.
3.  **Normalization:** Since both emit the *same* visible power, the 4000 K bulb must be consuming and emitting significantly more *total* power to match the visible output of the more efficient 6500 K bulb. The "wasted" energy (invisible IR) is much higher for the cooler 4000 K filament.

### Exercise 26.3
    
We computed the radiance emitted from each point of a spherical uniform source of radius r and total power $\Phi$ in each outward direction as $\frac{\Phi}{4\pi(\pi r^{2})}$. Now consider some distant point P, lying on a surface that faces the center C of the spherical source, and that the distance from C to P is $R>>r$. We can compute the irradiance at P by computing the solid angle subtended by the spherical source, and the radiance arriving at P along each direction of that solid angle, etc.
(a) Do this computation to determine the irradiance at P.
(b) Now suppose that the total power $\Phi$ remains constant, while the radius r of the source shrinks. What is the limit of the expression you found in part (a) as $r\rightarrow0$?

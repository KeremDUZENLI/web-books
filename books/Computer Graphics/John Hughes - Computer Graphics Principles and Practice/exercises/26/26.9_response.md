### Answer 26.9
    
Formula for solid angle: $\Omega = 2\pi(1 - \cos r)$.
For small $r$, we use the Taylor series expansion for cosine:
$\cos r \approx 1 - \frac{r^2}{2} + O(r^4)$.

Substitute into the formula:
$\Omega \approx 2\pi \left( 1 - \left( 1 - \frac{r^2}{2} \right) \right) = 2\pi \left( \frac{r^2}{2} \right) = \pi r^2$.

This matches the expected area of a disk in the plane ($\pi r^2$) divided by the radius squared (1) for small angles, confirming the formulas are consistent in the limit.

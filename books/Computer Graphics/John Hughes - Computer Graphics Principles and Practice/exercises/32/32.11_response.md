### Answer 32.11

**(a) Poisson Disk Process:**
Algorithm:

1. Attempt to place a point .
2. Check distance to all previously accepted points.
3. If , accept .
4. Repeat until  consecutive failures (saturation).

**(b) & (c) Frequency Analysis:**
The FFT of a blue-noise distribution (Poisson Disk) shows:

* **DC Spike:** At frequency 0 (average density).
* **Low Frequency:** Very low energy (dark region) near DC. This is the defining characteristic of blue noise.
* **High Frequency:** Energy dominates.

**(d) Cutoff Frequency:**
The energy is low for frequencies below roughly . The minimum distance  enforces a maximum frequency of packing, suppressing lower frequency variations (clumping).

**(e) Stratified Sampling Comparison:**
Stratified (Jittered) sampling:

* **Spectrum:** also suppresses low frequencies compared to white noise, but often exhibits grid artifacts (peaks at harmonics of the grid frequency) in the spectrum.
* **Difference:** Poisson disk is isotropic (in 2D) and lacks the grid spikes of stratified sampling.

**(f) 2D Generalization:**
Same algorithm, using 2D distance. The spectrum is radially symmetric with a dark ring around the center.

**(g) Mitchell's Algorithm:**
Mitchell's "Best Candidate" algorithm:

* To pick next point, generate  candidates.
* Choose the one that maximizes the distance to the nearest existing point.
* **Result:** Approximates Poisson disk distribution well, faster to generate, good blue noise characteristics.

### Exercise 10.10
It's good to be able to recognize the transformation represented by a matrix by looking at the matrix; for instance, it's easy to recognize a $3\times3$ matrix that represents a translation in homogeneous coordinates: Its last row is $[0~0~1]$ and its upper-left $2\times2$ block is the identity. Given a $3\times3$ matrix representing a transformation in homogeneous coordinates,
(a) how can you tell whether the transformation is affine or not?
(b) How can you tell whether the transformation is linear or not?
(c) How can you tell whether it represents a rotation about the origin?
(d) How can you tell if it represents a uniform scale?

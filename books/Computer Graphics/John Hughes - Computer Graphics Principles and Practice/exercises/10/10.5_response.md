### Answer 10.5
**(a)** The columns of $M$ are vectors in $R^n$. The maximum number of linearly independent vectors in $R^n$ is $n$. Since orthogonal vectors are linearly independent, $k$ must be less than or equal to $n$.
**(b)** The element $(M^T M)_{ij}$ is the dot product of the $i$-th row of $M^T$ (which is $v_i^T$) and the $j$-th column of $M$ ($v_j$).
$(M^T M)_{ij} = v_i \cdot v_j$.
Since the vectors are orthonormal:
* If $i=j$, $v_i \cdot v_i = ||v_i||^2 = 1$.
* If $i \neq j$, $v_i \cdot v_j = 0$.
Thus $M^T M = I_k$.

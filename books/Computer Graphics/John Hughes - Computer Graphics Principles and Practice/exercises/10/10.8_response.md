### Answer 10.8
**(a)** $||Mx||^2 = x^T M^T M x = x^T (V D U^T)(U D V^T) x = x^T V D^2 V^T x$. Let $y = V^T x$. Since $V$ is orthogonal, $||y|| = ||x|| = 1$.
Expression becomes $\sum d_i^2 y_i^2$. The maximum is achieved when $y$ is aligned with the largest singular value $d_1$, so $K = d_1^2$.
**(b)** The minimum squared length is determined by the smallest singular value: $d_n^2$ (or $d_2^2$ in 2D).
**(c)** In $R^3$, the image of the unit sphere is an ellipsoid. The squared lengths of its principal axes are $d_1^2, d_2^2, d_3^2$.

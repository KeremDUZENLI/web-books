### Answer 20.4
    
**(a) Normal Calculation:**
Curve: $z = \frac{1}{2}(1 - y^2) \implies 2z = 1 - y^2 \implies y^2 + 2z = 1$.
Gradient $\nabla(y^2 + 2z) = (2y, 2)$.
Normal vector $N = (y, 1)$.
(Normalized $n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{y^2+1}}(y, 1)$).

**(b) Reflection:**
Incoming ray direction $i = (0, -1)$ (parallel to -z).
Normal $n = (y, 1)$.
Reflection formula: $r = i - 2(i \cdot \hat{n})\hat{n} = i - 2\frac{i \cdot n}{|n|^2}n$.
$i \cdot n = (0)(y) + (-1)(1) = -1$.
$|n|^2 = y^2 + 1$.
$r = (0, -1) - 2\frac{-1}{y^2+1}(y, 1) = (0, -1) + \frac{2}{y^2+1}(y, 1)$.
$r_y = \frac{2y}{y^2+1}$.
$r_z = -1 + \frac{2}{y^2+1} = \frac{-(y^2+1) + 2}{y^2+1} = \frac{1-y^2}{1+y^2}$.
$r = \left( \frac{2y}{y^2+1}, \frac{1-y^2}{1+y^2} \right)$.

**(c) Specific Points:**
At $y = \pm 1$:
$r_y = \frac{\pm 2}{2} = \pm 1$.
$r_z = \frac{0}{2} = 0$.
$r = (\pm 1, 0)$. Correct.

**(d) Right Half-Plane:**
Let $r_y = \sin \theta, r_z = \cos \theta$.
$\frac{1-y^2}{1+y^2} = \cos \theta$.
Solving for $y$: $y = \tan(\theta/2)$.
As $y$ ranges from $-1$ to $1$, $\theta$ ranges from $-\pi/2$ to $\pi/2$. This covers all directions in the right half-plane ($z \ge 0$ is not strictly true here based on coordinate definitions, but it covers the 180 degree span).

**(e) 3D Paraboloid (Front):**
Spinning generates surface with normal $n = (x, y, 1)$.
Incident $i = (0, 0, -1)$.
Reflection $r = \left( \frac{2x}{x^2+y^2+1}, \frac{2y}{x^2+y^2+1}, \frac{1-(x^2+y^2)}{1+x^2+y^2} \right)$.
With $x^2+y^2 \le 1$, the $z$ component is non-negative. This maps the disk to the forward hemisphere.

**(f) Inverse Correspondence:**
Front paraboloid maps direction $(r_x, r_y, r_z)$ to disk coordinates:
$x = \frac{r_x}{1+r_z}, y = \frac{r_y}{1+r_z}$.
Back paraboloid (reflection $i=(0,0,1)$):
$x = \frac{r_x}{1-r_z}, y = \frac{r_y}{1-r_z}$.

**(g) Sphere Map Representation:**
Use two circular textures (disks).
1.  Map the hemisphere $z \ge 0$ to Disk A using $x = r_x/(1+r_z), y = r_y/(1+r_z)$.
2.  Map the hemisphere $z < 0$ to Disk B using $x = r_x/(1-r_z), y = r_y/(1-r_z)$.
This covers the entire sphere using two standard 2D textures.

**(h) Area Distortion:**
The mapping is conformal (preserves angles) but not area-preserving.
At the pole ($r_z=1$), $x=0, y=0$. Area scaling is 1/4.
At the equator ($r_z=0$), $x=r_x, y=r_y$. Area scaling is 1.
The distortion is modest (factor of 4 max) compared to latitude-longitude maps which have infinite distortion at poles. It uses memory effectively ($78.5\%$ of the square texture is used).

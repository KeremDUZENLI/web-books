### Answer 22.1
    
**(a) Derivatives of Hermite polynomials:**
Let the polynomials be:
$H_P(t) = 2t^3 - 3t^2 + 1$
$H_Q(t) = -2t^3 + 3t^2$
$H_v(t) = t^3 - 2t^2 + t$
$H_w(t) = t^3 - t^2$

Derivatives with respect to $t$:
$H_P'(t) = 6t^2 - 6t$
$H_Q'(t) = -6t^2 + 6t$
$H_v'(t) = 3t^2 - 4t + 1$
$H_w'(t) = 3t^2 - 2t$

**(b) Evaluation at endpoints:**
At $t=0$:
$H_P'(0) = 0$
$H_Q'(0) = 0$
$H_v'(0) = 1$
$H_w'(0) = 0$

At $t=1$:
$H_P'(1) = 6 - 6 = 0$
$H_Q'(1) = -6 + 6 = 0$
$H_v'(1) = 3 - 4 + 1 = 0$
$H_w'(1) = 3 - 2 = 1$

**(c) Explanation:**
The tangent vector of the curve $\gamma(t)$ is given by $\gamma'(t) = H_P'(t)P + H_Q'(t)Q + H_v'(t)v + H_w'(t)w$.
At the start ($t=0$), the derivative is $\gamma'(0) = 0 \cdot P + 0 \cdot Q + 1 \cdot v + 0 \cdot w = v$.
At the end ($t=1$), the derivative is $\gamma'(1) = 0 \cdot P + 0 \cdot Q + 0 \cdot v + 1 \cdot w = w$.
Since the weighting coefficients for $P$ and $Q$ are zero at the boundaries, the positions of the endpoints $P$ and $Q$ do not influence the instantaneous velocity (direction and speed) at those boundaries; only the specified tangent vectors $v$ and $w$ determine the direction.

### Exercise 22.3
    
In the development of the Catmull-Rom spline, we talked about placing a fictitious control point $P_{-1}$ that's symmetric to $P_{1}$ about $P_{0}$
(a) Show that the point $P_{-1}$ is given by $P_{0}-(P_{1}-P_{0})$, and simplify.
(b) Show that if we apply the rule $v_{0}=\frac{1}{3}(P_{1}-P_{-1})$, then the formula from part (a) lets us simplify this to $v_{0}=\frac{2}{3}(P_{1}-P_{0})$.

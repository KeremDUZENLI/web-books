### Answer 17.1

**Description of Blend Regions:**
The blend operation $B(U, V, s)$ linearly interpolates the coverage of $U$ and $V$.
1.  **Neither:** Transparent region remains transparent.
2.  **Just U:** Opacity scales by $(1-s)$.
3.  **Just V:** Opacity scales by $s$.
4.  **Both:** The overlapping region represents the combined probabilistic coverage.

**Consistency Check:**
The standard assumption for compositing is that coverage is uncorrelated.
For $blend(U, V, s) = dissolve(U, 1-s) + dissolve(V, s)$:
- Coverage from $U$ becomes $(1-s)\alpha_U$.
- Coverage from $V$ becomes $s\alpha_V$.
- The sum is $(1-s)\alpha_U + s\alpha_V$.
This assumes the contributions are additive (disjoint) rather than overlapping (uncorrelated). Therefore, the linear blend logic treats the opaque parts as if they are spatially separate or handled additively, which is **not** strictly consistent with the uncorrelated overlap assumption used in the standard "Over" operator ($ \alpha_U + \alpha_V - \alpha_U\alpha_V $).

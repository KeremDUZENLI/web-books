### Answer 21.2
    
**Advantages:**
This "differential" or "incremental" approach simplifies the state management since the system only tracks the previous frame's mouse position, not the start of the drag.

**Disadvantages:**
1.  **Drift and Precision:** Repeatedly multiplying matrices ($M_{new} = \Delta M \cdot M_{old}$) accumulates floating-point round-off errors. Over a long session, the object's transformation matrix may become non-orthogonal (shearing/scaling the object) unless explicitly re-orthogonalized (e.g., via Gram-Schmidt) periodically.
2.  **Path Dependence (Hysteresis):** In an integral (Arcball) approach, returning the mouse to the exact starting pixel guarantees the object returns to its exact starting orientation. In an incremental approach, due to the non-commutativity of finite rotations and the discrete sampling of the mouse path, moving the mouse in a closed loop $A \to B \to A$ might not return the object to the exact identity rotation. The user cannot reliably "undo" a small jitter by moving the mouse back.

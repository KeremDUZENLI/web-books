### Answer 21.3
    
**(a) Route Following Interface:**
* **Concept:** A "slider" or linear control representing the progression along the route (0% to 100%).
* **Interaction:** As the user drags the slider, the camera interpolates along the path.
* **Automatic Zoom:** The camera altitude (zoom level) automatically adjusts based on the speed of the car/cursor or the density of turns. On highways (straight segments), the camera zooms out to show context. Near junctions or start/end points, it zooms in for detail.
* **Visuals:** The route is highlighted. A "play" button automates the travel at a readable speed.

**(b) Integrated Zoom/Pan:**
* **Design:** A "speed-dependent zooming" or "Dasher-like" interface.
* **Mechanism:** The user clicks and holds to fly toward the cursor.
    * **Vector:** The direction from the screen center to the cursor defines the flight direction.
    * **Speed/Scale:** The distance of the cursor from the center defines the speed.
    * **Coupling:** As velocity increases, the camera automatically zooms out (altitude increases) to maintain optical flow consistency and provide context. As the user slows down (moves cursor near center) over a specific target (the city), the camera zooms in.
* **Result:** Large movements (home to friend's city) naturally trigger a zoom-out/fly-over/zoom-in sequence in a single continuous gesture without explicit mode switching.

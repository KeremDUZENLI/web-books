### Answer 21.7
    
**Multitouch Drawing Design:**
* **Creation:** Single finger drag creates shape.
* **Manipulation:** Two-finger tap on a shape selects it. Two-finger pinch/spread scales and rotates.
* **Modifiers:** Instead of keyboard keys (CTRL), use "chording" gestures.
    * *Example:* Hold one finger on the object (anchor) and drag a corner with another finger to resize.
    * *Constraint:* Tap a second finger elsewhere on screen while dragging to toggle "Maintain Aspect Ratio" or "Snap to Grid."

**Importance of Constraints:**
Control-limiting operations are **more important** in multitouch. Fingers are less precise than mice (the "fat finger" problem). It is physically difficult to drag exactly diagonally with a finger to maintain aspect ratio manually. Explicit constraints (snapping, aspect locking) are essential to allow users to create precise geometric shapes rather than loose sketches.

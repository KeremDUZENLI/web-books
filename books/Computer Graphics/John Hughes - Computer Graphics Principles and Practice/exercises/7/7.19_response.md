### Answer 7.19

**(a)** See algorithm logic below.
**(b)** Floating point math introduces rounding errors. For integer coordinates, the intersection is always a rational number. Storing it as $(x, y, w)$ preserves exact precision.
**(c)** Equality test: $x_1/w_1 == x_2/w_2 \iff x_1 w_2 == x_2 w_1$. Similarly for $y$.
**(d)** Use cross products ($2D$ determinant) to check orientation of endpoints relative to the other line segment. If signs differ, they straddle the line. Intersection parameters are ratios of these cross product areas. Avoid division by maintaining the denominator.

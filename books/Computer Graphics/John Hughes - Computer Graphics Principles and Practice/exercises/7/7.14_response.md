### Answer 7.14

**(a)** Area is invariant under shear. Shearing $P_1$ to $x$-axis and $P_2$ to $y$-axis reduces the triangle to a right triangle with vertices $(0,0), (x_1', 0), (0, y_2')$. Area is $0.5 x_1' y_2'$. The formula $0.5(x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1)$ reduces to this form under the coordinate mapping.
**(b)** The algebraic formula is continuous. Geometric area is continuous. Since they match on a dense set (generic triangles not aligned with axes), they match everywhere.
**(c)** A polynomial function (the area formula) is continuous. If it agrees with the geometric area on a dense open set, the extension to the boundary (degenerate or axis-aligned cases) is unique.

### Exercise 7.11
(a) Show that the normal vector to a triangle with vertices $P_{0}, P_{1}, P_{2}$ computed by Plücker's method is indeed perpendicular to $P_{1}-P_{0}$; a similar computation works to show it's perpendicular to $P_{2}-P_{0}$.
(b) Verify that for $P_{0}=(0,0,0)$, $P_{1}=(1,0,0)$, and $P_{2}=(0,1,0)$, the normal computed by Plücker's method points along the positive z-axis so that the vectors $P_{2}-P_{0},P_{1}-P_{0}$, and n form a right-handed coordinate system.

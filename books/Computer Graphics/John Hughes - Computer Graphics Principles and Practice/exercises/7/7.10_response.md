### Answer 7.10

The integral $\int \frac{dz}{z-z_0}$ measures the change in the argument (angle) of $z-z_0$ as $z$ moves along the curve. For a closed curve, this total angle change is $2\pi k$ for some integer $k$. Dividing by $2\pi i$ (using complex log properties) yields the integer $k$.
For a polygon, the integral splits into a sum over edges. The angle change for one edge $P_i P_{i+1}$ relative to $z_0$ corresponds to the angle term in the sum-of-inverse-cosines formula. Summing these discrete angle changes is equivalent to the integral.

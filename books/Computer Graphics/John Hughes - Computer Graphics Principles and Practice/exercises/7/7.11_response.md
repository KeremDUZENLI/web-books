### Answer 7.11

**(a)** Plücker's vector $n$ is defined by projected areas, which effectively computes the cross product $(P_1-P_0) \times (P_2-P_0)$. The cross product is always orthogonal to its factors. Thus $n \cdot (P_1-P_0) = 0$.
**(b)** $P_0=(0,0,0), P_1=(1,0,0), P_2=(0,1,0)$.
$A_{xy} = 0.5(1\cdot1 - 0) = 0.5$. $A_{yz} = 0$. $A_{zx} = 0$.
Vector $a = [0, 0, 1]^T$ (scaled by factor 2 area). This points in $+z$.
$(P_1-P_0) \times (P_2-P_0) = [1,0,0]^T \times [0,1,0]^T = [0,0,1]^T$. Matches.
**(c)** The geometric relationship of the cross product to the input vectors (right-hand rule) is invariant under coordinate transformations (rotation/translation). If it holds in the standard position, it holds everywhere.

### Answer 15.2
    
**Intersection:**
Given a ray $R(t) = O + tD$ and a sphere with center $C$ and radius $r$, the intersection satisfies:
$|P - C|^2 - r^2 = 0$
$|O + tD - C|^2 - r^2 = 0$

Let vector $\Delta = O - C$. The equation expands to a quadratic $At^2 + Bt + C_{coeff} = 0$:
1. $A = D \cdot D = 1$ (if $D$ is normalized)
2. $B = 2(D \cdot \Delta)$
3. $C_{coeff} = \Delta \cdot \Delta - r^2$

Solve for $t$ using the quadratic formula:
$t = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2 - 4AC_{coeff}}}{2A}$
The smallest positive $t$ is the intersection distance.

**Normal Computation:**
Spheres do not require barycentric interpolation for normals. The geometric normal at the intersection point $P$ is the vector from the center to the surface, normalized:
$n = \frac{P - C}{|P - C|} = \frac{P - C}{r}$

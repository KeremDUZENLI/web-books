### Answer 13.8
**(a) Motion:**
Let $w$ be the arm span (approx 1.5m to 2m) and $d$ be the distance to the wall.
$\theta = 2 \arctan(\frac{w}{2d})$.
For $w \approx d$, $\theta \approx 2 \arctan(0.5) \approx 53^\circ$.
Peripheral motion detection typically extends to nearly $180^\circ$.

**(b) Acuity:**
Let $w$ be the width of fingers held up ($\approx 0.05m$).
$\theta = 2 \arctan(\frac{w}{2d})$.
If $d=2m$, $\theta \approx 1.4^\circ$.
Detailed shape recognition is confined to the foveal region ($\approx 2^\circ$).

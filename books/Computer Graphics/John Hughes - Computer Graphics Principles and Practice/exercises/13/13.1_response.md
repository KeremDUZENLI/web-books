### Answer 13.1
We seek an affine transformation $T(x) = Ax + t$ such that $T(P) = \mathbf{0}$, $T(Q) = e_1$, and $T(R) = e_2$.
1.  **Translation:** Since $P \to \mathbf{0}$, $t = -AP$.
2.  **Linear Part:**
    $A(Q-P) = e_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$
    $A(R-P) = e_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$
    Let $u = Q-P = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $v = R-P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
    We need $A \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
    $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{6-1} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & -0.2 \\ -0.2 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}$.
3.  **Translation Vector:**
    $t = - \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & -0.2 \\ -0.2 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = - \begin{bmatrix} 0.4 \\ 0.2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -0.4 \\ -0.2 \end{bmatrix}$.
4.  **Final Matrix:**
    $M = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & -0.2 & -0.4 \\ -0.2 & 0.4 & -0.2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$

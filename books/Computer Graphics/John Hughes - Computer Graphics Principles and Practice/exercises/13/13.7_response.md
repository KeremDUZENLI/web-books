### Answer 13.7
(a) **Range:**
Moving the screen plane from $n$ to $k$ scales the geometric frustum cross-section by $k/n$.
However, the *viewing volume* defined by the angle (FOV) is the actual constraint.
The physical coordinates on the screen plane scale: $x_{new} = x_{old} \frac{k}{n}$.
The range of values increases if $k > n$.

(b) **Matrix:**
The perspective projection matrix is derived from the FOV and aspect ratio (or $l, r, b, t$ at the near plane).
If the "screen" is just a logical plane for intersection, but the frustum planes ($l,r,b,t$ at $z=-n$) are defined by the FOV angles, the parameters $l,r$ scale with $n$.
$l' = l \frac{k}{n}$.
The term $\frac{n}{r}$ in the matrix becomes $\frac{k}{r'}$.
$\frac{k}{r'} = \frac{k}{r(k/n)} = \frac{n}{r}$.
**Conclusion:** The projection matrix entries remain constant as long as the FOV and aspect ratio are preserved; the screen distance parameter $n$ in the formula represents the near clipping plane distance, not necessarily the display plane distance.

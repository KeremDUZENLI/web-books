### Answer 12.2
**Condition:**
The points $Q_i$ must be distinct enough to define a unique target configuration, but invertibility depends on the *source* points $P_i$.
The transformation is $M = A B^{-1}$.
* $B$ depends on $P_i$. General position of $P_i$ ensures $B$ is invertible.
* $A = [\alpha q_1, \beta q_2, \gamma q_3]$.
* For the transformation to be non-singular (invertible), $A$ must be invertible.
* This requires $q_1, q_2, q_3$ to be linearly independent (non-collinear).
* Also, for the mapping to be valid for $q_4$, the scalars $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ (derived from $Q^{-1}q_4$) must be non-zero.
* **Weakest Condition:** $Q_1, Q_2, Q_3$ must form a non-degenerate triangle, and $Q_4$ must not lie on any edge lines passing through the origin in the projective space (effectively $Q_4$ cannot result in a zero weight). Ideally, $Q_i$ should satisfy the same general position requirements to ensure a non-degenerate result.

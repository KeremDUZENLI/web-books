### Answer 27.5
    
The Fresnel reflectance for unpolarized light is $R_F = \frac{1}{2}(r_{\parallel}^2 + r_{\perp}^2)$.
Using Snell's Law: $n_1 \sin \theta_i = n_2 \sin \theta_t$.
Let $n = n_1/n_2$.
$$ \sin \theta_t = n \sin \theta_i $$
If $n \sin \theta_i > 1$, we have total internal reflection ($R_F = 1$).

**Plot Description:**
1.  **r = 1:** $n_1 = n_2$. Light passes through unchanged. $R_F = 0$ for all $\theta_i$.
2.  **r = 1.5, 2, 3:**
    * **Normal Incidence ($\theta_i = 0$):** $R_F$ starts at $((r-1)/(r+1))^2$. For $r=3$, this is $(2/4)^2 = 0.25$.
    * **Grazing Incidence ($\theta_i = 90^\circ$):** $R_F$ approaches 1.0.
    * **Shape:** The curve stays relatively flat near 0 and rises sharply towards 1 as $\theta_i$ approaches $90^\circ$. For higher $r$, the base reflectance is higher.

*(Note: If $r > 1$, this implies $n_1 > n_2$, so total internal reflection will occur at the critical angle $\theta_c = \arcsin(1/r)$. The plot will jump to or reach 1.0 at $\theta_c$ and stay there.)*

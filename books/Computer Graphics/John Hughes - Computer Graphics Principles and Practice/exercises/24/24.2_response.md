### Answer 24.2

(a) **Diagonal Consistency:**
Drawing a diagonal splits the square into two triangles. Piecewise-linear interpolation on a triangle defines a plane. The zero-level set of a plane is a line segment.
* If the corners have alternating signs (e.g., NE+, SW+, NW-, SE-), drawing the diagonal from NE to SW connects the two positive values.
* Along this diagonal, all interpolated values are positive (convex combination of two positive numbers).
* The zero curve cannot cross a line of positive values.
* Therefore, the zero curves must separate the negative corners (NW and SE) from the connected positive region, resulting in two separate curves cutting off the negative corners. This matches the topology where like-signs are connected across the diagonal.

(b) **Alternate Diagonal:**
Choosing the alternate diagonal (NW to SE) connects the two negative vertices.
* Values along this diagonal are negative.
* The zero curve cannot cross this diagonal.
* This forces the zero curves to separate the positive corners (NE and SW), creating the alternative topology where the negative regions are connected.

(c) **Center Vertex Algorithm:**
1.  Compute center value: $v_{center} = \frac{v_{NE} + v_{NW} + v_{SW} + v_{SE}}{4}$.
2.  Add vertex $C$ at the center. Add edges connecting $C$ to NE, NW, SW, SE.
3.  This forms four triangles within the square.
4.  Perform linear interpolation on each triangle.
5.  Find zero crossings on the 4 boundary edges and the 4 internal edges.
6.  Connect crossing points within each triangle to form the isocurve.

(d) **Resolution of Ambiguity:**
The topology is determined by the sign of $v_{center}$.
* If $v_{center}$ has the same sign as the NE/SW pair (e.g., positive), the center connects to them, creating a continuous positive region through the middle. The zero curves will pass through the triangles formed with the negative corners (NW/SE), effectively isolating the negative corners.
* If $v_{center}$ has the same sign as the NW/SE pair (e.g., negative), the negative region is continuous through the center, and the positive corners are isolated.
* Thus, the arithmetic average determines the topology, choosing one of the two consistent connectivities based on the data intensity.

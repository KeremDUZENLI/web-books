### Answer 24.1

Equation 24.14 is given by:
$F(x,y) = \sum_{i,j} v(i,j)h(x-i, y-j)$

The condition that "when all $v(i,j)$ are 1, the function $F$ is everywhere 1" implies:
$\sum_{i,j} 1 \cdot h(x-i, y-j) = \sum_{i,j} h(x-i, y-j) = 1$

This means the weights $w_{i,j} = h(x-i, y-j)$ form a partition of unity. Assuming standard interpolation kernels (like bilinear interpolation) where $h \ge 0$:
1. $F(x,y)$ is a weighted sum of the corner values $v(i,j)$.
2. The weights are non-negative ($w_{i,j} \ge 0$) and sum to 1.
3. $F(x,y)$ is therefore a convex combination of the values $v(i,j)$.

A convex combination of a set of values must lie within the convex hull of those values. For scalars, the convex hull is the interval $[v_{min}, v_{max}]$. Thus:
$v_{min} \le F(x,y) \le v_{max}$.

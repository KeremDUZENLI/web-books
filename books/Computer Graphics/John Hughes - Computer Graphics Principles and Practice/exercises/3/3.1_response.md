### Answer 3.1

**Eye Position Changes:**
* **Original:** $(0, 0, 10)$ (Looking at origin from +Z).
* **Move to $(5, 0, 10)$:** The camera moves to the *right*. The object appears to shift to the *left* of the screen.
* **Move to $(0, 0, 5)$:** The camera moves *closer* to the object. The object appears larger (zoomed in).
* **Move to $(0, 0, -10)$:** The camera moves to the *opposite side* (behind the object). Since the camera typically looks toward the origin, you are now looking at the "back" of the object. If back-face culling is enabled, you might see the back; otherwise, you see the reverse side.

**Geometric Logic:**
$\text{View Vector} = \text{Target} - \text{Eye}$. changing Eye changes the perspective calculation.

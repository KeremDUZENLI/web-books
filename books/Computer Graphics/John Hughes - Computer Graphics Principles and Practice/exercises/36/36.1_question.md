### Exercise 36.1
    
Typically in Sutherland-Hodgman clipping, the polygon to be clipped is small compared to the boundary polygon, and it intersects at most one side of the boundary polygon; often the input polygon starts on the inside, crosses the boundary, remains outside for a while, and then recrosses the same boundary edge and returns inside. In this case, clipping an input polygon with n edges against a boundary with k edges involves generating and inserting only two intersection points, although it involves testing $O(nk)$ edge pairs for intersections. Build an example input in which there are $O(nk)$ intersection points computed and inserted.

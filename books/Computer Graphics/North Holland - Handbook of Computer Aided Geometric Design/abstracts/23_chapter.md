# Chapter 23: Cyclides

---

## Main Idea

This chapter explores Dupin cyclides, a family of algebraic surfaces characterized by having constant principal curvatures along their lines of curvature. It details their dual geometric definitions—both as envelopes of moving spheres and as inversions of tori or cones—and demonstrates their utility in CAGD for tasks like blending pipes and constructing rational transition surfaces between quadrics.

---

## Keywords

**Dupin Cyclide:**
A surface defined as the envelope of a one-parameter family of spheres. It has the special property that its principal curvatures are constant along its lines of curvature.


**Principal Curvature:**
The maximum and minimum bending values of a surface at a specific point. For cyclides, these values do not change as you move along a curvature line.

**Lines of Curvature:**
Curves on a surface that follow the directions of maximum and minimum curvature. On a cyclide, these lines are always circles or straight lines.

**Inversion:**
A geometric transformation (reflection inside a sphere) that maps lines to circles and spheres to spheres. All Dupin cyclides can be generated by inverting a torus, cylinder, or cone.

**Characteristic Curve:**
The circle along which a sphere touches the envelope surface. A cyclide is formed by the motion of this circle through space.

**Canal Surface:**
A surface formed by the envelope of a sphere moving along a path (spine). A cyclide is a special type of canal surface where the spine is a conic section.

## Formulas

**Implicit Equation (Quartic)**

- **Formula:** $(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - \mu^2 - b^2)^2 = 4(ax - c\mu)^2 + 4b^2y^2$
- **Meaning:** The algebraic definition of a ring cyclide, relating spatial coordinates $(x,y,z)$ to shape parameters $a, b, c, \mu$.
- **Use in graphics:** Used for ray tracing or intersection testing to define the surface boundary.

**Rational Parametrization**

- **Formula:** $r(u, v) = \frac{1}{w(u, v)} (X(u, v), Y(u, v), Z(u, v))$
- **Meaning:** Defines the cyclide using quadratic polynomials, making it compatible with standard NURBS software.
- **Use in graphics:** Essential for rendering the shape and exporting it to CAD systems.

**Principal Curvature Property**

- **Formula:** $\frac{\partial \kappa_1}{\partial u} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial \kappa_2}{\partial v} = 0$
- **Meaning:** The curvature $\kappa_1$ stays constant while moving in the $u$ direction (and $\kappa_2$ in the $v$ direction).
- **Use in graphics:** Used to identify cyclides among other surfaces during reverse engineering.

## Practical Use

**Pipe Blending**
Cyclides are used to connect two pipes (cylinders) of different diameters or orientations smoothly. Because they are ring-shaped, they provide a natural "elbow" transition.

**Variable Radius Fillets**
Unlike standard toroidal fillets which have a constant radius, cyclides allow the radius of the blend to change smoothly from one end to the other, useful for complex mechanical parts.

**Motion Planning**
The space between obstacles in a robot's workspace can be modeled using cyclides to verify clearance, as they provide exact boundaries for spherical exclusion zones.

### Exercise 11.1

Suppose that $B_2(t)$ is a quadratic Bézier curve having the three control points $P_0$, $P_1$, and $P_2$. That is,
$$B_2(t) = (1 - t)^2 P_0 + 2t(1 - t)P_1 + t^2 P_2$$

Determine the four control points $P_0'$ through $P_3'$ such that the cubic Bézier curve
$$B_3(t) = (1 - t)^3 P_0' + 3t(1 - t)^2 P_1' + 3t^2(1 - t)P_2' + t^3 P_3'$$
is exactly coincident with the quadratic Bézier curve $B_2(t)$. (This process is called degree elevation.)

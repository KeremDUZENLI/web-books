### Answer 11.2

Using the de Casteljau algorithm to split the curve at $s = 1/2$, the control points for the first segment $Q$ are:
$$Q_0 = P_0$$
$$Q_1 = \frac{1}{2}P_0 + \frac{1}{2}P_1$$
$$Q_2 = \frac{1}{4}P_0 + \frac{1}{2}P_1 + \frac{1}{4}P_2$$
$$Q_3 = \frac{1}{8}P_0 + \frac{3}{8}P_1 + \frac{3}{8}P_2 + \frac{1}{8}P_3$$

Expressing $[Q_0 \ Q_1 \ Q_2 \ Q_3]$ as a product of $[P_0 \ P_1 \ P_2 \ P_3] M_Q$:
$$M_Q = \begin{bmatrix} 
1 & 1/2 & 1/4 & 1/8 \\ 
0 & 1/2 & 1/2 & 3/8 \\ 
0 & 0 & 1/4 & 3/8 \\ 
0 & 0 & 0 & 1/8 
\end{bmatrix}$$

The control points for the second segment $R$ are:
$$R_0 = Q_3 = \frac{1}{8}P_0 + \frac{3}{8}P_1 + \frac{3}{8}P_2 + \frac{1}{8}P_3$$
$$R_1 = \frac{1}{4}P_1 + \frac{1}{2}P_2 + \frac{1}{4}P_3$$
$$R_2 = \frac{1}{2}P_2 + \frac{1}{2}P_3$$
$$R_3 = P_3$$

Expressing $[R_0 \ R_1 \ R_2 \ R_3]$ as a product of $[P_0 \ P_1 \ P_2 \ P_3] M_R$:
$$M_R = \begin{bmatrix} 
1/8 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 
3/8 & 1/4 & 0 & 0 \\ 
3/8 & 1/2 & 1/2 & 0 \\ 
1/8 & 1/4 & 1/2 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}$$

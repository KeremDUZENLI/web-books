### Answer 11.3

**(a)**
The Catmull-Rom spline has the tangent definition $T_i = 0.5(P_{i+1} - P_{i-1}) = 0.5(P_i - P_{i-1}) + 0.5(P_{i+1} - P_i)$.
For the TCB tangent equations to evaluate to this, the coefficients must satisfy:
$$\frac{(1 - \tau_i)(1 \pm \gamma_i)(1 \pm \beta_i)}{2} = 0.5$$
This requires:
$\tau_i = 0$, $\gamma_i = 0$, and $\beta_i = 0$.

**(b)**
For $C^1$ continuity, the incoming tangent ($T_{i,2}$) must equal the outgoing tangent ($T_{i,1}$) at point $P_i$.
Equating the coefficients of $(P_i - P_{i-1})$:
$$\frac{(1 - \tau_i)(1 - \gamma_i)(1 + \beta_i)}{2} = \frac{(1 - \tau_i)(1 + \gamma_i)(1 + \beta_i)}{2}$$
$$1 - \gamma_i = 1 + \gamma_i \implies \gamma_i = 0$$
The condition is that the continuity parameter $\gamma_i$ must be exactly $0$.

**(c)**
Define the constants for the tangent equations at $P_i$ (start of segment) and $P_{i+1}$ (end of segment):
$c_1 = \frac{(1-\tau_i)(1+\gamma_i)(1+\beta_i)}{2}$
$c_2 = \frac{(1-\tau_i)(1-\gamma_i)(1-\beta_i)}{2}$
$c_3 = \frac{(1-\tau_{i+1})(1-\gamma_{i+1})(1+\beta_{i+1})}{2}$
$c_4 = \frac{(1-\tau_{i+1})(1+\gamma_{i+1})(1-\beta_{i+1})}{2}$

The Hermite geometry maps to the TCB geometry via a transformation matrix $M_{trans}$:
$$G_{Hermite} = \begin{bmatrix} P_i \\ P_{i+1} \\ T_{start} \\ T_{end} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -c_1 & c_1-c_2 & c_2 & 0 \\ 0 & -c_3 & c_3-c_4 & c_4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} P_{i-1} \\ P_i \\ P_{i+1} \\ P_{i+2} \end{bmatrix}$$

Multiply the standard Hermite basis matrix by $M_{trans}$:
$$M_{KB} = M_{Hermite} M_{trans} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 & 1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -c_1 & c_1-c_2 & c_2 & 0 \\ 0 & -c_3 & c_3-c_4 & c_4 \end{bmatrix}$$

$$M_{KB} = \begin{bmatrix} 
-c_1 & 2 + c_1 - c_2 - c_3 & -2 + c_2 + c_3 - c_4 & c_4 \\ 
2c_1 & -3 - 2c_1 + 2c_2 + c_3 & 3 - 2c_2 - c_3 + c_4 & -c_4 \\ 
-c_1 & c_1 - c_2 & c_2 & 0 \\ 
0 & 1 & 0 & 0 
\end{bmatrix}$$

### Answer 12.2

1. The axis of the cylinder passes through the origin $(0,0,0)$ and point $A$. The direction of the axis is vector $A$.
2. To find the normal at point $Q$ on the surface, we need to find the point on the axis closest to $Q$. Let's call this point $Q_{axis}$.
3. $Q_{axis}$ is the projection of vector $Q$ onto vector $A$:
$$Q_{axis} = \left( \frac{Q \cdot A}{A \cdot A} \right) A$$
4. The vector from the axis to the surface point $Q$ is $(Q - Q_{axis})$. This vector is perpendicular to the surface.
5. The unit normal vector $N$ is the normalized version of this vector:
$$N = \frac{Q - Q_{axis}}{||Q - Q_{axis}||}$$
Since $Q$ is on the surface of a cylinder with radius $r$, $||Q - Q_{axis}|| = r$. Therefore:
$$N = \frac{1}{r} \left( Q - \left( \frac{Q \cdot A}{A \cdot A} \right) A \right)$$
